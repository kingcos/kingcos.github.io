Weekly Address: A Conversation About Addiction

每周演讲：一场关于瘾症的对话

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, Grammy Award-winning artist Macklemore joined President Obama to discuss a disease that affects far too many Americans: addiction.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，荣获格莱美奖的艺术家 Macklemore 和奥巴马总统共同探讨一种影响太多美国人的疾病：瘾症。
Macklemore opened up about his own experience, his life in recovery, and the loss of a friend who overdosed on prescription drugs at a young age – emphasizing that substance use disorder affects people from all walks of life.
Macklemore 公开了他自己经历，康复中的生活，以及一位年轻时滥用处方药而逝去的朋友－（他）强调了物质使用障碍（译者注：这里也指药物滥用）影响了所有阶层的人。
To underscore the importance of Macklemore’s story, the President noted that we can help those suffering in private by making the conversation public, and we should do everything we can to make treatment available to everyone who needs it.
为了强调 Macklemore 所经历的重要性，总统指出我们可以通过将（关于瘾症的）对话公开来帮助那些默默忍受（疾病）的人们，并且我们应该尽一切努力使得医疗可以为任何需要的人所用。
The President noted that while the House of Representatives passed several bills on opioids this week, without more funding to expand treatment, these bills will not be enough to provide Americans the help they need.
总统指出，本周白宫代表通过了几项有关鸦片类药物的法案，（但是如果）没有加大投资扩大医疗（范围），这些法案将不足以为美国人提供需要的帮助。
President Obama said there is hope, and that when we treat substance use disorder as the public health problem it is, more people will get the help they need.
奥巴马总统谈到，希望仍存，并且我们可以将物质使用障碍（译者注：这里特指药物滥用）作为公众健康问题对待，（这样，）更多的人将得到需要的救助。
If you are looking for treatment in your area, call 1-800-662-HELP.
如果你正在寻找附近的医疗，（请）拨打 1-800-662-HELP。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, May 14, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 5 月 14 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama and Macklemore as Prepared for Delivery
贝拉克·奥巴马总统和 Macklemore 的预备演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
May 14, 2016
2016 年 5 月 14 日


Text：
正文：

THE PRESIDENT: Hi, everybody.
总统： 大家好。
I’ve got a special guest with me this week – Macklemore.
本周有一位特殊的客人（作客）－Macklemore。
For those of you who don’t share the same love[i] for hip-hop, he’s a Grammy-winning artist – but he’s also an advocate who’s giving voice to a disease we too often just whisper about: the disease of addiction.
对于那些不对嘻哈 same love 感同身受的你们来说，他是一位荣获格莱美奖的艺术家－但他同样也是一位声援一种我们平时只是私下提及的疾病：瘾症的拥护者。

MACKLEMORE: Hey, everybody.
MACKLEMORE: 大家好。
I’m here with President Obama because I take this personally.
我能和奥巴马总统在这里，是因为我本身就（曾）患有瘾症。
I abused prescription drugs and battled addiction.
我（曾）滥用处方药以和瘾症战斗。
If I hadn’t gotten the help I needed when I needed it, I might not be here today.
如果当我需要帮助时无法得到需要的援助，也许今天我就不会在这里。
And I want to help others facing the same challenges I did.
进而我想帮助其他人，那些面对与我之前所经历相同挑战（的患者）。

THE PRESIDENT: Drug overdoses now take more lives every year than traffic accidents.
总统： 现在，因服药过量（而导致的死亡人数）已经超过了交通事故。
Deaths from opioid overdoses have tripled since 2000.
自 2000 年（至今），由于服用鸦片类药物过量而造成的死亡（人数）已经三番。
A lot of the time, they’re from legal drugs prescribed by a doctor.
大多数时间，（这些药物）来自医生嘱咐的合法药物。
So addiction doesn’t always start in some dark alley – it often starts in a medicine cabinet.
所以，瘾症并不总是在昏暗的小巷（嗑药）开始－它有时也从一个（个）药物储藏柜开始（让人沾染）。
In fact, a new study released this month found that 44 percent of Americans know someone who has been addicted to prescription pain killers.
实际上，本月发布的一个新研究表明，44% 的美国人知道（身边有）某个已经对处方止痛药依赖的人。

MACKLEMORE: I didn’t just know someone – I lost someone.
MACKLEMORE: 我（曾）不仅知道这个人－我（甚至）失去了这个人。
My friend Kevin overdosed on painkillers when he was just 21 years old.
我的朋友 Kevin，仅仅从 21 岁开始滥用止痛药。
Addiction is like any other disease – it doesn’t discriminate.
瘾症如其它所有疾病一样－它不区别对待（任何人）。
It doesn’t care what color you are, whether you’re a guy or a girl, rich or poor, whether you live in the inner-city, a suburb, or rural America.
它无关你的肤色，（无论）你是男孩或是女孩，富有或贫困，（无论）你生活在美国的内城区，郊区，或是农村。
This doesn’t just happen to other people’s kids or in some other neighborhood.
这不只是发生在其他人的孩子中，或是某些社区。
It can happen to any of us.
它有可能发生在我们任何人中。

THE PRESIDENT: That’s why just talking about this crisis isn’t enough – we need to get treatment to more people who need it.
总统： 这就是为什么仅仅讨论危机并不足够－我们需要让更多需要的人得到治疗。
My administration is working with communities to reduce overdose deaths, including with medication.
本届政府正和社区协作减少滥用药物的死亡（人数），包括药物治疗。
We’re working with law enforcement to help people get into treatment instead of jail.
我们正在通过实施法律，来帮助患者得到医疗，取代被送入监狱。
And under Obamacare, health plans in the Marketplace have to include coverage for treatment.
并且在奥巴马医改（的保障）之下，市场中的医疗计划必须覆盖治疗。

MACKLEMORE: I know recovery isn’t easy or quick, but along with the 12-step program, treatment has saved my life.
MACKLEMORE: 我知道康复（并）不是又简单又迅速，但是随着十二步项目，医疗已拯救了我的生命。
Recovery works – and we need our leaders in Washington fund it and people know how to find it.
康复得很有效－并且我们希望华盛顿的领导为（医疗康复）设立基金，而人们（要）知道如何得到（医疗康复）。

THE PRESIDENT: We all need to do more to make that happen.
总统： 让这一（改变）发生，需要我们所有人做的更多。
I’ve asked Congress to expand access to recovery services, and to give first responders the tools they need to treat overdoses before it’s too late.
我已经要求国会扩大康复服务的享用机会，并给予急救员所需要的工具，在为时未晚前拯救滥用药物（的患者）。
This week, the House passed several bills about opioids – but unless they also make actual investments in more treatment, it won’t get Americans the help they need.
本周，白宫通过了几项有关鸦片类药物的法案－但是除非他们同样在更多医疗中加大投资，否则并不能让美国人得到（实际）需要的帮助。

On top of funding, doctors also need more training about the power of the pain medication they prescribe, and the risks they carry.
除了（设立）基金，医生同样需要更多有关止痛药医嘱威力的培训，以及其所附带的风险。
Another way our country can help those suffering in private is to make this conversation public.
我们国家所能帮助这些默默忍受疾病的人们的另一个方式是，让（关于瘾症的）对话公开。

MACKLEMORE: When you’re going through it, it’s hard to imagine there could be anything worse than addiction.
MACKLEMORE: 当你正在遭受疾病，很难想象有什么比瘾症更加难受。
But shame and the stigma associated with the disease keeps too many people from seeking the help they need.
但是疾病带来的羞愧和耻辱使得太多人远离寻求他们所需要的帮助。
Addiction isn’t a personal choice or a personal failing.
瘾症不是个人的选择，也不是个人的失败。
And sometimes it takes more than a strong will to get better – it takes a strong community and accessible resources.
而有时，他需要的不止是强大的求生欲－他需要一个强大的集体和可以得到的资源。

THE PRESIDENT: The good news is, there’s hope.
总统： 好消息是，有希望了。
When we talk about opioid abuse as the public health problem it is, more people will seek the help they need.
当我们将鸦片类药物滥用作为公众健康问题谈及，更多人将寻求他们所需要的帮助。
More people will find the strength to recover, just like Macklemore and millions of Americans have.
更多的人将建立康复的信心，就像 Macklemore 以及数百万的美国人所拥有的。
We’ll see fewer preventable deaths and fewer broken families.
我们将预见更少不必要的死亡，和更少破碎的家庭。

MACKLEMORE: We have to tell people who need help that it’s OK to ask for it.
MACKLEMORE: 我们必须告诉需要帮助的人们，寻求（帮助）没什么大不了。
We’ve got to make sure they know where to get it.
我们已经开始确保他们知道哪里能得到（帮助）。

THE PRESIDENT: We all have a role to play.
总统： 我们每个人都要各司其职。
Even if we haven’t fought this battle in our own lives, there’s a good chance we know someone who has, or who is.
尽管一生中我们没有经历过这场（与瘾症的）战斗，（但）这是一个好的契机让我们知道有人正在经历，以及这场斗争是什么。

MACKLEMORE: President Obama and I just had a powerful conversation here at the White House about opioid abuse, and what we can do about it.
MACKLEMORE: 奥巴马总统和我刚刚在白宫进行了一场关于鸦片类药物的强有力对话，以及关于它我们所能做的。
You can catch it this summer on MTV.
这个夏天你可以在音乐电视频道看到。
And to find treatment in your area, call 1-800-662-HELP.
并且（如果想）找到你附近的治疗，拨打 1-800-662-HELP。

THE PRESIDENT: Thanks, and have a great weekend.
总统： 感谢大家（倾听），并祝周末愉快。

[i] “Same Love” is the title of Macklemore’s hit 2012 song about marriage equality.
“Same Love” 是 Macklemore 2012 年畅销歌曲的名字，（该曲）关于婚姻平等。
