Weekly Address: Continuing to Strengthen the Middle Class

每周演讲：继续加强中产阶级

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, President Obama highlighted wage growth over the course of his presidency.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，奥巴马总统强调了其任期内的工资上涨。
Since 2012, real wages have grown about 20 times faster than they did for almost three decades between 1980 and 2007.
从 2012 年起，工资上涨的速度几乎比 1980 年到 2007 年的三十年间快 20 倍。
Last year, typical household income rose by $2,800 – the single largest increase on record.
去年，市民普通家庭收入增加了 2800 美元-记录以来的最大（收入）单年增长。
Across every race and age group in America, incomes rose and poverty rates fell; and 3.5 million people were lifted out of poverty – the largest one-year drop in the poverty rate since 1968.
在美国的各种族和年龄层的收入均有上升，而贫困率下降；350 万人脱贫-自 1968 年以来最大的单年贫困率下降。
Thanks in part to states and communities that have raised the minimum wage, lower- and middle-income families saw the biggest boost in incomes.
归功于（许多）州和社区已经提高最低工资，低收入和中等收入家庭在收入上增幅最大。
Although we’ve made significant progress, the President said more work needs to be done to strengthen the middle class – and this starts with a Congress that will put politics aside and act on commonsense ideas.
虽然我们已经成果斐然，（但）总统称仍需做的更多来加强中产阶级-而这起自一个最终把政治搁置一边，并对这些普通的想法采取行动的国会。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, October 8, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 10 月 8 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama as Delivered
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲递送
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
October 8, 2016
2016 年 10 月 8 日


Text：
正文：

Hi, everybody.
大家好。
Eight years ago, we were in the early stages of what would become the worst economic crisis of our lifetimes.
八年前，我们身处一生中最严重的经济危机的开端。
It was a scary time.
那是一个惊恐的时代。
We didn’t even know where the bottom would be.
我们甚至不知道（经济）谷底将会在哪里。

But thanks to your hard work and determination, and some smart decisions we made, today’s a different story.
但是归功于你们的辛勤工作和坚定决心，以及我们所作出的一些明智的决策，今天成为了不同的故事。
We turned a recession into a record streak of job growth, creating more than 15 million new private-sector jobs and cutting the unemployment rate in half.
我们化经济危机为破纪录的就业增长，创造了超过一千五百万新的私营部门的工作，并将失业率腰斩。

Getting wages to rise again was a harder task.
让工资再次上升是一个更加艰巨的任务。
Even before the recession, working Americans faced decades of slow wage growth.
即使在经济危机之前，美国工人（也）面临数十年的工资缓慢上涨。
Between 1980 and 2007, real wages barely grew each year.
在 1980 年到 2007 年之间，实际收入几乎每年都没有增长。
But because the policies we’ve put in place are working, working families are finally seeing their wages and incomes rise, too.
但是由于我们实行的政策正在奏效，工薪家庭终于将看到他们的工资和收入也在上升。
Since 2012, wages have grown around 20 times faster than they did over the almost three decades between 1980 and 2007.
从 2012 年起，工资上涨的速度几乎比 1980 年到 2007 年的三十年间快 20 倍。

Last year, folks’ typical household income rose by $2,800.
去年，市民普通家庭收入增加了 2800 美元。
That’s the single biggest increase on record.
这是记录以来的最大（收入）单年增长。
And across every race and age group in America, incomes rose and poverty rates fell.
并且在美国的各种族和年龄层的收入均有上升，而贫困率下降。
We lifted 3.5 million people out of poverty – the largest one-year drop in the poverty rate since 1968.
我们帮助 350 万人脱贫-自 1968 年以来最大的单年贫困率下降。

What’s more, lower- and middle-income families saw the biggest boost in incomes – in part because 18 states and the District, as well as more than 50 communities, have given millions of Americans a raise by raising the minimum wage.
此外，低收入和中等收入家庭在收入上增幅最大-部分原因是 18 个州和区，以及超过 50 个社区，已经通过为数百万的美国人提高最低工资来加薪。
And states that have raised their minimum wage have seen stronger earnings growth in low-wage jobs compared to states that have not.
提高低薪的最低工资的州比其他州收入更高。

Strengthening benefits at work helps, too.
增加工作福利也十分奏效。
Last week, for example, I took action to make sure up to one million more workers can earn seven days of paid sick leave on the job.
例如，在上周，我争取确保多达一百万工人享有七天的带薪病假。
We’re also helping states expand opportunities for workers to save for retirement.
我们同样帮助各个州扩大工人退休储蓄机会。
But there’s a lot more we should do to strengthen the middle class and help more Americans get ahead.
但是我们仍需做的更多来加强中产阶级，并帮助更多美国人走向成功。
Making childcare more affordable, for example.
例如，使儿童保育更加负担得起。
Making sure women earn equal pay for equal work.
确保女性在相同工作下享有相同的薪水。
Guaranteeing paid family and sick leave.
保证带薪家庭假和病假。
Increasing the federal minimum wage.
提高联邦最低工资。
Preparing workers for the jobs of the future.
为工人未来的工作做准备。
And closing tax loopholes that benefit just the wealthy and big corporations.
并减少税收漏洞，（因为其）仅仅有利于富豪和大公司。

Now we just need a Congress that cares about these issues – one that will finally put politics aside and act on these commonsense ideas.
现在，我们只需要一个关注这些问题的国会-一个最终把政治搁置一边，并对这些普通的想法采取行动。
That’s how we’ll build on the progress we’ve made over these past eight years, and achieve one thing we should all agree on – securing a brighter future for all our children.
这就是我们将如何建立在过去八年的进展，并达成我们共同的目标-为我们所有的孩子确保一个更加光明的未来。

Thanks everybody.
感谢大家。
Have a great weekend.
祝周末愉快。

presidency n.总统任期
race n.种族；竞赛，竞争
recession n.衰退
loophole n.漏洞
commonsense n.常识

real wage 纯收入
lift out of poverty 脱贫
benefits at work 工作福利
