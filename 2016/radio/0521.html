Weekly Address: Expanding Overtime Pay

每周演讲：提高加班费

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, President Obama discussed one of the single most important steps to help grow middle-class wages – expanding the number of workers who are eligible for the overtime that they have earned.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，奥巴马总统谈到了帮助中产阶级提高薪水中最至关重要的一步－扩大有资格加班获得更高薪水的工人数量。
Every week, millions of Americans work more than 40 hours but do not receive the overtime pay they deserve.
每周，数百万的美国人工作超过 40 小时，却没有获得他们应得的加班费。
This week, the Department of Labor finalized a rule to extend overtime protections to 4.2 million more Americans, which will boost wages for working Americans by 12 billion dollars over the next 10 years.
本周，劳工部最终确定了条例，将加班保护（的范围）延伸到 420 万以及更多的美国人，这将在下一个十年中为美国劳动者增收 120 亿美元。
This action will not only mean a better life for more American families, but will also strengthen our economy across the board.
这一举措不仅意味着更多美国家庭（将享受到）更好的生活，而且也全面地强化了我们的经济。
The President emphasized that he will continue to build an economy where everyone gets a fair shot to get ahead.
总统强调，他将继续建立一个每个人都有平等机会以获得成功的经济。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, May 21, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 5 月 21 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
May 21, 2016
2016 年 5 月 21 日


Text：
正文：

Hi, everybody.
大家好。
Last summer, I got a letter from a woman named Elizabeth Paredes from Tucson, Arizona.
去年夏季，我收到了一封来自亚利桑那州杜桑市 Elizabeth Paredes 女士的一封信。
Elizabeth is the mom of a 3-year-old boy, and an assistant manager at a sandwich shop.
Elizabeth 是一个三岁男孩的妈妈，在三明治商店做助理经理。
She earns about 2,000 dollars a month, and she routinely works some 50 hours a week, sometimes even more.
她月薪大约 2000 美元，通常她每周工作约 50 个小时，有时会更长。
But because of outdated overtime regulations, she doesn’t have to be paid a dime of overtime.
但是由于过时的加班费条例，她不得不接受很低的加班费。

She wrote: “It’s not easy work and requires a lot of time away from my son… at times I find [it’s] not worth it.”
她写道：”这并不是（一份）简单的工作，而且需要离开孩子很长时间… 有时我发现这（译者注：即加班）并不值得。”

Things like the 40-hour workweek and overtime are two of the most basic pillars of a middle class life.
每周 40 小时的工时和加班是中产阶级生活的两大最基本的支柱。
But for all the changes we’ve seen in our economy, our overtime rules have only been updated once since the 1970s.
但是，在我们经济中所已经发生的所有变化中，我们的加班条例从 20 世纪 70 年代（至今）仅仅更新了一次。
Just once.
仅仅一次。
In fact, forty years ago, more than 60 percent of workers were eligible for overtime based on their salaries.
实际上，四十年前，超过 60% 的工人有资格（争取）在他们薪水基础之上的加班费。
But today, that number is down to seven percent.
但是今天，这个数字下降到 7%。
Only seven percent of full-time salaried workers are eligible for overtime based on their income.
只有 7% 全职职工有资格（争取）在他们薪水基础之上的加班费。

That’s why this week, my Administration took a step to help more workers get the overtime pay they’ve earned.
这就是为什么，本届政府努力帮助更多的工人争取他们所应得的加班费。
The Department of Labor finalized a rule to extend overtime protections to 4.2 million more Americans.
本周，劳工部最终确定了条例，将加班保护（的范围）延伸到 420 万美国人。
It’s a move that will boost wages for working Americans by 12 billion dollars over the next 10 years.
这是一个转变，将在下一个十年中为美国劳动者增收 120 亿美元。
We’re more than doubling the overtime salary threshold.
我们更将加班的起点薪水翻倍。
And what that means is, most salaried workers who earn less than about 47,500 dollars a year will qualify for overtime.
而这意味着，大多数年薪少于 47,500 美元的职工将有资格加班。
Or, their employers can choose to give them a raise so that they earn more than 47,500 dollars.
或者，他们的雇主可以选择为他们加薪，因此他们的薪水将突破 47,500 美元。
Or, if employers don’t want to raise wages, they can let them go home after 40 hours and see their families or train for new jobs.
或者，如果雇主不想加薪，可以让职工在 40 小时工时之后回家陪伴家人或者（参与到）为新的工作培训。
Any way you slice it, it’s a win for working families.
不管你们如何分配，这是工薪家庭的胜利。
And we’re making sure that every three years, there will be an automatic update to this threshold – so that working families won’t fall through the cracks for decades at a time ever again.
并且我们将确保每三年，这个起点将会自动调整－因此工薪家庭将不会每隔十年再一次被忽视。

This is the single biggest step I can take through executive action to raise wages for the American people.
这是我所能通过行政权为美国人民提高薪水做的最大努力。
It means that millions of hardworking Americans like Elizabeth will either get paid for working more than 40 hours, or they’ll get more time with their families.
这意味着数百万如 Elizabeth 女士一样努力的美国人将在 40 小时的工时之外得到薪水，或者他们可以和家人（相处）更长的时间。
Either way, they win.
总之，他们赢了。
The middle class wins.
中产阶级赢了。
And America wins.
美利坚也赢了。

We still have more work to do to make sure this economy works for everybody, not just those at the top.
我们仍需做更多努力来保证经济利于每一个人，而不只是那些高层人士。
That’s why I’ll never stop fighting for as long as I hold this office – to restore the sense that in America, hard work should be rewarded with the chance to get ahead.
这就是为什么只要我还在任期就决不停息的努力－来重建美利坚的意义，努力的耕耘应当有机会成功。

Thanks everybody.
感谢大家（倾听）。
Have a great weekend.
并祝周末愉快。

eligible adj.有资格的
boost n.存进 v.增加
routinely adv.例行地
workweek=working week n.工作周；一周工作时间
pillar n.柱子；基础
dime n.美国 10 分硬币；少量的钱
finalize v.最后确定
threshold n.门槛；下限
slice n.薄片；部分 v.切片

overtime pay 加班费
across the board 全面地
