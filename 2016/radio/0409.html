Weekly Address: Playing by the Same Rules

每周演讲：遵守相同的规则

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President highlighted two specific steps the Administration took this week to make sure everyone plays by the same rules.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统强调了政府本周采取的两大举措，以确保所有人遵守相同的规则。
First, in order to help more Americans retire with the security they have earned, the Department of Labor finalized a rule that will ensure retirement advisers compete based on the quality of advice they give rather than acting in their own financial interests.
首先，为了帮助更多美国人退休时的盈利安全，劳工部最终通过一则法律，这将确保退休顾问以建议质量为基竞争，而不是其自己的经济利益。
In addition, the Treasury Department took action this week to crack down on corporations that take advantage of inversions, a loophole that allows some businesses to avoid paying their fair share in taxes here at home.
另外，财政部本周采取措施打击大公司滥用”倒转”，一个允许一些企业在本土避免公平赋税的漏洞。
These actions underscore the President’s belief that we should continue to build an economy where everyone gets a fair shot, does their fair share, and plays by the same rules.
这些举措证明了总统的信念，我们应当继续建立这样一个经济，人人都有机会获得成功，所有人公平分享，所有人都遵守相同规则的经济。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, April 9, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 4 月 9 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
April 9, 2016
2016 年 4 月 9 日


Text：
正文：

Hi, everybody.
大家好。
Over the past seven years, we haven’t just been recovering from crisis, we’ve been rebuilding our economy on a new foundation for growth – growth that benefits everybody, not just folks at the top.
在过去的七年中，我们不仅从（经济）危机中恢复，我们已经在发展的新基础上重建了经济－（这种）发展使所有人受益，不仅仅是上层阶级。
Our businesses have created jobs for 73 straight months – 14.4 million new jobs in all.
我们的企业已连续 73 个月创造就业岗位－总共 1440 万份新的工作。
We’ve covered another 20 million Americans with health insurance.
我们的医疗保险（也）覆盖了另外的 2000 万美国人。
We’ve helped more Americans afford college, and invested in industries that create good jobs that pay well, like clean energy.
我们已经（同样）帮助了更多的美国人进入职业学校，并为创造良好的高薪岗位的所在工业投资，例如清洁能源。
And wages are finally rising again.
而薪水最后也上涨了。

But there will always be more work to do.
但仍有许多要做。
And this week, my Administration took two big steps that will help make sure your hard work is rewarded, and that everybody plays by the same rules.
本周，本届政府采取了两大举措，这将帮助保障你的辛勤工作是值得的，而且所有人都要遵守相同的规则。

First, we’re helping more Americans retire with security and dignity.
首先，我们将帮助更多的美国人安全且有尊严的退休。
Right now, if you go to a retirement advisor for investment advice, some of them don’t have to act in your best interest.
现在，如果你去（询问）退休顾问投资建议，其中的一些（顾问）没有必要使你的利益最大化。
Instead of telling you the best way to save your hard-earned money, these advisors can get backdoor payments from big companies for steering you toward investments that cost more and earn you less.
这些顾问受大公司所贿，指使你投资成本更高，赚的更少（的理财），而不是告诉你储蓄辛苦钱的最佳方式。
As a result, when you retire, you might be missing out on tens of thousands of dollars – because your advisor got paid more to give you bad advice.
结果，当你退休了，你可能错过了上万美元－因为顾问通过给你出馊建议而牟利更多。

If that seems wrong, that’s because it is.
若这似乎是不正常的，因为这确实是（不正常）的。
That’s why the Department of Labor just finalized a rule to crack down on these kinds of conflicts of interest.
这就是为什么劳工部最后通过一则法律，以打击这种类型的利益冲突。
And a lot of Wall Street special interests aren’t very happy about it.
而很多华尔街特殊利益对此很不乐意。
But across the country, this new rule will boost working folks’ retirement savings by billions of dollars a year.
但是纵观全国，新的法律将使工人增长每年数十亿美元储蓄。
And it will level the playing field for the many good advisors who do work in their clients’ best interest.
而这（也）将对许多优秀的、为客户利益最大化的顾问公平。

Second, the Treasury Department took action to crack down on big corporations that change their address overseas after acquiring smaller companies, in order to reduce their tax bill here at home.
其次，财政部采取行动，打击大型公司在收购小公司后更改海外地址，以减少在国内的纳税额。
It’s a loophole called “corporate inversion.”
这一种叫做”企业反转”的漏洞。
And it means that American companies can take advantage of America’s technology, America’s infrastructure, America’s workers – but then, when it comes to paying their fair share of taxes, suddenly claim they’re not American companies after all.
而这意味着美国的公司可以利用美国的技术，美国的基础设施，美国的工人－但是，当谈到他们的税款，他们突然宣布不是美国（本土）公司了。
That’s why, this week, the Treasury Department made it more difficult for companies to exploit this loophole and stick the rest of us with the tab.
这就是为什么，本周，财政部加大了让公司滥用此漏洞的难度，并为其他人树立规范。

Together, these steps build on the work we’ve already done to make our tax code fairer and consumer protections stronger.
同时，这些举措建立在更加公平的税法和更健全的消费者保障（之上）。
Because I believe that rather than double down on policies that allow a few at the top to play by their own rules, we should build an economy where everybody has a fair shot, everybody does their fair share, and everybody plays by the same set of rules.
因为我相信，与其在顶层阶级自己规则中双倍征税，（不如）我们应该建立一个人人都有机会获得成功，所有人公平分享，所有人都遵守相同规则的经济。

That’s what this country is all about.
这就是这个国家的全部（意义）。
That’s what we’ve been working toward these past seven years.
这就是我们这七年中的努力。
And that’s what I’m going to keep fighting for as long as I’m your President.
这也是我身为总统将继续努力做的。

Thanks everybody.
感谢大家（倾听）。
Have a great weekend.
并祝周末愉快。

corporation n.大公司
inversion n.逆转
loophole n.漏洞
dignity n.尊贵；高尚
advisor n.顾问
backdoor n.后门 adj.偷偷摸摸的
steer v.驾驶；带领
treasury n.资金；财政收入
infrastructure n.基础设施

take advantage of 利用
fair share 公平分享
… straight months 连续…月
