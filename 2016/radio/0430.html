Weekly Address: It’s Time for the Senate To Do Its Job

每周演讲：参议院应当在此时尽其职责

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President repeated his call for Republicans in the United States Senate to give Chief Judge Merrick Garland a fair hearing and a vote.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统再次呼吁美国参议院共和党为 Merrick Garland 审判长听证并投票表决。
It has been 45 days since President Obama nominated Judge Garland to the Supreme Court.
（今天）已是距离奥巴马总统向最高法院提名 Garland 法官的第 45 天。
The President highlighted that Senate Republicans have said that Judge Garland is a man of experience, integrity and impeccable qualifications.
总统强调，参议院共和党已经承认 Garland 是一位经验丰富，为人正直，资历无暇的法官。
Despite this, most Senate Republicans refused to do their job and give Judge Garland the consideration he deserves.
尽管如此，大多数参议员共和党人士拒绝尽其职责，并拒绝为 Judge Garland 考虑其所应得的。
The President made clear that the Supreme Court must remain above partisan politics, and that’s why the President did his job in nominating Merrick Garland.
总统明确，最高法院必须高于党派政治，而这就是总统尽责提名 Merrick Garland 的原因。
Now, it’s time for the Senate to do its job.
现在，（轮到）参议院是时候尽其职责了。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, April 30, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 4 月 30 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
April 30, 2016
2016 年 4 月 30 日


Text：
正文：

Hi, everybody.
大家好。
It’s now been 45 days since I nominated Judge Merrick Garland to the Supreme Court.
今天已是我向最高法院提名 Merrick Garland 法官的第 45 天。
Judge Garland is a man of experience, integrity, and unimpeachable qualifications.
Judge Garland 经验丰富，为人正直，且资历无暇。
Judge Garland is someone who Senate Republicans are on record saying is “a man of accomplishment and keen intellect;” a man who’s “honest and capable;” a man whose “reputation is beyond reproach.”
Judge Garland 被参议院共和党人誉为”一位成就显著，才智过人（的法官）”；一位”诚实能干”（的法官）；一位”名声不可非议”（的法官）。
Those are all quotes from Republicans in the Senate.
这些（赞誉）均引自参议院的共和党。

But so far, most Senate Republicans have refused to even meet with Judge Garland.
但到现在为止，大多数参议员共和党人士甚至拒绝接见 Judge Garland。
Which means they’ve also refused to do their job and hold a hearing on his nomination, or an up-or-down vote.
这意味着他们同样拒绝尽自己职责，拒绝他的听证会，也拒绝投票表决。
But they’ve still found time to head home for recess over the next week.
但是在下周，他们还是有时间休会之后回家。

This is an abdication of the Senate’s responsibility.
这是参议员放弃（自己的）职责。
Every Supreme Court nominee since 1875 who hasn’t withdrawn from the process has received a hearing or a vote.
从 1875 年以来，所有未被驳回的最高法院被提名者均得到了听证会和投票表决（的资格）。
For over 40 years, there’s been an average of 67 days between a nomination and a hearing.
40 年来，提名与（举行）听证会之间平均间隔 67 天。
This time should be no different.
这个时间不应当再有所不同。
This is not about partisan politics – it’s about upholding the institutions that make our democracy work.
这无关党派政治－这事关保证民主的机构的维持。

There’s a reason Judge Garland has earned the respect of people from both political parties.
Judge Garland 赢得两党人民的尊重缘由于此。
As a young lawyer, he left a lucrative private firm to work in public service.
作为一位年轻的律师，他离开有利可图的私有企业，而选择公共服务业。
He went to oversee the federal response to the Oklahoma City bombing.
他（曾）监督联邦政府对于俄克拉荷马爆炸案的反应。
For the last 19 years, Judge Garland has served on the D.C. Circuit Court – often called “the Second Highest Court in the Land” – and for the past three years, he’s served as that court’s Chief Judge.
在过去的 19 年，Garland 法官曾在特区巡回法庭任职－（特区巡回法庭）被誉为”这片土地上位列第二的最高法院”－并在近三年中，以法院审判长任职。
In fact, Judge Merrick Garland has more federal judicial experience than any other Supreme Court nominee in history.
实际上，Merrick Garland 法官比历史上任何最高法院提名者都更具备联邦司法经验。
With a brilliant mind, a kind spirit, and a good heart, he has dedicated his life to protecting our rights, and ensuring that the voices of everyday Americans are heard.
他用明晰的思维，善良的精神，和仁慈之心，已经将自己的一生奉献于保障我们的权利，并确保每个美国人的心声被关照。

So there is absolutely no reason for Republican Senators to deny him the basic courtesy of a hearing and a vote – the same courtesy that has been extended to others.
所以共和党参议员没有任何理由拒绝他听证会和投票表决的基本礼遇－这些已经延续到其他人的同样礼遇。
This refusal to treat a Supreme Court nomination with the seriousness it deserves is what makes people so cynical about Washington.
这样对最高法院提名者的严肃拒绝，将使人民对华盛顿（首府）产生巨大怀疑。
That’s why poll after poll shows a majority of Americans think Senate Republicans should do their job; give Judge Garland a hearing; and give Judge Garland a vote.
这就是为什么，民调接二连三显示大多数美国人认为参议员共和党应尽其职责；为 Garland 法官听证；并为 Garland 法官投票表决。

For all of our political differences, Americans understand that what unites us is far greater than what divides us.
对于我们所有的政治分歧，美国人理解把我们团结起来（的力量）远大于分裂。
And in the middle of a volatile political season, it is more important than ever that we fulfill our duties – in good faith – as public servants.
而在动荡的政治季中，履行我们的职责比以往更加重要－真心实意－作为人民公仆。
The Supreme Court must remain above partisan politics.
最高法院必须高于党派政治。
I’ve done my job – I nominated someone as qualified as Merrick Garland.
我已经尽我所能－我提名了一位如 Merrick Garland 合乎资格的人。
Now it’s time for the Senate to do their job.
现在，（轮到）参议院是时候尽其职责了。
Give Judge Garland a hearing.
为 Garland 法官听证。
Give Judge Garland an up-or-down vote.
为 Garland 法官投票表决。
Treat him – and our democracy – with the respect they deserve.
对待他们－和我们的民主－用其所值得的尊敬。

Thanks for listening, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），并祝周末愉快。
