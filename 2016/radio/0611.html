Weekly Address: Addressing Puerto Rico’s Economic Crisis

每周演讲：解决波多黎各的经济危机

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, President Obama discussed the crippling economic crisis harming 3.5 million Americans in Puerto Rico.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，奥巴马总统谈到了严重的经济危机正侵袭着美国的 350 万波多黎各人民。
Today, the island is spending over a third of its tax revenue on debt payments – and on July 1, Puerto Rico is facing another 2 billion dollars in debt payments that it cannot make.
今天，该岛将三分之一的税收花费在债务偿还上－而到 7 月 1 日，波多黎各将（又）要面对无法偿还的额外 20 亿美元债务。
The President said the only way for Puerto Rico to overcome this crisis is by restructuring its debt and finding a sustainable path toward growth and opportunity for its people.
总统说到，波多黎各克服危机的唯一出路便是改革其债务，并为人民（提供）发展和机遇从而找到一条可持续的财政道路。
But this requires help from Congress in order to give Puerto Rico the tools it needs to restructure its debt.
但是这需要来自国会的援助，来给予波多黎各改革其债务之所需。
The President commended the House of Representatives, which overwhelmingly passed a bipartisan bill to address the crisis, and called on the Senate to quickly follow suit.
总统称赞众议院，以压倒多数通过了一项两党同意的议案来处理其危机，也呼吁参议院尽快行动，通过议案。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, June 11, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 6 月 11 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
June 11, 2016
2016 年 6 月 11 日


Text：
正文：

Hi, everybody.
大家好。
Today, I want to talk with you about the crisis in Puerto Rico – and why it matters to all of us.
今天，我想和你们谈谈波多黎各的危机－以及为什么这事关我们所有人。

Puerto Ricans are American citizens, just like folks in Maine or Oklahoma or New Mexico.
波多黎各人是美国的公民，就像缅因州，俄克拉何马州，以及新墨西哥州的人们一样。
And over the last decade, Puerto Rico has suffered through a deep and painful recession – but unlike the rest of the United States, it hasn’t recovered.
而过去十年，波多黎各经历了严重和痛苦的衰退－但不像美利坚的其他（州），它仍未复苏。

Today, the island continues to face a crippling economic crisis.
今天，这个岛仍面对经济危机带来的严重后果。
Schools are closing.
学校关门（停课）。
Power is being cut off at homes and hospitals.
房子和医院的电力中断。
Teachers have to choose between turning on the lights or turning on the computers.
老师不得不在打开电灯还是电脑中抉择。
Doctors can’t get medicine to treat newborns unless they pay in cash.
医生无法医治新生儿，除非他（的父母）用现金支付。
And as the Zika virus threatens both the island and the mainland, workers dealing with mosquito control to help protect women and their unborn babies are at risk of being laid off.
而当寨卡病毒肆虐这座岛屿与大陆时，人们冒着被解雇的风险进行蚊虫控制，以帮助保护女性以及其胎儿。

Right now, Puerto Rico is spending about a third of its tax revenue on debt payments – far more than anywhere else in America.
现在，波多黎各将三分之一的税收花费在债务偿还上－（这个比例）远远大于美国的其他地方。
And on July 1, the island faces another 2 billion dollars in debt payments that it cannot pay.
而在 7 月 1 日，该岛又要面对无法偿还的另外 20 亿美元债务。

There is only one way for Puerto Rico to pull itself out of this crisis – and that’s by restructuring its debt and finding a sustainable fiscal path toward growth and opportunity for its people.
波多黎各要想脱离这场危机只有一条路－即改革自己的债务，并为人民（提供）发展和机遇从而找到一条可持续的财政道路。
But here’s the problem.
但是，这有一个问题。
Right now, Puerto Rico doesn’t have the tools it needs to restructure its debt – tools available elsewhere in America.
现在，波多黎各没有改革债务的能力－美国的其他地方所拥有的能力。

And only Congress can fix the problem, and put Puerto Rico on a path to recovery.
而只有国会可以解决这个问题，并将波多黎各推上（经济）恢复的正轨。

Thankfully, this week, the House overwhelmingly passed a bipartisan bill to address the crisis, and I now urge the Senate to move quickly to follow suit.
幸运的是，本周，白宫以压倒多数通过了一项两党同意的议案来处理其危机，而现在，我急切希望参议院尽快行动，通过议案。
This bill won’t cost federal taxpayers a dime.
这项议案不会花费联邦纳税人一分钱。
It doesn’t include special-interest bailouts.
其中没有对特殊利益的紧急财政援助。
And it gives Puerto Rico the ability to restructure its debt, safeguard essential services, and provide important protections to public pensions that more than 300,000 folks rely on to retire with dignity.
而这给予波多黎各改革其债务的能力，保护基础服务（的能力），以及为公共养老金提供重要保护（的能力），这是（事关）超过 30 万市民所依赖的体面退休（的根本）。

This bill also includes something else – a temporary system of oversight to help implement needed reforms and ensure transparency.
这项议案同样包括其他－一个短期的监督系统，以帮助执行所需的改革并确保透明。
I know that some folks in Puerto Rico are worried about this kind of oversight.
我知道一些波多黎各市民担心这种监督。
But I’ve always insisted that any solution to this crisis has to respect the democratic rights of the people of Puerto Rico.
但是我会一直坚持，该危机的任何解决方式必须尊重波多黎各人民的民主权利。
And I am committed to making sure that Puerto Ricans are well-represented in this process, so that we can be sure we’re taking steps that are in the island’s best interests.
而我已承诺确保波多黎各人在这个过程中拥有广泛的代表，因此我们可以确保我们采取的努力是以全岛的最大利益为重。

This bill is not a perfect solution – nobody’s saying it is.
这项议案并非一个完美的解决方案－没有人这么说。
That’s what happens in divided government.
这是分立政府所发生的。
But it’s the only option on the table to save Puerto Rico from spiraling out of control.
但这是摆在台面上的唯一选择，以拯救（即将）失控的波多黎各。
And that’s exactly what would happen if Congress fails to do its job.
而如果国会不能履行其职责，这将注定会发生。

There’s no question this is a trying time for folks in Puerto Rico.
毫无疑问，这对于波多黎各的人们是个尝试的时候了。
They’ve seen too many jobs lost and too many neighbors leave in search of better opportunity elsewhere.
他们已经目睹太多人失业，以及太多人离开，去其他地方找寻更好的机遇。
It’s clear that it’s time for Puerto Rico to chart a new course and make a fresh start.
很明显，波多黎各是时候制定新的规划，并重新开始。
This bill is just a first step.
这项议案仅仅是第一步。

We all have more work to do to make sure that the people of Puerto Rico receive the health care they deserve and the good jobs and economic opportunities they need to build a better future for their kids.
我们每个人都可以尽更大努力来确保，波多黎各人民可以享受到他们应得的医疗保障，以及可以为他们孩子建立一个更好的未来的工作岗位和经济机遇。
And I want the people of Puerto Rico to know that my administration is committed to your success.
而我希望波多黎各人民明白，本届政府将为你们的成功努力。
Because you’re vital to America’s success.
因为你们对于美国的成功至关重要。

That’s what this is all about.
这就是（这项议案）的全部意义。
We don’t turn our backs on our fellow Americans.
我们不会背弃同胞。
We don’t treat folks differently because of where they live.
我们不会因为生活的地方（不同）而区别对待人民。
Instead, we treat each other as Americans.
相反，我们彼此都是美国人。
We come together, especially when it’s hard.
我们团结一致，尤其当他们陷入困境。
That’s how we’ve always set ourselves on a course toward a brighter day.
这就是我们如何总是向着美好明天前进。

Thanks everybody, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），并祝周末愉快。
