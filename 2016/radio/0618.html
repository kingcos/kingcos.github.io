Weekly Address: Standing with Orlando

每周演讲：与奥兰多并肩

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, President Obama reflected on his visit with the families of the victims of the Orlando terrorist attack.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，奥巴马总统深思看望奥兰多恐怖袭击中受害者之行。
He reiterated that we will always stand with those impacted by the Orlando attack – and we will do everything in our power to stop homegrown terrorism attacks and ultimately destroy ISIL.
他重申，我们将永远和被奥兰多袭击影响的人们并肩－并且我们将尽我们所能阻止本土恐怖袭击并粉碎 ISIL。
With Father’s Day coming up, the President emphasized the responsibilities we have to each other – particularly to our children.
随着父亲节到来，总统强调了我们彼此的责任－尤其是对我们的孩子。
Too often, events like these have been followed by silence and inaction.
很多时候，这样的事件在沉默和无为中而沉没。
President Obama said in order for us to raise our children in a more loving, safer world, we must speak up for it.
奥巴马总统说，为了让我们帮助孩子们在一个更加有爱，更加安全的世界，我们必须发声呼吁。
Whether it’s speaking up about the risks guns pose to our communities, or why tolerance and equality matter, our children need to hear us respond to these events and lead our lives with love.
无论这些声音是关于指向我们社区的枪支风险，或是为什么宽容和平等十分重要，我们的孩子需要听到我们对于这些事件的反应，并用爱领导我们的人生。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, June 18, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 6 月 18 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
June 18, 2016
2016 年 6 月 18 日


Text：
正文：

It’s been less than a week since the deadliest mass shooting in American history.
距美国历史上最致命的枪击案还未一周时间。
And foremost in all of our minds has been the loss and the grief felt by the people of Orlando, especially our friends who are lesbian, gay, bisexual, or transgender.
我们的内心为奥兰多人民感到失落和悲伤，尤其我们的女同，男同，双性，或变性的朋友。
I visited with the families of many of the victims on Thursday.
我在周四看望了许多受害者的家人。
And one thing I told them is that they’re not alone.
而我告诉他们的一件事是他们并不孤单。
The American people, and people all over the world, are standing with them – and we always will.
美国人民，以及全世界人民，与他们并肩－并且我们将永远（与他们并肩）。

The investigation is ongoing, but we know that the killer was an angry and disturbed individual who took in extremist information and propaganda over the internet, and became radicalized.
侦查正在开展，但是我们知道杀手是一个愤怒的骚乱分子，在网络获取极端主义信息并受到宣传鼓动，变得激进。
During his killing spree, he pledged allegiance to ISIL, a group that’s called on people around the world to attack innocent civilians.
在他杀戮过程中，他宣称效忠 ISIL，一个号召全世界人民袭击无辜人民的组织。

We are and we will keep doing everything in our power to stop these kinds of attacks, and to ultimately destroy ISIL.
我们正在，也将继续尽我们所能阻止这些类型的袭击，以最终粉碎 ISIL。
The extraordinary people in our intelligence, military, homeland security, and law enforcement communities have already prevented many attacks, saved many lives, and we won’t let up.
情报机构，军队，国土安全局，以及执法社区中的卓越之人已经阻止了许多袭击，解救了许多生命，而我们将不会松懈。

Alongside the stories of bravery and healing and coming together over the past week, we’ve also seen a renewed focus on reducing gun violence.
除了在过去一周的发生的勇敢，愈合，以及团结的故事，我们同样重新着眼于减少枪支暴力。
As I said a few days ago, being tough on terrorism requires more than talk.
正如我几日之前所说，对恐怖分子的强硬需要比空谈（做的）更多。
Being tough on terrorism, particularly the sorts of homegrown terrorism that we’ve seen now in Orlando and San Bernardino, means making it harder for people who want to kill Americans to get their hands on assault weapons that are capable of killing dozens of innocents as quickly as possible.
对恐怖分子强硬，尤其是本土的恐怖主义，如我们现在在奥兰多和圣贝纳迪诺所见，意味着使那些妄想尽可能快地杀害美国人的恐怖分子难以触及杀伤性武器。
That’s something I’ll continue to talk about in the weeks ahead.
这就是我将在未来几周所将谈及的。

It’s also part of something that I’ve been thinking a lot about this week – and that’s the responsibilities we have to each other.
这也是本周我所考虑的一部分－而且这是我们共同的责任。
That’s certainly true with Father’s Day upon us.
这就如父亲节如我们一样。

I grew up without my father around.
我的成长并没有父亲陪伴。
While I wonder what my life would have been like if he had been a greater presence, I’ve also tried extra hard to be a good dad for my own daughters.
当我想象如果父亲在场的生活将会是怎样时，我同样试着付出额外的努力为我的女儿们做一位好父亲。
Like all dads, I worry about my girls’ safety all the time.
就像所有的父亲，我总是担心孩子们的安全。
Especially when we see preventable violence in places our sons and daughters go every day – their schools and houses of worship, movie theaters, nightclubs, as they get older.
尤其当我们看到孩子们每天经过的地方发生可以预防的暴力－孩子们的学校，教堂，电影院，夜店，当他们长大。
It’s unconscionable that we allow easy access to weapons of war in these places – and then, even after we see parents grieve for their children, the fact that we as a country do nothing to prevent the next heartbreak makes no sense.
在这些地方我们允许如此易于持有武器是不合理的－并且，甚至在我们看到父母为他们的孩子感到悲痛之后，我们身为一个国家（却）对预防下一个心碎无所作为的事实（也）说不通。

So this past week, I’ve also thought a lot about dads and moms around the country who’ve had to explain to their children what happened in Orlando.
所以在过去的一周，我也思考了很多关于全国的爸爸妈妈不得不向孩子们解释发生在奥兰多的事。
Time and again, we’ve observed moments of silence for victims of terror and gun violence.
一次又一次，我们已察觉到恐怖主义和枪支暴力受害者的沉默的瞬间。
Too often, those moments have been followed by months of silence.
很多时候，这些瞬间随着时间而沉没。
By inaction that is simply inexcusable.
无所作为完全无法宽恕。
If we’re going to raise our kids in a safer, more loving world, we need to speak up for it.
如果我们继续让我们的孩子生活在一个更加安全，更加有爱的世界，我们必须为其发声。
We need our kids to hear us speak up about the risks guns pose to our communities, and against a status quo that doesn’t make sense.
我们要让孩子们听到我们为枪支对我们社区带来风险的呼声，并反对无意义的现状。
They need to hear us say these things even when those who disagree are loud and are powerful.
他们需要听到我们这样说，哪怕反对的声音响亮有力。
We need our kids to hear from us why tolerance and equality matter – about the times their absence has scarred our history and how greater understanding will better the future they will inherit.
我们需要让孩子们听到为什么宽容和平等十分重要－关于他们缺席危及我们历史的时代，以及理解他们继承的未来将多么美好。
We need our kids to hear our words – and also see us live our own lives with love.
我们需要孩子们听到我们的声音－并同样见证我们用爱生活下去。

And we can’t forget our responsibility to remind our kids of the role models whose light shines through in times of darkness.
而且我们不能忘记自己的责任，提醒孩子们铭记在黑暗中点亮光明的典范。
The police and first responders, the lifesaving bystanders and blood donors.
警察和现场急救员，救生员以及献血者。
Those who comfort mourners and visit the wounded.
他们安慰了悼念者们，并看望伤员。
The victims whose last acts on this earth helped others to safety.
那些最后帮助其他人的安全的受害者。
They’re not just role models for our kids – their actions are examples for all of us.
他们不仅是孩子们的典范－他们的行为是我们所有的榜样。

To be a parent is to come to realize not everything is in our control.
为人父母要知道并不是所有事情在我们的掌控之中。
But as parents, we should remember there’s one responsibility that’s always in our power to fulfill: our obligation to give our children unconditional love and support; to show them the difference between right and wrong; to teach them to love, not to hate; and to appreciate our differences not as something to fear, but as a great gift to cherish.
但是作为父母，我们应当记得我们所能履行的一个责任：给予孩子无条件的爱和支持的义务；为他们指出正确和错误的不同；教会他们去爱，而不去仇恨；以及不要惧怕欣赏我们的不同，而要作为一份巨大的礼物来珍视。

To me, fatherhood means being there.
对我而言，父亲的身份意味着（履行这些责任）。
So in the days ahead, let’s be there for each other.
所以，在以后的日子里，让我们彼此（履行）。
Let’s be there for our families, and for those that are hurting.
让我们为家人，并为那些受伤的人（声援）。
Let’s come together in our communities and as a country.
让我们团结一致，在我们社区，以及作为一个国家。
And let’s never forget how much good we can achieve simply by loving one another.
让我们永远不要忘记，通过彼此支持，我们能够做到多么美好。

Happy Father’s Day to all the dads out there, and have a great weekend.
祝福所有的爸爸父亲节快乐，并祝周末愉快。
