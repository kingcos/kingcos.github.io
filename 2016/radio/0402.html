Weekly Address: Securing the World from Nuclear Terrorism

每周演讲：保护全球免于核恐怖主义

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President spoke from the Nuclear Security Summit on one of the greatest threats to global security—terrorists getting their hands on a weapon of mass destruction, such as a nuclear weapon.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统在核安全峰会发表讲话，（关于）全球安全的最大威胁之一－恐怖分子正将魔爪伸向大规模杀伤性武器－例如核武器。
He discussed the global effort we have been leading to secure the world’s nuclear materials and highlighted the fact that working with other nations, we have removed or secured enough nuclear material for more than 150 nuclear weapons—material that will now never fall into the hands of terrorists.
他谈到，我们所作出的全球努力使得世界核原料被保护，并强调和其他国家合作的事实，我们已经或是保护了（足以制造）超过 150 个核武器的核原料－现在，（这些）原料将绝不会落入恐怖分子之手。
The President also reiterated his commitment to making sure the world remains united and focused on destroying ISIL.
总统同样重申其承诺，以确保世界保持团结，并专心于摧毁 ISIL。
He emphasized that defeating ISIL remains our top priority, and during the summit, he focused on ways to step up our efforts to disrupt terrorist attacks.
他强调，打击 ISIL 是我们的当务之急，他专心于加大努力以打击恐怖主义的方式。
Because of the progress of this week’s summit, and over recent years, more of the world’s nuclear material is secure, and the President will continue to do everything in his power to keep our nation safe and strong and free.
由于本周峰会及最近几年所取得的成就，世界上多数核原料是被保护的，而且总统将尽其所能保卫我们的祖国安全，强大，与自由。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, April 2, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 4 月 2 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
April 2, 2016
2016 年 4 月 2 日


Text：
正文：

Hi, everybody.
大家好。
This week, I’m speaking to you from our Nuclear Security Summit.
本周，我正在核安全峰会向大家发表讲话。
I welcomed more than 50 leaders from around the world to make sure we’re working together to meet one of the greatest threats to global security—terrorists getting their hands on a weapon of mass destruction, like a nuclear weapon.
我迎接了全球超过 50 位领袖人物，以确保我们正共同致力于全球安全的最大威胁之一－恐怖分子正着手大规模杀伤性武器，例如核武器。

Fortunately, because of our efforts so far, no terrorist group has yet succeeded in obtaining a nuclear device or producing a dirty bomb using radioactive materials.
幸运的是，因为迄今为止我们（所作出）的努力，没有恐怖分子团伙成功获取到核装置，也没有成功利用放射性物质制造出放射性炸弹。
But we know that al Qaeda has tried.
但是我们知道基地组织已在尝试。
ISIL has already used chemical weapons in Syria and Iraq.
ISIL 已经在叙利亚和伊拉克使用过化学武器。
And if they ever got hold of a nuclear weapon or nuclear material, we have no doubt they’d use it.
而如果他们曾拥有核武器或核原料，我们确定他们会使用。

That’s why we’ve been leading a global effort to secure the world’s nuclear materials.
这就是为什么我们集全球之力，以保护世界的核原料。
And with summits like this, we’ve made important progress.
如同此次峰会，我们已取得了重要进展。
Working with other nations, we have removed or secured enough nuclear material for more than 150 nuclear weapons—material that will now never fall into the hands of terrorists.
与其他国家（共同）努力，我们已经移走或是保护了（足以制造）超过 150 个核武器的核原料－现在，（这些）原料将绝不会落入恐怖分子之手。

All of South America is now free of these deadly materials.
整个南美洲现在已没有这些致命原料。
Central Europe and Southeast Asia are on track to be free of them later this year.
中欧和东南亚将在今年晚些时候移除它们。
That means that as terrorists and criminal gangs look around for the deadly ingredients for a nuclear device, vast regions of the world are now off limits.
这意味着，当恐怖分子和犯罪团伙四处寻找（制造）核装置的致命原料，现已在世界上的大量区域设为禁区。
This is a remarkable achievement.
这是一个标志性的成就。
And at this summit, we pledged to keep up our efforts to prevent the world’s most deadly networks from obtaining the world’s most deadly weapons.
并且在此次峰会，我们保证继续努力，以阻止从世界最致命的网络上获得世界上最致命的武器。

Our summit was also another opportunity to make sure the world remains united and focused on destroying ISIL.
我们的峰会也是另一个机遇，确保世界保持团结并专心于打击 ISIL。
A majority of the nations who came here are part of our global coalition against ISIL.
与会的大多数国家属于我们对抗 ISIL 的全球联盟部分。
A number of our countries have been targeted by ISIL.
（其中）一些国家已被 ISIL 针对。
Just about all our nations have seen citizens travel to join ISIL in Syria and Iraq.
几乎所有这些国家都已看到有公民到叙利亚和伊拉克以加入 ISIL。

In Syria and Iraq, ISIL continues to lose ground.
在叙利亚和伊拉克，ISIL 接连失利。
Our coalition continues to take out its leaders, including those planning terrorist attacks against our countries.
我们的联盟继续对付其头目，包括那些安排恐怖分子袭击我们这些国家的头目。
They’re losing their oil infrastructure and revenues.
他们正在失去他们的石油设施和收入。
Their morale is suffering.
他们的士气渐颓。

As ISIL is squeezed in Syria and Iraq, it’s lashing out elsewhere, as we’ve seen most recently— and tragically—from Turkey to Brussels.
ISIL 进攻叙利亚和伊拉克，（又）袭击其他地方，因为我们已经从土耳其到布鲁塞尔看到了最近的悲惨（景象）。
During our summit, we focused on ways to step up our efforts to disrupt terrorist attacks.
在我们峰会期间，我们专心于加大努力以打击恐怖主义的方式。
It requires even more cooperation to prevent the flow of foreign terrorist fighters and sharing even more information and intelligence.
这需要更多合作，以阻止他国恐怖主义分子之流，并分享更多的信息和情报。
That’s why I invited all the nations represented at this summit to join us in a broader discussion among our intelligence and security services on how we can improve information sharing to prevent terrorist attacks.
这就是为什么我邀请参与此次峰会的所有国家代表加入我们情报和安全部门的更广泛讨论如何改善信息分享，以阻止恐怖分子袭击。

This continues to be a difficult fight.
这仍然是一个艰难的战斗。
But every day, our dedicated professionals—military, diplomatic, intelligence, counterterrorism, homeland security, law enforcement, nuclear experts—are working to protect us.
但是每一天，我们敬业的专职人员－军队，外交官，情报机构，反恐（机构），国土安全（部），执法（机构），核专家－正努力保护着我们。
Because of the progress we made this week, and over recent years, more of the world’s nuclear material is secure.
由于本周峰会及最近几年所取得的成就，世界上多数核原料是被保护的。
It’s harder for terrorists to get it.
这将使恐怖分子更加难以获得。
And as Commander in Chief, I want you to know that we’re going to keep doing everything in our power to keep our nation safe and strong and free.
作为总司令，我希望你们知道我们正计划尽一切所能保卫我们的祖国安全，强大，与自由。
