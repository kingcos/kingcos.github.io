Weekly Address: Celebrating the National Museum of African American History and Culture

每周演讲：庆祝国家非裔美国人历史与文化博物馆

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, President Obama commemorated the opening of the National Museum of African American History and Culture.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，奥巴马总统出席国家非裔美国人历史与文化博物馆开幕式。
The President recognized the museum for celebrating the many accomplishments of the African American community – and for telling the fuller story of America by facing the uncomfortable truths of our Nation’s history all while embracing the knowledge that America is a constant work in progress.
总统指出，将庆祝许多非美社区的成就-并讲述更加完整的美国故事，通过面对国家历史上不舒服的事实，包括（建设）美利坚是一项持续改进的工作。
The National Museum of African American History and Culture not only tells the African American story – it tells the American story.
国家非裔美国人历史与文化博物馆不仅讲述了非裔美国人的故事；更是美国人传奇的一部分。
By telling the fuller account of the American story, the President said, the museum will give all of us a chance to reflect and set the course for generations to come.
通过讲述一个更加完整的美国传奇，总统谈到，这个博物馆将给予所有人一个机会，以思考并为下一代矫正方向。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, September 24, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 9 月 24 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama as Delivered
贝拉克·奥巴马总统递送的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
September 24, 2016
2016 年 9 月 24 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
This weekend, we’ll dedicate the newest American icon on our National Mall – the National Museum of African American History and Culture.
周末，我们将在国家广场揭牌最新的美国标志-国家非裔美国人历史与文化博物馆。
It’s a beautiful building, five stories high and some 70 feet below the ground, situated just across the street from the Washington Monument.
这是一座宏伟的建筑，（地上）五层楼高，地下约 70 英尺，距华盛顿纪念碑仅有一街之隔。

And this museum tells a story of America that hasn’t always taken a front seat in our national narrative.
而这座博物馆讲述了一个有关美国的故事，美利坚并不总是在我们的国家叙事中拔得头筹。
As a people, we’ve rightfully passed on the tales of the giants who built this country.
作为一个民族，我们理所应当地传承建国立业的伟人故事。
But too often, willful or not, we’ve chosen to gloss over or ignore entirely the experience of millions upon millions of others.
但太多时候，（无论）有意与否，我们选择性掩饰，或是完全忽略了大多数人的经历。

But this museum chooses to tell a fuller story.
但这座博物馆选择讲述一个更加完整的故事。
It doesn’t gauze up some bygone era or avoid uncomfortable truths.
它不为一些很久以前的时代遮羞，也（不为）避免不舒服的事实。
Rather, it embraces the patriotic recognition that America is a constant work in progress; that each successive generation can look upon our imperfections and decide that it is within our collective power to align this nation with the high ideals of our founding.
相反，它包含爱国的辩证，（建设）美利坚是一项持续改进的工作；一代一代的人民可以看到我们的不足，并决心通过我们共同的力量来按照建国之初的高准则校正这个国家。

That’s what you’ll see inside.
这就是你将在（这里）中所看到的。
You’ll see it in the shackles of an enslaved child and in the hope of Harriet Tubman’s gospel hymnal.
你会看到带着手铐被奴隶的孩子，（感受到）Harriet Tubman 福音诗歌中的希望。
You’ll see it in the tragedy of Emmett Till’s coffin and in the resilience of a lunch counter stool and in the triumph of a Tuskegee Airplane.
你会看到 Emmett Till 之死的悲剧，午餐柜台前的静坐（抗议）以及 Tuskegee 飞机的伟大胜利。
You’ll see it in the shadow of a prison guard tower and in the defiance of Jesse Owens’ cleats and in the American pride of Colin Powell’s uniform.
你会看到监狱守卫塔的阴影，Jesse Owens 战靴下的反抗，以及 Colin Powell 制服的美国荣耀。

All of that isn’t simply the African-American story; it’s part of the American story.
所有的这些不仅仅是非裔美国人的故事；更是美国人传奇的一部分。
And so it is entirely fitting that we tell this story on our National Mall, the same place we tell the stories of Washington and Jefferson and our independence; the story of Lincoln who saved our union and the GIs who defended it; the story of King who summoned us all toward the mountaintop.
因此，在国家广场讲述这个故事是完全恰当的，与我们讲述华盛顿，杰弗逊，以及（美国）独立故事是相同的地方；讲述林肯拯救我们联邦以及为此守卫的军人故事（是相同的地方）；讲述在山顶召唤我们的 King 的故事（是相同的地方）。（译者注：最后一个 King 未查到确切资料，不确定是指哪一事件）。

That’s what we’ll celebrate not just this weekend, but in the years and generations ahead – a fuller account of our glorious American story.
这就是我们将不仅仅在这个周末所庆祝的，而是在世世代代中（传颂）-一个更加详尽的美国光辉故事。
It’s a chance to reflect on our past and set a course for the future.
这是一个前事不忘，后事之师的机会。
Because here in this country, all of us, no matter what our station in life, have the chance to pick up the pen, and write our own chapter for our time.
因为在这个国家，我们所有人，不论身处人生的哪一站，都有机会拾起笔墨，书写我们时代的篇章。

Thanks everybody, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家，并祝周末愉快。
