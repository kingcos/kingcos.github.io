Weekly Address: President Obama’s Supreme Court Nomination

每周演讲：奥巴马总统的最高法院提名

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President discussed his decision to nominate Chief Judge Merrick Garland to the Supreme Court of the United States.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统谈到了，他对于提名 Merrick Garland 作为美国最高法院审判长的决定。
Chief Judge Garland has earned the respect of both Democrats and Republicans through his years of public service.
Garland 审判长已通过其在公共服务业的（的努力），双双赢得了民主党和共和党的尊重。
The President made clear that even though we are in the midst of a volatile political season, we should treat the appointment of a Supreme Court justice seriously.
总统明确，尽管我们正处在动荡的政治季，（但是）我们应当严肃对待最高法院审判长的提名。
The President repeated his call for Republicans in the United States Senate to give Chief Judge Garland a fair hearing and a vote.
总统重申其呼吁，美国参议院的共和党应为 Garland 审判长听证并投票表决。
The President did his job.
总统已尽其职责。
Now Senators should do theirs and quickly move to consider the President’s nominee.
现在，参议院应尽其职并尽快考虑总统的提名。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, March 19, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 3 月 19 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
March 19, 2016
2016 年 3 月 19 日


Text：
正文：

Hi, everybody.
大家好。
One of the most consequential responsibilities our Constitution grants a President is appointing a Supreme Court Justice.
我们的宪法所授予总统的最大权利之一便是任命最高法院审判长。
The men and women who sit on the Supreme Court safeguard our rights.
最高法院中的人守护着我们的权利。
They ensure that ours is a system of laws, not of men.
他们确保我们处在法律系统之下，而不是人（译者注：即法治，非人治）。
And they’re given the essential task of applying the principles written into our founding documents to the most challenging questions of today.
而他们所被给予的基本任务，即应用这些写进建国文件中的原则，变成了现今的最具挑战的问题。

So this is a duty I take very seriously.
所以这是我非常严肃对待的责任。
It requires me to set aside short-term politics in order to maintain faith with our founders.
这需要让我将短期政策搁置一旁，以维持我们建国者的信仰。
And on Wednesday, after weeks of consultations with Republicans, Democrats, and leaders across the country, I selected a nominee whose unmatched experience and integrity have earned him the respect and admiration of both parties – Chief Judge Merrick Garland.
而在周三，和共和党，民主党，以全国的负责人（进行）商讨会后的数周，我选择了一位被任命者，其拥有无与伦比的经验和正直（的人格），这已使他赢得了两党的尊敬和敬佩－Merrick Garland 审判长。

Judge Garland grew up in my hometown of Chicago, with parents who taught him to work hard and deal fairly.
Garland 法官成长于我的故乡，芝加哥，与教他努力工作并（为官）公平父母（一起）。
As a young lawyer, he left a lucrative private firm to work for half as much in public service.
作为一位年轻的律师，他离开了可以发大财的私企，而献身于公共服务业。
Eventually, he oversaw the federal response to the Oklahoma City bombing, working side-by-side with first responders, victims, and their families to bring justice for an unspeakable crime.
之后，他（曾）监督联邦政府对于俄克拉荷马爆炸案的反应，与现场急救员，受害者，以及他们的家人并肩作战，为这场可怕的罪行带来正义。
And everywhere he went during that investigation, he carried with him in his briefcase the program from the memorial service with each of the victims’ names inside.
而在调查中的任何地方，他的公文包里都放着从追悼会（拿到）的遇害者的名单程序。

For the last 19 years, Judge Garland has served on what’s known as “the second highest court in the land” – the D.C. Circuit Court – including the last three years as Chief Judge.
在过去的 19 年，Garland 法官已在被誉为 “这片土地上位列第二的最高法院”－特区巡回法院（任职）－并在近三年中，以法院审判长（职位）任职。
On the bench, he’s shown a dedication to protecting our basic rights.
在担任法官职务中，他展现了，一种保卫我们基本权利的奉献精神。
A conviction that powerful voices must not be allowed to drown out those of everyday Americans.
（展现了）一种决不允许强权之声压过普通美国人的信念。
An understanding that justice isn’t simply abstract legal theory; it affects people’s daily lives.
（展现了）一种司法并不是简单抽象的法律理论的理解；这将影响人们的日常生活。
And a spirit of decency, modesty, and even-handedness in his work.
以及一种工作中的正派，谦逊，以及公平的意志。
Judge Garland is admired for his courtesy, his devotion to family, and his civic-mindedness – for the past 18 years, he’s served as a tutor for young students at a local D.C. elementary school.
Garland 法官因有礼教，对家庭忠诚，以及公民意识与社会责任被人称赞－在过去的 18 年里，他曾在本地的特区小学作为青少年的导师。

During my time as President, through three separate Supreme Court appointments, in conversations with Republicans and Democrats alike, one name came up more than any other – Merrick Garland.
在我任职总统期间，通过三个独立的最高法院任命，在和共和党与民主党对话中，一个人比其他任何都更合适－Merrick Garland。

I understand that we’re in the middle of an especially noisy and volatile political season.
我知道我们正处在十分嘈杂且动荡的政治季中。
But at a time when our politics are so polarized; when norms and customs of our political rhetoric seem to be corroding – this is precisely the time we should treat the appointment of a Supreme Court justice with the seriousness it deserves.
但在这样一个我们的政治如此两极分化的时间；当（由于）政治辞令，规范和习俗似乎受到侵蚀之时；这正是我们用其应得的严肃，来对待最高法院审判长的任命。
Because our Supreme Court is supposed to be above politics, not an extension of politics.
因为我们的最高法院应高于政治，而不是政治的延伸。
And it should stay that way.
而这应该保持下去。

So I ask Republicans in the Senate to give Judge Garland the respect he has earned.
所以我要求参议院的共和党给予 Garland 法官应得的尊重。
Give him a hearing.
为 Garland 法官听证。
Give him an up-or-down vote.
为 Garland 法官投票表决。
To deny it would be an abdication of the Senate’s Constitutional duty.
拒绝（听证和投票表决）将是参议院对宪法规定的职责放弃。
It would indicate a process for nominating and confirming judges that is beyond repair.
这将使得提名和确定法官的进程无法弥补。
It would make it increasingly impossible for any President, Republican or Democrat, to carry out their Constitutional function.
这将使其对任何总统，（不论）共和党或是民主党，执行其宪法规定的职能变得越来越不可能。
To go down that path would jeopardize our system of justice, it would hurt our democracy, and betray the vision of our founding.
走这样的路将会危及我们的司法系统，这将损害我们的民主，也违背我们的建国愿景。

I fulfilled my Constitutional duty.
我已尽宪法规定的职能。
Now it’s time for Senators to do theirs.
现在，参议院应当在此时尽其职责。
I hope that they take the time to reflect on the importance of this process to our country.
我希望他们将时间花在思考我们国家这一进程的重要性上。
I hope that they’ll act fairly.
我希望他们会公平行事。
And I hope they’ll work in a bipartisan fashion to confirm Merrick Garland to the Supreme Court.
而且我希望他们将以两党支持的方式批准 Merrick Garland 进入最高法院。
That’s how we can uphold our pledge to liberty and justice for all – for our time and for generations to come.
这就是我们对所有人如何保证自由和司法的诺言－对我们的时代，以及未来的一代。

Thanks everybody.
感谢大家（倾听）。
Have a good weekend.
并祝周末愉快。
