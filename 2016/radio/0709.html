Weekly Address: Standing Together to Stop the Violence

每周演讲：共同阻止暴力

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — Vice President Joe Biden commemorated the lives of the five police officers who were killed and the seven people who were wounded in Dallas.
华盛顿特区－副总统乔·拜登缅怀了在达拉斯五名被杀害的警察以及七位伤者。
The police officers were providing safety to those who were peacefully marching against racial injustices in the criminal justice system – and the shocking images of the lives lost in St. Paul and Baton Rouge.
这些警察曾为那些在司法体系中和平抗议种族不公平的人们保卫安全。
Echoing the remarks of Dallas Mayor Mike Rawlings and Police Chief David Brown, the Vice President called on the American people to act with unity and to stop the violence.
达拉斯市长 Mike Rawlings 和警察局长 David Brown 的讲话，副总统呼吁美国人民团结一致，并阻止暴力。
He emphasized that it is the responsibility of everyone to speak out against disparities within the criminal justice system, just as much as it is the responsibility of everyone to stand up for the police who protect us every day.
他强调，这是每一个人的责任，来呼吁抵制司法体系中悬殊的差异，就像没一个我警察每天保护我们。
Because together, we as a nation will persevere and overcome.
因为团结，作为一个国家我们将坚韧不拔，并克服困难。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, July 9, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 7 月 9 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of Vice President Joe Biden as Prepared for Delivery
乔·拜登副总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
July 9, 2016
2016 年 7 月 9 日


Text：
正文：
Although I didn’t know the five police officers who were killed, or the seven who were wounded in Dallas this week – I knew them.
尽管我不能（清楚）记得本周在达拉斯被杀害的五名警员以及七名伤员－（但是）我了解他们。

They were the folks I grew up with: The boy with the most courage and the most compassion; the man with a brave heart and a generous soul, whose words were always encouraging; the son who made his mother proud every time he turned and smiled at her; and the friend who you could always count on.
他们是和我一起成长的人：（他们是）最有勇气又富有慈悲的男孩；拥有一颗勇敢的心和宽容之魂的男人，他们的话语总是鼓舞人心；当他冲母亲转身或是微笑，母亲次次为之自豪的儿子；以及我们总是依靠的朋友。
Being a cop wasn’t just what they did.
身为一名警察不仅仅是尽其职责。
It was who they were—like every officer who joined for essentially the same reason.
正是他们－如同每一个因本质上的相同理由而加入的警员一样。
There was something about them that made them think they could help, that they should serve, that they had a duty.
使他们认为能够伸出援手，应该服役，因为他们有这个责任。

So when an assassin’s bullet targeted the police force in Dallas, it touched the soul of the nation.
所以，当暗杀者的子弹瞄准达拉斯的警察，这件事触动了全国人的心。
Those killed and wounded were protecting the safety of those who were peacefully protesting against racial injustices in the criminal justice system.
这些死伤者曾为那些在司法体系中和平抗议种族不公平的人们保卫安全。
Those who were marching against the kind of shocking images we saw in St. Paul and Baton Rouge—and have seen too often elsewhere—of too many black lives lost.
他们曾游行反对我们在圣保罗和巴吞鲁日市看到的那种令人震惊的景象－以及在其他地方尤其常见－太多的黑人生命被掠夺。

I believe the Dallas Police Department is one of the finest in the nation—and this incredibly diverse city can bridge any divide.
我相信达拉斯警察局是这个国家最优秀的（警局）之一－并且这个令人难以置信的多样化城市可以跨越一切对立。
To paraphrase Dallas Mayor Mike Rawlings, let us use our words carefully.
当我们提到达拉斯市长 Mike Rawlings，我们应该斟酌字句。
Let us act with unity, not division.
让我们团结，而不是分裂。
As Dallas Police Chief David Brown—one of the leading chiefs in America—said, “There are no words to describe the atrocity that occurred to our city, all I know is that this must stop, this divisiveness between our police and our citizens.”
作为达拉斯警察局长 David Brown－美国领导局长之一－说，”没有言语来形容发生在我们城市的（这场）残暴事件，我只知道这必须阻止，阻止我们警察和市民之间的分裂。”

As Americans, we are wounded by all of these deaths.
作为美国人，我们为亡者伤痛。
It’s on all of us to stand up, to speak out about disparities in our criminal justice system—just as it’s on all of us to stand up for the police who protect us in our communities every day.
这需要我们所有人站起来，为司法体系中的悬殊差异呼喊－仅仅因为这需要我们所有人为那些每日在社区保护我们的警察撑腰。
In the days and weeks ahead, we’ll continue offering our thoughts and prayers to provide comfort to the broken-hearted families.
在今后的几日几周，我们将继续牵挂和祷告，为那些心碎的家庭带去安慰。
But they will only be redeemed by the courage of our actions that honor their memories.
而他们将被我们怀念他们的行为的勇气而救赎。

So while we’re being tested, we can’t be pulled apart.
所以当我们面临考验，我们不能一盘散沙。
We are America, with bonds that hold us together.
我们是美利坚，团结是我们之间的纽带。
We endure, we persevere, we overcome, we stand together.
我们坚韧，我们坚持，我们克服困难，我们团结一致。

commemorate v.纪念
disparity n.悬殊的差异
compassion n.同情
assassin n.暗杀者
divisiveness n.分歧
redeem v.赎回；兑现

marching against 游行示威
racial injustice 种族不公平
bridge divide 跨越对立
