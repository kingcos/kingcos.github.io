Weekly Address: It’s Time to Give Families Access to Paid Sick Leave

每周演讲：是时候让更多的家庭有机会享有带薪病假

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, President Obama discussed the importance of ensuring that American families have access to paid leave.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，奥巴马总统谈到了要确保让美国家庭有机会享有带薪休假的重要性。
Today, millions of Americans do not have access to paid sick leave and have to face tough choices when their families face illness – choices that could risk their jobs or their health.
现今，数百万的美国人不能享有带薪病假，当家人生病时不得不面对艰难的选择-选择（的结果）可能会使他们的工作（丢失）或是危及健康。
The President has repeatedly called on Republicans in Congress to pass a law guaranteeing most workers the chance to earn seven days of paid sick leave each year.
总统一再呼吁国会中的共和党通过一项法律，以保证大多数工人每年拥有七天的带薪病假日。
To this day, no action has been taken in the Republican Congress.
到现在，共和党参议员无动于衷。
But, that hasn’t stopped the President from taking action where he can to help more Americans access paid sick time, and starting January 1st, federal contractors will be required to give their employees working on new federal contracts up to seven paid sick days each year.
但是，这并没有阻止总统采取行动来帮助更多美国享有带薪病假的时间，并且从 1 月 1 日起，联邦承包商将被要求必须给予其雇员每年七天的带薪病假日。
That’s because paid leave isn’t something that just nice to have – it’s a must have.
因为带薪休假并不是可有可无的-（而）是必须拥有的。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, October 1, 2016.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2016 年 10 月 1 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama as Delivered
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲递送
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
October 1, 2016
2016 年 10 月 1 日


Text：
正文：

There are a couple different stories you can tell about our economy.
关于我们的经济，你能听到许多不同的故事。

One goes like this.
其中有一个是这样的。
Eight years after the worst economic crisis of our lifetimes, our economy has created jobs for 71 straight months.
在经历了一生中最严重的经济危机之后的八年，我们的经济已经连续 71 个月创造了（更多的）工作岗位。
That’s a new record.
这是一个新的纪录。
Unemployment has fallen below five percent.
失业率下降到百分之五以下。
Last year, the typical household saw its income grow by about twenty-eight hundred dollars – the biggest one-year increase ever.
去年，普通家庭的收入增长了约 2800 美元-史上增长幅度最大的一年。
And the uninsured rate is at an all-time low.
而且未保险率处于历史最低点。

All that is true.
这一切都是真实的。
What’s also true is that too much of our wealth is still taken by the top – and that leaves too many families still working paycheck to paycheck, without a lot of breathing room.
同样真实的是我们的大多数财富掌握在顶层-而太多的家庭仍然依靠薪水生存，没有太多喘息的空间。

There are two things we can do about this.
为此我们能做两件事。
We can prey on people’s worries for political gain.
我们可以将人们的担心转移到政治掠夺。
Or we can actually do something to help working families feel more secure in today’s economy.
或者我们可以付出实际行动，帮助工薪家庭在当今经济中感到安全。

Count me in the latter camp.
把我算到（支持）后者的阵营中（译者注：即奥巴马支持后者）。
And here’s one thing that will help right away: making sure more of our families have access to paid leave.
而这里有一件事将立即奏效：确保更多的家庭有机会带薪休假。

Today, having both parents in the workforce is an economic necessity for many families.
今天，劳动力拥有双亲已成为许多家庭的经济必要。
But right now, millions of Americans don’t have access to even a single day of paid sick leave.
但现在，成千上万的美国人甚至无法享受到仅仅一天的带薪病假。
So if you get sick, that sticks you with a lousy choice.
所以如果你生病了，这将使你陷入糟糕的选择。
Do you go to work and get everyone else sick, too?
你是选择继续上班，也传染给其他人？
Or do you take care of yourself at the risk of a paycheck?
还是冒着没有薪金的风险照料自己？
If your kid gets sick, do you send her to school anyway?
如果你的孩子生病了，你还送她去学校吗？
Or do you stay home to take care of her, lose a day’s pay, and maybe even put your own job at risk?
还是呆在家里照顾她，失去一天的工资，甚至冒着失业的风险？

We shouldn’t have to make choices like that in America.
在美国，我们没有必要必须作出这样的选择。
That’s why I’ve repeatedly called on the Republican Congress to pass a law guaranteeing most workers in America the chance to earn seven days of paid sick leave each year.
这就是为什么我一再呼吁共和党议会通过一项法律，保障大多数美国的工人每年拥有七天带薪病假的机会。
Of course, Congress hasn’t acted.
当然，国会没有回应。
But we’ve also worked with states, cities, and businesses to get the job done – and many have, pointing to research showing that paid leave actually helps their bottom line.
但是我们同样与州，城市，以及企业来合作落实这项议案-许多人指出，研究表明带薪休假改善了他们底线。
In fact, since I took office, another ten million private sector workers have gained paid sick leave – making up a record share of our workforce.
事实上，自我上任以来，另外的一千万私营部门的员工已经获得了带薪病假（的权利）-创造了我们劳动力中的纪录。

Unfortunately, there are still about 40 million private sector workers who don’t get a single day.
不幸的是，仍有四千万私营部门的工人不能（带薪病假）哪怕一天。
That’s why I’m doing what I can on my own.
这就是为什么我在尽己所能。
Effective on January 1st, federal contractors will be required to give their employees working on new federal contracts up to seven paid sick days each year.
从 1 月 1 日起，联邦承包商将被要求必须给予其雇员每年七天的带薪病假日。
That’s happening.
这，即将生效。
It will help about one million workers when they or a loved one gets sick.
这将帮助约一百万工人，当他们或是心爱之人生病之时。
It will cover time you need for preventive care.
这将涵盖必要的预防性护理时间。
It will cover absences resulting from domestic violence or sexual assault.
这将涵盖家庭暴力或性侵造成的缺勤。
And it means everyone else is less likely to catch what someone else has got – whether it’s a coworker or the person preparing or serving your food.
而且这意味着每个人都不太可能顶替他人-无论是一起工作的人，还是为你准备或供应食物的人。

Paid sick leave isn’t a side issue, or a women’s issue, or something that’s just nice to have.
带薪病假不是一个次要问题，不是女性问题，也不是可有可无。
It’s a must-have.
这是必需的。
By the way, so are economic priorities like child care, paid family leave, equal pay, and a higher minimum wage.
也如同一些经济优先议案，例如儿童保险，带薪家庭假，同工同酬，以及更高的法定最低工资。
We need a Congress that will act on all these issues, too, because they’d make a meaningful difference in the lives of millions of Americans who are working hard every day.
我们需要一个对这些问题同样采取实际行动的国会，因为这些（议案）将对数百万每天辛勤工作的美国人的生活产生有意义的影响。
It’s more than talk – it’s action.
这不仅仅是谈论-而是行动。
And that’s what you should demand of every politician who wants the privilege to serve you.
而就是你们应当向每一个想要为（人民）服务的政治家所要求的。

Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家，并祝周末愉快。

contractor n.承包商
necessity n.必要
lousy adj.非常糟糕的
assault n./v.袭击
coworker n.同事
privilege n.特权；荣幸

paid leave 带薪休假
paid sick leave 带薪病假
