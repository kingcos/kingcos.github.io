Weekly Address: Creating Opportunity for All

每周演讲：为所有人创造机会

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President highlighted the importance of expanding opportunity for all Americans – a principle that has guided his work throughout the past six years.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统强调了扩大每个美国人机会的重要性——（这）是在过去六年中指导他工作的原则。
This past week, the President attended a summit at Georgetown University where he discussed issues like poverty and inequality, and what we can do to ensure everyone gets a fair shot.
在过去的一周，总统在乔治城大学（Georgetown University）出席了高层会议，在那里他就一些问题展开了讨论，例如贫穷和不平等，还有我们可以为保障每个人享有公平的机会而做些什么。
We’ve seen real results in this area, but there is still more that can be done.
我们已经看到了这方面的实际结果，但是（我们）为此可以做得更多。
And lack of opportunity is not the only barrier to success.
并且机会的缺失（并）不是通向成功的唯一障碍。
That’s why, on Monday, the President will travel to Camden, New Jersey to visit with local law enforcement, meet with young people, and hear directly about efforts to build trust between the police and the community in a city that has faced one of the highest crime rates in America.
这就是为什么，下周一，总统将前往新泽西州（New Jersey）的卡姆登（Camden）访问当地的执法机构，与年轻人见面，并在全美犯罪率最高的城市之一，直接听取（有关）警察与社会之间建立信任（所做的）努力。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, May 16, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 5 月 16 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
May 16, 2015
2015 年 5 月 16 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
Everything we’ve done over the past six years has been in pursuit of one overarching goal: creating opportunity for all.
在过去六年中，我们所做的一切（都）是为了追求一个中心目标：为所有人创造机会。

What we’ve long understood, though, is that some communities have consistently had the odds stacked against them.
然而长久以来我们了解到，在一些社区，不平等一直制约着他们。
That’s true of rural communities with chronic poverty.
这（样的事实）对于农村长期贫困的社区而言就是真实存在的。
That’s true of some manufacturing communities that suffered after the plants they depended on closed their doors.
这（样的事实）对于一些制造业社区而言（也）是真实存在的，即那些（人们）所依赖的临近家门的工厂遭到重创的社区。
It’s true of some suburbs and inner cities, where jobs can be hard to find and harder to get to.
在一些郊区和内陆城市这同样真实存在，这些地方工作难以找到，（而且）上班更加艰难。

That sense of unfairness and powerlessness has helped to fuel the kind of unrest that we’ve seen in places like Baltimore, Ferguson, and New York.
（民众的）不公平和无力感已经促使一些地区的社会些许的不稳定，比如巴尔的摩（Baltimore），弗格森（Ferguson）和纽约。
It has many causes – from a basic lack of opportunity to groups feeling unfairly targeted by police – which means there’s no single solution.
这有很多（方面的）原因——从机会的基本缺乏到警察针对感到不公平的群众——这意味着没有单一的解决方法。
But there are many that could make a different and could help.
但是有很多与众不同能够有所帮助的方案。
And we have to do everything in our power to make this country’s promise real for everyone willing to work for it.
并且我们必须尽我们所能让这个国家的承诺对于每个愿意努力的人来说是真实可及的。

That’s why last Tuesday, at a summit organized by Catholics and evangelicals, I sat down with a conservative scholar and a poverty expert for a discussion on what it takes to open more doors of opportunity.
这就是为什么，上周二在一个由天主教（Catholics）和基督教福音派（evangelicals）组织的会议上，我与一个保守派学者和一个（研究）贫困的专家坐在一起讨论（如果）机会之门敞开将会导致什么。
We know our efforts matter: since 1967, we’ve brought poverty down by about 40 percent, thanks in part to programs like Social Security and the Earned Income Tax Credit for working families.
我们知道我们的努力（十分）重要：从 1967 年开始，我们已将贫困率降低了约 40%，这部分要归功于例如社会保险制度（Social Security）和针对工薪家庭的所得税抵免政策（Earned Income Tax Credit）的实施。
And we know that there are folks from all faiths, and across the ideological spectrum, who care deeply about “the least of these.”
并且我们知道，不同信仰，和不同观念的人们，他们深深地关心着”最卑微的一族”。
So I hope this conversation continues, not as a question of whether, but of how, we can work together to grow opportunity.
所以，我希望这个对话可以继续下去，并不是作为一个”是否”的问题，而是”怎样做”，我们（才能）争取更多的机会。
Because it’s not words, but deeds, that make a difference.
因为这不是空言，而是实干，并带来改变。
And from expanding tax cuts for working parents, to raising high school graduation rates, to helping millions of Americans secure health insurance when they didn’t have it just a few years ago – our actions are making a difference.
并且从扩大工薪阶层的税务减免，到提高高中毕业率，再到帮助千百万美国人享有可靠的健康保险，他们多年前还不曾拥有——（那时，）我们的行动（才）正在发挥效用。

Of course, lack of opportunity is not the only barrier between too many of our young people and the kind of future they deserve.
当然，机会的缺失并不是我们的年轻人与他们应得的未来之间的唯一障碍。
On Monday, I’ll travel to Camden, New Jersey, a city that has faced one of the highest violent crime rates in America.
下周一，我将前往新泽西州（New Jersey）的卡姆登（Camden），一个面临全美犯罪率最高的城市之一。
I’ll highlight some of the innovative things they’ve done to help police do their jobs more safely and reduce crime in the process.
我将强调他们所执行的一些创新性的措施，来协助警察更加安全地开展工作并在这个过程中减少犯罪。
And I’ll highlight steps all cities can take to maintain trust between the brave law enforcement officers who put their lives on the line, and the communities they’re sworn to serve and protect.
同样，我将强调所有城市可以采取的措施，在不惜生命勇敢的执法人员与他们曾宣誓要服务和保护的社区之间保持信任。

Whether we are Democrats, Republicans, or independents; whether we live in one of our poorest communities, one of our wealthiest, or anywhere in between, we all want our country to be one where hard work pays off and responsibility is rewarded.
无论我们是民主党人（Democrats），或是共和党人（Republicans），又或是无党派人士；无论我们生活在最贫穷的社区之一，或是在最富有的社区之一，又或是在介于前两者之间的社区。我们都希望我们生活在一个付出能有回报并且功夫可以不负有心人的国家。
We want a place where you can make it if you try.
我们希望有一个地方，如果你付出努力便可以获得（应得的）成功。
That’s the promise we make to our young people.
这是我们向年轻人所作出的承诺。
That’s the promise that makes us exceptional.
这是可以让我们变得出色的承诺。
And it’s the promise I’ll never stop fighting to keep, for my children and for yours.
并且这是我将为你我的孩子们不遗余力、坚持奋斗的承诺。

Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快。
