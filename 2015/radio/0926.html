Weekly Address: Dispose of Your Expired and Unwanted Prescription Drugs

每周演讲：处理掉过期且不需要的处方药

返回

Overview：
概览：

In this week’s address, on “National Prescription Drug Take-Back Day”, the President spoke about the importance of preventing and treating substance use disorders.
本周演讲中，在”全国处方药物回收日”，总统谈到了预防和处理药物使用混乱的重要性。
Overdoses from prescription pain medications kill thousands of Americans every year, and more often than not, those drugs come from the family medicine cabinet.
每年，成千上万的美国人死于服用过度计量的止痛处方药，并且往往，这些药物来自家庭医药箱。
In addition, many heroin users started out by misusing prescription drugs.
另外，许多海洛因吸食者因滥用处方药而受沾染。
That’s why it’s important to take advantage of the DEA’s National Prescription Drug Take-Back Day and safely, conveniently, and responsibly dispose of expired and unwanted prescription drugs at collection sites throughout your community—no questions asked.
这就是为什么要利用禁药取缔机构的全国处方药物回收日的重要性，并安全地，便利地，负责任地通过你们社区的（处方药）收集点销毁过期且不需要的处方药－（这一点）毋庸置疑。
Drug disposal programs are part of the President’s 2011 Prescription Drug Abuse Prevention Plan, which also included increasing education for prescribers, expanding Prescription Drug Monitoring Programs, and pursuing Smart on Crime enforcement.
药物处置项目是总统 2011 年度预防处方药滥用计划的一部分，其中同样包括加大对处方药使用者的教育，扩大处方药监管程序，并在犯罪强制中追求更智慧（的方案）。
In his address, the President called on us all to continue to work to reduce substance use disorders through evidence-based treatment, prevention, and recovery.
在他的演讲中，总统呼吁我们所有人继续通过基础治疗，预防和恢复以减少药物滥用。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, September 26, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 9 月 26 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
September 26, 2015
2015 年 9 月 26 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
Today, September 26th, is “National Prescription Drug Take-Back Day”.
今天，9 月 26 日，是”全国处方药物回收日”。
It’s a day where you can safely, conveniently, and responsibly dispose of expired and unwanted prescription drugs at collection sites in your community.
今天你可以安全地，便利地，并且负责任地在你们社区的收集点处理掉过期且不需要的处方药。

Here’s why this matters.
其意义所在就在此。
More Americans now die every year from drug overdoses than they do in car crashes.
现在，每年死于服用药物过量的美国人已经超过车祸（所造成的死亡）。
And most of those deaths aren’t due to drugs like cocaine or heroin – but rather prescription drugs.
并且，其中大部分的死亡并不是因为可卡因和海洛因等毒品－而是处方药。
In 2013 alone, overdoses from prescription pain medications killed more than 16,000 Americans.
仅仅 2013 年一年，超过 16,000 的美国人死于服用过量的止痛处方药。
And most young people who begin misusing prescription drugs don’t buy them in some dark alley – they get them from the medicine cabinet.
并且大多数开始滥用处方药的年轻人并不是在黑暗的小巷买的（处方药）－他们是从（自己家的）医药箱拿到的。

If that’s not a good enough reason to participate in “National Prescription Drug Take-Back Day”, here’s another.
如果这不是一个足够好的理由（说服你）参加全国处方药物回收日，还有另一个（原因）。
Many prescription pain medications belong to the same class of drugs as heroin.
许多处方止痛药和海洛因类的毒品同属一类。
In fact, four in five heroin users started out by misusing prescription drugs.
实际上，4/5 的海洛因吸食者是（因为）滥用处方药而沾染的。
And over the course of just one year, between 2013 and 2014, we saw a 33% increase in the number of heroin users.
并且仅仅在 2013-2014 年间，海洛因吸食者的数量增长了 33%。

All of this takes a terrible toll on too many families, in too many communities, all across the country – big and small, urban and rural.
所有的这些严重的毁坏了太多的家庭，遍布太多的社区，遍及全国上下－或大或小的城市和乡村。
It strains law enforcement and treatment programs.
这使得执法机构和治疗项目变得紧张。
It costs all of us – in so many different ways.
这是在消费我们所有人－以各种不同的方式。

That’s why, four years ago, my Administration unveiled a Prescription Drug Abuse Prevention Plan.
这就是为什么，四年前，我的政府推出了预防处方药滥用计划。
We’ve been partnering with communities to combat overdoses, and we’re seeing some promising results.
我们已经联合了社区打击药物滥用，并且我们已经看到了一些可喜的结果。
That’s why the budget I put forward this year would build on those efforts.
这就是为什么今年我将建立在这些成效之上（继续）提升预算。
It would make critical investments in things like drug monitoring programs, equipping more first responders to save more lives, and expanding medication-assisted treatment programs – including in our prisons.
这将使得重要的投资可以投入例如药物监管项目，装备更多的应急人员来拯救更多的生命，和扩大医疗救助治疗项目－包括我们的监狱。

In fact, getting smarter about how we address substance use disorders is a vital part of reforming our criminal justice system.
实际上，如何更聪明处理药物滥用是改革刑事司法制度中必不可少的部分。
Rather than keep spending billions of taxpayer dollars on needlessly long prison sentences for nonviolent drug offenders, we could save money and get better outcomes by getting treatment to those who need it.
而不是继续不必要地花费纳税人数十亿美元判处非暴力毒品罪犯长期监禁，我们可以节省开支并使需要治疗的人们拥有更好的结果。
And we could use some of the savings to make sure the brave men and women of law enforcement have the resources they need to go after drug kingpins and violent gangs, disrupt the flow of drugs into our country, and address the real threats to our communities.
并且，我们可以使用其中的一些钱来确保执法机构勇敢的人们有他们需要的资源，追查毒品关键（信息）和暴力团伙，中断毒品在我国的流通，并处理我们社区中的真正恐吓。

With no other disease do we expect people to wait until they’re a danger to themselves or others to self-diagnose and seek treatment.
仅仅由于这种疾病我们希望民众等待，直到知道他们自身面临危险或其他人自我诊断并寻找医疗。
So we should approach abuse as an opportunity to intervene, not incarcerate.
所以我们应该将处理滥用作为介入的一个契机，而不是囚禁。
And we all have a role to play here.
并且我们都可以在这里发挥作用。
Parents, we have to understand how important it is to talk to our kids, and to safely store medications in the house.
（作为）父母，我们必须懂得和自己孩子交谈的重要性，以及安全地在房子里储存药物。
The medical community has to be engaged, too – because better prescribing practices will make a difference.
医学界也必须保证－因为更好的处方工作将带来不同的（体验）。

And as a country, we have to keep working to reduce drug use through evidence-based treatment, prevention, and recovery.
作为一个国家，我们必须继续通过基础治疗，预防和恢复以减少药物滥用。
Because research shows it works.
因为研究表明其有成效。
Courageous Americans show it works, every day.
每一天，勇敢的美国人也展示了其可以成功。
That’s why the man I named to head the office of National Drug Control Policy – Michael Botticelli – is a man in long-term recovery himself.
这就是为什么我提名他领导国家药物管控政策办公室－Michael Botticelli－他自己便是一名长期恢复的人。
He talks about it openly and honestly, precisely to strike down the shame and stigma that too often keep people from seeking care before it’s too late.
他说道，这是开放地，诚实地，并恰好地在为时已晚前打消了往往人们在就医时的遗憾和耻辱。

This is something I’ll be talking about more in the weeks to come, in communities across the country.
这就是我将在未来几周谈到的事情，在遍及全国的社区。
Because it’s a challenge we can solve if we work together.
因为这是我们齐心协力便能完成的一项挑战。

Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），并祝周末愉快。
