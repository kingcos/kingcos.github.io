Weekly Address: Meeting the Global Threat of Climate Change

每周演讲：应对全球气候变化的威胁

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President spoke about his upcoming trip to Alaska, during which he will view the effects of climate change firsthand.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统谈到了他即将到阿拉斯加州的旅行，在其间他将看到直接的气候变化结果。
Alaskans are already living with the impact of climate change, with glaciers melting faster, and temperatures projected to rise between six and twelve degrees by the end of the century.
阿拉斯加州人已经在气候变化冲突中生活，冰川融化速度越来越快，并且气温预计将在本世纪末上升六到十二摄氏度。
In his address, the President spoke to ways in which we can address these challenges, including the transition away from fossil fuels to more renewable energy sources like wind and solar, an effort in which America is already leading.
在他的演讲中，总统谈到我们能处理这些挑战的方法，包括由化石燃料过渡到更多的可再生能源，例如风能和太阳能，美国已经在努力领先。
And he stressed that while our economy still has to rely on oil and gas during that transition, we should rely more on domestic production than importing from foreign counties who do not have the same environmental or safety standards as the United States.
并且他强调，当我们的经济在过渡期仍然必须依赖石油和煤气，我们应当依赖更多国内的制造商（而）不是引入和美利坚合众国环境条件或是安全标准不同的国外（制造商）。
The President looked forward to his upcoming trip, and promised that while he is in office, America will lead the world to meet the threat of climate change before it’s too late.
总统期待他即将到来的旅行，并承诺当他在任，美国将在为时已晚前领导世界面对气候变化的威胁。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, August 29, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 8 月 29 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
August 29, 2015
2015 年 8 月 29 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
This Monday, I’m heading to Alaska for a three-day tour of the state.
本周一，我将前往阿拉斯加州旅行三天。

I’ve been looking forward to this for a long time.
我一直期待（这次旅行）已经很久了。
Not only because Alaska is one of the most beautiful places in a country that’s full of beautiful places – but because I’ll have several opportunities to meet with everyday Alaskans about what’s going on in their lives.
不仅仅是因为阿拉斯加州景色优美，是国内最美丽的地方之一－而是因为我将有机会面对日常的阿拉斯加州人，问问他们的生活怎么样。
I’ll travel throughout the state, meeting with Alaskans who live above the Arctic Circle, with Alaska natives, and with folks who earn their livelihoods through fishing and tourism.
我将环游这个周，和这些地方的阿拉斯加州人见面，阿拉斯加州生活在北极圈的本地人，和那些毕生靠渔业和旅游业为生的人们。
And I expect to learn a lot.
并且我期待能学到很多东西。

One thing I’ve learned so far is that a lot of these conversations begin with climate change.
我至今学到的一件事就是很多的这些交谈以气候变化开始。
And that’s because Alaskans are already living with its effects.
并且这是因为阿拉斯加州人已经在其影响下生活了。
More frequent and extensive wildfires.
更加频繁和大面积的山火。
Bigger storm surges as sea ice melts faster.
海上冰山融化更快激发了更大的暴风。
Some of the swiftest shoreline erosion in the world – in some places, more than three feet a year.
一些世界上最迅速的海岸线正在（遭受）侵蚀－在某些地方，每年超过了三英尺。

Alaska’s glaciers are melting faster too, threatening tourism and adding to rising seas.
阿拉斯加州的冰川也正在加速融化，威胁到了旅游业并使海平面上升。
And if we do nothing, Alaskan temperatures are projected to rise between six and twelve degrees by the end of the century, changing all sorts of industries forever.
并且如果我们什么都不做，阿拉斯加州的气温将在本世纪末上升六到十二摄氏度，永久地改变所有类型的工业。

This is all real.
这一切都是真实的。
This is happening to our fellow Americans right now.
这就是此时此刻发生在我们美国同胞身上（真实）的事。
In fact, Alaska’s governor recently told me that four villages are in “imminent danger” and have to be relocated.
事实上，阿拉斯加州的地方行政长官最近告诉我，四个村庄已经”迫在眉睫”，并且不得不迁移。
Already, rising sea levels are beginning to swallow one island community.
目前，正在上升的海平面正在吞并一个岛级社区。

Think about that.
试想一下。
If another country threatened to wipe out an American town, we’d do everything in our power to protect ourselves.
如果其他国家以消灭一座美国城镇威胁（我们），我们将尽自己所能保护我们自己。
Climate change poses the same threat, right now.
现在，气候变化带来同样的威胁。

That’s why one of the things I’ll do while I’m in Alaska is to convene other nations to meet this threat.
这就是我将做的事情之一的原因，当我在阿拉斯加州将和其他国家召开会议面对威胁。
Several Arctic nations have already committed to action.
一些北极国家已经承诺行动。
Since the United States and China worked together to set ambitious climate targets last year, leading by example, many of the world’s biggest emitters have come forward with new climate plans of their own.
自从美国和中国去年一起制定了远大的气候目标，以身作则，许多世界最大的排放国已经制定了他们自己的新气候计划。
And that’s a good sign as we approach this December’s global climate negotiations in Paris.
这是十二月我们将在巴黎（召开）的全球气候谈判的一个好预兆。

Now, one of the ways America is leading is by transitioning away from dirty energy sources that threaten our health and our environment, and by going all-in on clean, renewable energy sources like wind and solar.
现在，美国领导的方式之一便是通过过渡期避免污染的能源威胁我们的健康和环境，并继续使用清洁，可再生能源例如风能和太阳能。
And Alaska has the natural resources to be a global leader in this effort.
在这方面阿拉斯加州拥有自然资源而可以成为全球领导者。

Now even as we accelerate this transition, our economy still has to rely on oil and gas.
现在尽管加速过渡期，我们的经济仍不得不依赖石油和煤气。
As long as that’s the case, I believe we should rely more on domestic production than on foreign imports, and we should demand the highest safety standards in the industry – our own.
只要这种情况（存在），我相信我们应该更加依赖国内的制造商而不是从国外进口，并且我们应当制定工业中的最高标准－我们自己。
Still, I know there are Americans who are concerned about oil companies drilling in environmentally sensitive waters.
不过，我知道有些美国人担心石油公司在环境保护的敏感水域开采。
Some are also concerned with my administration’s decision to approve Shell’s application to drill a well off the Alaskan coast, using leases they purchased before I took office.
有些人同样担心我的政府决定批准壳牌的申请，开采阿拉斯加海岸，（通过）使用他们在我任期之前所付过的租约。
I share people’s concerns about offshore drilling.
我理解人们关于海岸开采的担忧。
I remember the BP spill in the Gulf of Mexico all too well.
我仍然清晰地记得英国石油公司在墨西哥湾的漏油事件。

That’s precisely why my administration has worked to make sure that our oil exploration conducted under these leases is done at the highest standards possible, with requirements specifically tailored to the risks of drilling off Alaska.
这恰好就是为什么我的政府努力确保我们的石油勘探行为要在最高标准的租约之下，根据特殊需求和风险程度开采阿拉斯加州。
We don’t rubber-stamp permits.
我们不会未经慎重考虑就发许可证。
We made it clear that Shell has to meet our high standards in how they conduct their operations – and it’s a testament to how rigorous we’ve applied those standards that Shell has delayed and limited its exploration off Alaska while trying to meet them.
我们明确，壳牌必须接受我们（规定）的他们如何进行操作的高标准－并且（已经）证明我们所应用的标准是如此的严格，在当我们尝试和他们面谈时，壳牌已经推迟并限制了其在阿拉斯加州的勘探。
The bottom line is, safety has been and will continue to be my administration’s top priority when it comes to oil and gas exploration off America’s precious coasts – even as we push our economy and the world to ultimately transition off of fossil fuels.
（我们的）底线是，当（我们）继续开采美国珍贵海岸的石油和煤气时，安全已经并将继续是本届政府的最高优先事务－尽管我们在推进我们的经济和世界要从根本上从化石能源过渡。

So I’m looking forward to talking with Alaskans about how we can work together to make America the global leader on climate change around the globe.
所以我在期待和阿拉斯加州人谈谈我们如何一起使美国在全球气候变化中处于领先地位。
And we’re going to offer unique and engaging ways for you to join me on this trip all week at WhiteHouse.gov/Alaska.
并且我们为你计划了唯一的参与方式，使你本周可以在 WhiteHouse.gov/Alaska 随时参与这场旅行中。
Because what’s happening in Alaska is happening to us.
因为阿拉斯加州的现状正在我们身边发生。
It’s our wakeup call.
这我们的警钟。
And as long as I’m President, America will lead the world to meet the threat of climate change before it’s too late.
只要我还在任，美国将在为时已晚前领导世界（共同）面对气候变化。

Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快。
