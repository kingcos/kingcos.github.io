Weekly Address: Celebrating Immigrant Heritage Month

每周演讲：庆祝移民文化传统月

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President recognized Immigrant Heritage Month, an occasion that allows us to celebrate our origins as a nation of immigrants.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统提到了移民文化传统月，作为一个移民之国，这是一个允许我们庆祝自己起源的庆典（之月）。
The basic idea of welcoming people to our shores is central to our ancestry and our way of life.
迎接人们到我们的海岸的这个基本概念对于我们的祖先和生活方式的核心。
That’s why the President asked everyone to visit whitehouse.gov/NewAmericans and share stories of making it to America.
这就是为什么总统请求每个人访问 whitehouse.gov/NewAmericans 并分享自己到达美国的故事。
And as we celebrate our heritage and our diversity, the President promised to continue to fight to fix our current broken immigration system and make it more just and more fair, strengthening America in the process.
并且，当我们庆祝我们的传统化和多元化时，总统许诺继续为了修订我们现行不完善的移民系统而努力，并使其更加适合更加公平，并在这个过程中使得美国更加强大。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, June 6, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 6 月 6 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
June 6, 2015
2015 年 6 月 6日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
One of the remarkable things about America is that nearly all of our families originally came from someplace else.
有关美国的非凡事件之一就是几乎所有的家庭起初都来自别的地区。
We’re a nation of immigrants.
我们（国家）是一个移民之国。
It’s a source of our strength and something we all can take pride in.
这是我们强盛的起源和所有的骄傲。
And this month – Immigrant Heritage Month – is a chance to share our American stories.
并且在本月－移民文化传统月－这是一个分享我们的美国故事的（好）机会。

I think about my grandparents in Kansas – where they met and where my mom was born.
我回想起在堪萨斯州的祖父母－在那里他们相遇，我的母亲（也）在那里诞生。
Their family tree reached back to England and Ireland and elsewhere.
他们的家谱（可以）追溯到英格兰，爱尔兰和其它地区。
They lived, and raised me, by basic values: working hard, giving back, and treating others the way you want to be treated.
他们（一起）生活，并养育了我，（教会了我）基本的价值观：努力奋斗，懂得感恩，用期望别人对待自己的方式对待他人（己所不欲，勿施于人）。

I think of growing up in Hawaii, a place enriched by people of different backgrounds – native Hawaiian, Filipino, Japanese, Chinese, Portuguese and just about everything else.
我（又）记起在夏威夷的成长之路，（那是）一个充斥着不同背景的人群的地方，本地夏威夷人，菲律宾人，日本人，中国人，葡萄牙人，和其它很多地区的人。
Growing up in that vibrant mix helped shape who I am today.
成长在那种鲜明的（不同地区的人们）混合（的环境）中，帮助我塑造了今天的自己。
And while my father was not an immigrant himself, my own life journey as an African-American – and the heritage shared by Michelle and our daughters, some of whose ancestors came here in chains – has made our family who we are.
并且虽然我的父亲他自己并不是一个移民，但作为一个非洲裔美国人自己的人生旅程，我，米歇尔，和我们的女儿分享了（移民的）传统文化，一些祖先陆续来到这里，使得我们的家庭得以建立。
This month, I’m inviting you to share your story, too.
本月，我正在邀请大家也来分享自己的故事。
Just visit whitehouse.gov/NewAmericans.
只需要访问 whitehouse.gov/NewAmericans。
We want to hear how you or your family made it to America – whether you’re an immigrant yourself or your great-great-grandparents were.
我们希望听到你或者你的家庭是如何植根于美国的－不论你自己是（第一代）移民或者你的曾曾祖父母是（第一代）移民。

Of course, we can’t just celebrate this heritage, we have to defend it – by fixing our broken immigration system.
当然，我们不能仅仅庆祝这个文化传统，我们也必须要捍卫它－通过修订我们不完善的移民系统。
Nearly two years ago, Democrats and Republicans in the Senate came together to do that.
大约两年前，参议院中的民主党和共和党携手处理。
They passed a commonsense bill to secure our border, get rid of backlogs, and give undocumented immigrants who are already living here a pathway to citizenship if they paid a fine, paid their taxes, and went to the back of the line.
他们通过了一个常识法案来稳固我们的边境，减免储备金，并且如果那些已经生活在这里却无证明文件的移民上交罚款，缴纳税款，并退居到国境线以外，便给予他们一条通往（成为美国）公民的通道。
But for nearly two years, Republican leaders in the House have refused to even allow a vote on it.
但是近两年，众议院中的共和党领导人甚至拒绝在这个法案上投票（表决）。

That’s why, in the meantime, I’m going to keep doing everything I can to make our immigration system more just and more fair.
这就是为什么，现在，我将尽一切所能使得我们的移民系统更加适合，并且更加公平。
Last fall, I took action to provide more resources for border security; focus enforcement on the real threats to our security; modernize the legal immigration system for workers, employers, and students; and bring more undocumented immigrants out of the shadows so they can get right with the law.
上个秋天，我采取了一项措施来为边境安全提供更多的资源；专注于对我们安全的真正威胁；为工人，雇主和学生将合法移民系统现代化；并且将更多的无证明文件的移民带出阴影，所以他们可以用法律获得他们的权利。
Some folks are still fighting against these actions.
（但有）一些人仍在奋力反对这些措施。
I’m going to keep fighting for them.
我将继续和他们抗争到底。
Because the law is on our side.
因为法律是站在我们这一边的。
It’s the right thing to do.
这是完全正确的行为。
And it will make America stronger.
并且这将使得美国更加强大。

I want us to remember people like Ann Dermody from Alexandria, Virginia.
我想让大家铭记来自弗吉尼亚州亚历山德里亚的 Ann Dermody 。
She’s originally from Ireland and has lived in America legally for years.
她来自爱尔兰，而且她已经在美国合法生活了四年。
She worked hard, played by the rules and dreamed of becoming a citizen.
她工作努力，遵纪守法，并且梦想成为一名（美国）公民。
In March, her dream came true.
三月，她梦想成真。
And before taking the oath, she wrote me a letter.
并且在她宣誓之前，她给我写了封信。
“The papers we receive… will not change our different accents [or] skin tones,” Ann said.
“我们收到的这张纸…将不会改变我们不同的口音或者不同的肤色，” Ann 说。
“But for that day, at least, we’ll feel like we have arrived.”
“但是那天，至少，我们感到自己（真正）到达了（这片土地）。”

Well, to Ann and immigrants like her who have come to our shores seeking a better life – yes, you have arrived.
对于 Ann 和像她一样已经到达我们（这片）海岸来寻找一个更好的生活的移民（来说）－是的，你们已经到达。
And by sharing our stories, and staying true to our heritage as a nation of immigrants, we can keep that dream alive for generations to come.
通过分享我们的故事，和作为一个移民之国对我们的文化传统保持发扬，我们可以为（新的）一代人守护这个鲜活的梦。

Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快。
