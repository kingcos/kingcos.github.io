Weekly Address: Continuing Work to Improve Community Policing

每周演讲：继续努力加强社区治安

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President spoke about the work the Administration is doing to enhance trust between communities and law enforcement in the year since the death of Michael Brown in Ferguson.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统谈到了距在弗格森市发生的迈克尔·布朗枪击案之后的一年，政府正在提高社区和执法机构之间信任的工作。
In May, the President’s Task Force on 21st Century Policing released their final report setting out concrete proposals to build trust and enhance public safety.
在五月，总统的特别工作组在 21 世纪警务报告中发布了最终报告，列出以建立信任并加强警察安全的具体计划。
And across America local leaders are working to put these ideas into action in their communities.
并通过美国各地领导将这些计划在其社区付诸行动。
The President noted that while progress is being made, these issues go beyond policing, which is why the Administration is committed to achieving broader reforms to the criminal justice system and to making new investments in our children and their future.
总统指出，虽然已有一些进展，但这些问题超出了警察（的范围），这就是为什么政府承诺去实行更加广泛的刑事司法系统改革并对我们的孩子和他们的未来投资。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, August 15, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 8 月 15 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
August 15, 2015
2015 年 8 月 15 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
It’s now been a year since the tragic death of Michael Brown in Ferguson, Missouri.
现在距发生在密苏里州弗格森市令人悲痛的迈克尔·布朗枪击案发生已经过去一年。
His death—along with the events in Cleveland, Staten Island, Baltimore, Cincinnati, and other communities—sparked protests and soul searching all across our country.
他的去世，克里夫兰市，斯坦顿岛，巴尔的摩市，和其它社区的类似事件，引发了全国范围内的抗议和反省。
Over the past year, we’ve come to see, more clearly than ever, the frustration in many communities of color and the feeling that our laws can be applied unevenly.
在过去的一年，我们已经看到，许多黑人社区的不满意和法律给人不平等的感觉比以往更加明显。

After Ferguson, I said that we had to face these issues squarely.
在弗格森市（的事件）之后，我曾说我们必须直面这些问题。
I convened a task force on community policing to find commonsense steps that can help us drive down crime and build up trust and cooperation between communities and police, who put their lives on the line every single day to help keep us safe.
我召集社区警察方面的特别工作组开会，目的是寻找常识性的步骤来帮助减少犯罪并在社区和那些每天献身于前线保卫我们安全的警察之间建立信任与合作。
And I’ve met personally with rank and file officers to hear their ideas.
并且我已经亲自接见（不同）级别和从属的警察听取他们的意见。

In May, this task force made up of police officers, activists and academics proposed 59 recommendations – everything from how we can make better use of data and technology, to how we train police officers, to how law enforcement engages with our schools.
在五月份，这个特别工作组由推荐的 59 名包括警务人员，激进分子和高校教师组成－从我们如何利用好数据和科技，到如何训练警务人员，到执法机构与我们的学校合作。
And we’ve been working with communities across America to put these ideas into action.
并且我们已经努力和遍布美国的社区一起把这些计划付诸行动。

Dozens of police departments are now sharing more data with the public, including on citations, stops and searches, and shootings involving law enforcement.
现在，许多警察局开始给警察分享更多的数据，包括在涉及执法机构的传票，站点和搜查以及射击数据。
We’ve brought together leaders from across the country to explore alternatives to incarceration.
我们已经召集国内的领先者来探索非监禁措施。
The Justice Department has begun pilot programs to help police use body cameras and collect data on the use of force.
司法部已经开始试点，以帮助警察使用随身相机并在使用武力时收集数据。
This fall, the department will award more than $160 million in grants to support law enforcement and community organizations that are working to improve policing.
今年秋天，该部门将会拨款超过 1.6 亿美元资金奖励支持执法机构以及努力加强警力的社区组织。
And all across the country – from states like Illinois and Ohio, to cities like Philadelphia, Boston, and Nashville – local leaders are working to implement the task force recommendations in a way that works for their communities.
而在全国各地－从例如伊利诺和俄亥俄这些州，到像费城，波士顿和纳什维尔这些市－当地的领导者正在努力执行特别工作组推荐（的方式）来为他们的社区服务。

So we’ve made progress.
所以我们已经卓有成效。
And we’ll keep at it.
并且我们将继续努力。
But let’s be clear: the issues raised over the past year aren’t new, and they won’t be solved by policing alone.
但是让我们明确一点：在过去的一年，上升的问题（并）不是新的，并且它们不能仅仅靠警察解决。
We simply can’t ask our police to contain and control issues that the rest of us aren’t willing to address—as a society.
作为（同）一个社会，我们不能简单地要求我们的警察阻止并控制问题，而余下的我们只是期望处理。
That starts with reforming a criminal justice system that too often is a pipeline from inadequate schools to overcrowded jails, wreaking havoc on communities and families all across the country.
这从改革刑事司法系统开始，这往往是一个渠道，从贫乏的学校，到过度拥挤的监狱，对全国的社区和家庭（进行）报复性破坏。
So we need Congress to reform our federal sentencing laws for non-violent drug offenders.
所以我们需要国会来改革我们针对非暴力吸毒贩的联邦制量刑。
We need to keep working to help more prisoners take steps to turn their lives around so they can contribute to their communities after they’ve served their time.
我们需要继续努力来帮助更多的罪犯采取一定的措施使他们弃恶从善，因此他们可以在服刑之后为他们的社区（再）做贡献。

More broadly, we need to truly invest in our children and our communities so that more young people see a better path for their lives.
更宽泛地说，我们需要真正地投入我们的孩子和我们的社区，这样更多的年轻人会看到他们一生中更好的道路。
That means investing in early childhood education, job training, pathways to college.
这意味着投资幼儿教育，工作培训，大学之路。
It means dealing honestly with issues of race, poverty, and class that leave too many communities feeling isolated and segregated from greater opportunity.
这意味着真诚的处理（诸如）民族、贫穷、以及阶级（等这些）让太多社区感到与更大机会之间孤立和分裂的问题。
It means expanding that opportunity to every American willing to work for it, no matter what zip code they were born into.
这意味着为每一个美国人扩大其愿意的工作的机会，无论他们出生于何处。

Because, in the end, that’s always been the promise of America.
因为，到头来，这始终是美国的承诺。
And that’s what I’ll keep working for every single day that I’m President.
并且这就是我身为总统每一天所努力寻求的。
Thanks everybody, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快。
