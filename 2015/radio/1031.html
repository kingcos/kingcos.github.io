Weekly Address: It’s Time To Reform our Criminal Justice System

每周演讲：是时候改革刑事司法体系了

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President spoke to the need for meaningful criminal justice reform in America.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统谈到了在美国有意义的刑事体系改革的必要性。
America faces a cycle of poverty, criminality, and incarceration that traps too many Americans and weakens too many communities.
美国面临着贫穷，（进而）犯罪，（致使）监禁的（恶性）循环，使得许多美国人沦陷，也使得许多社区衰退。
The President believes that we can disrupt the pipeline from underfunded schools to overcrowded jails, and make our criminal justice system smarter, fairer, and more effective.
总统相信，我们能够阻断从资金不足的学校到人满为患的监狱之间的通道，并让我们的刑事司法体系更加智能，更加公平，并且更加行之有效。
That’s why in recent weeks, he has been traveling the country and meeting with Americans who are working to improve the criminal justice system, from law enforcement officials working to lower the crime and incarceration rates, to former prisoners who are earning their second chance.
这就是为什么，在近几周，他已经走遍全国并和那些致力于提升刑事司法体系的人们谈话，从降低犯罪和监禁率的执法人员，到重获新生的前犯人。
And on Monday, the President will travel to Newark, New Jersey to highlight efforts to help Americans who’ve paid their debt to society rejoin their communities.
并且下周一，总统将前往新泽西州纽瓦克，强调要努力帮助那些偿还清亏欠社会的人们重新融入到他们的社区。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, October 31, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 10 月 31 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
October 31, 2015
2015 年 10 月 31 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
Today, there are 2.2 million people behind bars in America and millions more on parole or probation.
今天，在美国，有 220 万人正在监狱，更有数以百万人得到假释或是缓刑。
Every year, we spend $80 billion in taxpayer dollars to keep people incarcerated.
每一年，我们花费纳税人 800 亿在关押犯人上。
Many are non-violent offenders serving unnecessarily long sentences.
许多非暴力罪犯正服役着没有必要的长期监禁。

I believe we can disrupt the pipeline from underfunded schools to overcrowded jails.
我相信我们能够阻断从资金不足的学校到人满为患的监狱之间的通道。
I believe we can address the disparities in the application of criminal justice, from arrest rates to sentencing to incarceration.
我相信我们可以致力于刑事司法应用的悬殊差异，从逮捕率到监禁的判决。
And I believe we can help those who have served their time and earned a second chance get the support they need to become productive members of society.
并且，我相信，我们可以帮助那些服刑并重获新生（的人们）以获得支持使他们成为社会中有收获的一员。

That’s why over the course of this year, I’ve been talking to folks around the country about reforming our criminal justice system to make it smarter, fairer, and more effective.
这就是为什么一年来，我已经和遍布全国的人们谈及重新改革刑事司法体系来使它更加智能，更加公平，并且更加行之有效。

In February, I sat down in the Oval Office with police officers from the country.
二月，我在总统办公室和来自全国各地的警务人员一起探讨。
In the spring, I met with police officers and young people in Camden, New Jersey, where they’re using community policing and data to drive down crime.
春天的时候，我在新泽西州卡姆登和警务人员以及年轻人会晤，他们正在使用社区警力和数据来让犯罪率下降。
Over the summer, I visited a prison in Oklahoma to talk with inmates and corrections officers about rehabilitating prisoners, and preventing more people from ending up there in the first place.
夏天的时候，我在俄克拉何马州会见了一位犯人，谈到同狱的犯人和改造犯人并阻止更多人在那里结束的矫正人员。
Two weeks ago, I visited West Virginia to meet with families battling prescription drug and heroin abuse, as well as people who are working on new solutions for treatment and rehabilitation.
两周前，我去西弗吉尼亚州和与处方药和海洛因滥用抗争的家庭会面，同样是研究新的解决方案以对待和恢复的人们。
Last week, I traveled to Chicago to thank police chiefs from across the country for all that their officers do to protect Americans, to make sure they’ve got the resources to get the job done, and to call for commonsense gun safety reforms that would make officers and their communities safer.
上周，我去芝加哥感谢警察局长们，（因为他们属下的）警务人员遍布全国以保护我们，确保他们能够得到资源来完成工作，并呼吁普遍的持枪安全改革，这将使得警务人员和他们的社区更加安全。

And we know that having millions of people in the criminal justice system, without any ability to find a job after release, is unsustainable.
并且我们知道有数以百万的人正在服刑，（而）刑满释放后没有任何找工作的能力，这是无法维持的。
It’s bad for communities and it’s bad for our economy.
这有害于我们的社区，有害于我们经济。

So on Monday, I’ll travel to Newark, New Jersey to highlight efforts to help Americans who’ve paid their debt to society reintegrate back into their communities.
所以，在周一，我将前往新泽西州纽瓦克强调要努力帮助那些偿还清亏欠社会的人们重新融入到他们的社区。
Everyone has a role to play, from businesses that are hiring ex-offenders to philanthropies that are supporting education and training programs.
每个人都扮演着一个角色，从在企业雇佣前犯人到支持教育和训练项目的慈善。
And I’ll keep working with people in both parties to get criminal justice reform bills to my desk, including a bipartisan bill that would reduce mandatory minimums for non-violent drug offenders and reward prisoners with shorter sentences if they complete programs that make them less likely to commit a repeat offense.
并且我将继续和来自两党的人们一起努力将刑事司法体系的法案递交在我的案前，包括一个被两党接受的法案，这将减少法定非暴力毒犯的（被监禁）年限并奖励更短刑期的罪犯，如果他们完成项目被认定极低可能会二次犯罪。

There’s a reason that good people across the country are coming together to reform our criminal justice system.
遍布全国的善良的人们正一起改革刑事司法体系有一个原因。
Because it’s not about politics.
因为这不关乎政治。
It’s about whether we as a nation live up to our founding ideal of liberty and justice for all.
这是关乎我们作为一个国家是否践行每个人的自由和公平的建国理想。
And working together, we can make sure that we do.
而当我们一起努力，我们一定可以做到。

Thanks, everybody.
感谢大家（倾听）。
Have a great weekend.
祝周末愉快。
And have a safe and Happy Halloween.
并祝（大家）度过一个安全快乐的万圣节。

criminality n.犯罪
incarceration n.关押
trap n.陷阱
disrupt v.中断
underfunded adj.资金不足的
parole n.假释
probation n.缓刑
disparity n.悬殊的差异
productive adj.高产的
commonsense n.常识
reintegrate v.使成为一体，使重新融入
philanthropy n.慈善
ideal adj.理想的
