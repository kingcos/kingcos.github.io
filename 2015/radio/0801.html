Weekly Address: Celebrating Fifty Years of Medicare and Medicaid

每周演讲：庆祝《医疗保障及补助计划》实施五十周年

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President celebrated the fiftieth birthdays of Medicare and Medicaid, which together have allowed millions to live longer and better lives.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统庆祝了《医疗保障及补助计划》实施的第五十个生日，它们一起使得数百万人活得更加长寿，活得更加美满。
These programs are a promise that if we work hard, and play by the rules, we’ll be rewarded with a basic measure of dignity, security, and the freedom to live our lives as we want.
这些计划是一个承诺，如果我们努力奋斗，照规矩办事，我们将会享受到尊严、安全和自由的基础措施的奖励以活得如自己所想。
Every American deserves the sense of safety and security that comes with health insurance.
每个美国人都应当富有安全感并得到医疗保险的保障。
That’s why the President signed the Affordable Care Act, and that’s why he will continue to work to ensure that Medicare and Medicaid, programs that are fundamental to our way of life, stay strong.
这就是为什么总统签署《平价医疗法案》，这就是为什么他继续努力以保障《医疗保障及补助计划》（实施），这些计划是我们生活方式的基础，使得我们强健。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, August 1, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 8 月 1 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
August 1, 2015
2015 年 8 月 1 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
This week, there was a big birthday you might have missed.
本周，有一个伟大的生日你们可能错过了。
Medicare and Medicaid turned 50 years old.
《医疗保障及补助计划》已经实施 50 年之久。
And that’s something worth celebrating.
并且这是值得庆祝的。

If one of the best measures of a country is how it treats its more vulnerable citizens – seniors, the poor, the sick – then America has a lot to be proud of.
如果一个国家最好的措施之一是它如何对待弱势群体——老人，穷人，病人——那么美国有许多引以为傲的地方。
Think about it.
想想吧。
Before Social Security, too many seniors lived in poverty.
在《公共福利计划》实施之前，太多的老人生活在贫困之中。
Before Medicare, only half had some form of health insurance.
在《医疗保障计划》实施之前，只有一半的人有某种形式的健康保险。
Before Medicaid, parents often had no help covering the cost of care for a child with a disability.
在《医疗补助计划》实施之前，父母经常无力负担护理残疾儿童的费用。

But as Americans, we declared that our citizens deserve a basic measure of security and dignity.
但作为美国人，我们宣布，我们的公民应当享有安全和尊严的基本（保障）措施。
And today, the poverty rate for seniors is less than half of what it was fifty years ago.
并且今天，老人的贫困率已经低于五十年前时的一半。
Every American over 65 has access to affordable health care.
每个年龄大于 65 岁的美国人即可享受平价医疗保障。
And today, we’re finally finishing the job – since I signed the Affordable Care Act into law, the uninsured rate for all Americans has fallen by about one-third.
并且今天，我们最终完成了这项工作——自从我签署了（并将）《平价医疗法案》写进了法律，所有美国人的未投保率已下降了约 1/3。

These promises we made as a nation have saved millions of our own people from poverty and hardship, allowing us new freedom, new independence, and the chance to live longer, better lives.
我们作为国家所做的这些承诺已经从贫困艰难中拯救了数百万我们自己的人民，使得我们获得了新的自由，新的独立，以及活得更加长寿更加美满的机会。
That’s something to be proud of.
这是引以为傲的事情。
It’s heroic.
这是史诗般伟大的事。
These endeavors – these American endeavors – they didn’t just make us a better country.
这些努力——这些美国人的努力——它们不仅仅使得我们的国家（变得）更加美好。
They reaffirmed that we are a great country.
它们又一次证明了我们是一个伟大的国家。

And a great country keeps the promises it makes.
而一个伟大的国家会坚持它的承诺。
Today, we’re often told that Medicare and Medicaid are in crisis.
今天，我们经常被告知《医疗保障及补助计划》处在危机之中。
But that’s usually a political excuse to cut their funding, privatize them, or phase them out entirely – all of which would undermine their core guarantee.
但这通常是政治借口，（目的是）减少他们的基金，私有化他们，或者逐步完全地废除——所有这些将会侵蚀他们的核心利益。
The truth is, these programs aren’t in crisis.
（而）事实是，这些计划并没有陷入危机。
Nor have they kept us from cutting our deficits by two-thirds since I took office.
自我上任以来，它们也没有阻止使我们的赤字削减了 2/3。
What is true is that every month, another 250,000 Americans turn 65 years old, and become eligible for Medicare.
事实是，每个月，都有 25 万美国人年满 65 岁，（进而）有资格享有《医疗保障计划》。
And we all deserve a health care system that delivers efficient, high-quality care.
并且我们都应当享有高效高质的医疗体系。
So to keep these programs strong, we’ll have to make smart changes over time, just like we always have.
所以为了使的这些计划更加完善，我们将不得不随时间变化作出灵活的调整，就像往常那样。

Today, we’re actually proving that’s possible.
今天，我们正在证明这是可行的。
The Affordable Care Act has already helped secure Medicare’s funding for another 13 years.
平价医疗法案已经为下一个 13 年的医疗保险基金来源提供了支持。
The Affordable Care Act has saved more than nine million folks on Medicare 15 billion dollars on their prescription medicine.
平价医疗法案已经为医保计划中超过九百万人节省处方药开支 150 亿美元。
It has expanded Medicaid to help cover 12.8 million more Americans, and to help more seniors live independently.
它也扩大了《医疗补助计划》，使其覆盖了 1280 万美国人，并帮助更多老人独立地生活。
And we’re moving our health care system toward models that reward the quality of the care you receive, not the quantity of care you receive.
并且我们正在转变我们的医疗体系，提高获得保障的质量，而不是（仅仅提升）数量。
That means healthier Americans and a healthier federal budget.
这意味着更加健康的美国人和更加健康的联邦预算。

Today, these programs are so fundamental to our way of life that it’s easy to forget how hard people fought against them at the time.
今天，这些计划对我们的生活方式而言是如此的基本，以至于会很容易忘记当时为之斗争的人们是多么地努力。
When FDR created Social Security, critics called it socialism.
当罗斯福总统建立社会保障金，批评家们称其为社会主义。
When JFK and LBJ worked to create Medicare, the cynics said it would take away our freedom.
当肯尼迪总统和约翰逊总统为《医疗保障计划》努力时，犬儒主义者称其可能带走自由。
But ultimately, we came to see these programs for what they truly are – a promise that if we work hard, and play by the rules, we’ll be rewarded with a basic measure of dignity, security, and the freedom to live our lives as we want.
但最终，我们看到了这些计划所真正的面目——即一个承诺，如果我们努力奋斗，照规矩办事，我们将会享受到尊严、安全和自由的基础措施的奖励以活得如自己所想。

It’s a promise that previous generations made to us, and a promise that our generation has to keep.
这是老一代给我们做出的承诺，也是我们这一代所必须坚守的一个承诺。

Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快。
