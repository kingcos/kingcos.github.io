网易云音乐的电台链接～

Weekly Address: Working for Meaningful Criminal Justice Reform

每周演讲：努力（推动）有意义的刑事司法改革

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President highlighted the problems in our criminal justice system.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统强调了我们刑事司法系统中（存在）的问题。
Our country faces a vicious cycle of poverty, criminality, and incarceration that traps too many Americans and weakens too many communities.
我们的国家面临着（陷入）贫困，（致使）犯罪和（再被）监禁的恶性循环，使得太多的美国人和太多的社区受到伤害。
There are 2.2 million people behind bars in America today, compared to 500,000 just 30 years ago.
当今，美国有 220 万人在监狱，而 30 年前（这个数字）是 500,000。
This topic isn’t new – the President has talked about the unfairness of much of the criminal justice system since his time in the Senate.
这个话题并不新鲜－自从进入参议院，总统曾已多次谈论刑事司法系统的不公平（现象）。
And while we’ve taken steps to address this issue, members of both parties agree that we can do more.
尽管我们已经采取措施解决这个问题，（但是）两党的议员均同意我们可以做得更多。
Over the next few weeks, the President will travel the country and meet with Americans who are working to fix the criminal justice system, from law enforcement officials working to lower the crime and incarceration rates, to former prisoners who are earning their second chance.
在接下来的几周，总统将前往各地并和正在努力修订刑事司法系统的人们见面，（他们是）致力于降低犯罪率和监禁率并让先前的犯人重获新生的执法人员。
And he promised to continue to work with Congress to pass meaningful criminal justice reform that makes the system cost-effective, fairer, and smarter, while enhancing the ability of law enforcement to keep our communities safe.
并且（总统）他承诺继续努力（推动）国会通过有意义的刑事司法改革，使得（刑事司法）系统廉洁高效，更加公平并更加智能，并提高执法的能力保障我们社区的安全。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, October 17, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 10 月 17 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
October 17, 2015
2015 年 10 月 17 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
Thirty years ago, there were 500,000 people behind bars in America.
三十年前，美国有 500,000 人面临牢狱之灾。
Today, there are 2.2 million.
今天，（这个数字）是 220 万人。
The United States is home to 5 percent of the world’s population, but 25 percent of the world’s prisoners.
美国的人口拥有世界人口的 5% ，但世界 25% 的罪犯在美国。
Every year, we spend $80 billion to keep people locked up.
每一年，我们（需要）花费 800 亿美元关押这些人。

Now, many of the folks in prison absolutely belong there – our streets are safer thanks to the brave police officers and dedicated prosecutors who put violent criminals behind bars.
现在，许多监狱的人们完全属于那里－（因此）我们的街道更加安全，多亏了将那些暴力罪犯送入监狱的勇敢的警察和检察官们。
But over the last few decades, we’ve also locked up more non-violent offenders than ever before, for longer than ever before.
但是在过去的几十年，我们同样关押了比以往数量更多，年限更长的非暴力罪犯。
That’s the real reason our prison population is so high.
这就是我们监狱人口如此之高的真正原因。

Ever since I was a Senator, I’ve talked about how, in too many cases, our criminal justice system is a pipeline from underfunded schools to overcrowded jails.
自从我还是一个参议员（的时候），我已经谈到过在许多情况下，我们的刑事司法系统如何（成为了）投入资金不足的学校和人满为患的监狱之间的通道。
And we’ve taken steps to address it.
并且我们已经采取措施来解决它。
We invested in our schools to give at-risk young people a better shot to succeed.
在我们的学校，我们向（那些）高风险的年轻人投资以（使他们）有一个更好的机会来获得成功（而不是走向犯罪）。
I signed a bill reducing the 100 to 1 sentencing disparity between crack and powder cocaine.
我签署过一项法案，在 和可卡因粉末之间的量刑悬殊从 100 已减少至 1。
I’ve commuted the sentences of dozens of people sentenced under old drug laws we now recognize were unfair.
当我们意识到有几十个人在按照原有法律量刑是不公平的（时候），我已经减轻了量刑。
The Department of Justice has gotten “Smart on Crime”, refocusing efforts on the worst offenders, and pursuing mandatory minimum sentences less frequently.
司法部门已经开始”智止犯罪”，重新将努力（方向）聚焦于最严重的罪犯，并将量刑降到了法定的最低（刑期）。

Still, much of our criminal justice system remains unfair.
不过，我们很多的刑事司法系统仍旧不公平。
In recent years, more of our eyes have been opened to this truth.
在近几年，更多（民众）已经亲眼看到了真相。
We can’t close them anymore.
我们不能再诓骗了。
And good people, of all political persuasions, are eager to do something about it.
并且，所有政治派别、宗教信仰中善良的人们都渴望为此做些什么。

Over the next few weeks, I’ll travel the country to highlight some of the Americans who are doing their part to fix our criminal justice system.
在接下来的几周，我将前往全国各地去和正在努力修订刑事司法系统的人们交流。
I’ll visit a community battling prescription drug and heroin abuse.
我将和与处方毒药和海洛因滥用斗争的社区。
I’ll speak with leaders from law enforcement who are determined to lower the crime rate and the incarceration rate, and with police chiefs who have dedicated their careers to keeping our streets and officers safe.
我将和（那些）决心降低犯罪率和监禁率的执法领导，以及献身职业的警察局长们谈话。
I’ll meet with former prisoners who are earning their second chance.
我（也）将和先前重获新生的犯人见面。

And I’ll keep working with lawmakers from both parties who are determined to get criminal justice reform bills to my desk.
并且我将和（那些）决心将刑事司法改革法案放在我的案桌来自两党的立法者一起努力。
Earlier this month, Democrats and Republicans came together in the Senate to introduce such a bill – one that would reduce mandatory minimums for non-violent drug offenders, and reward prisoners with shorter sentences if they complete programs that make them less likely to commit a repeat offense.
本月早些时候，民主党人和共和党人一起在参议院引入了一项这么一项法案－为非暴力罪犯减少法定的最低限度量刑，并褒奖那些较少量刑并不太可能二次作案的犯人。
There’s a similar bill working its way through the House, and I’m encouraged by these kinds of bipartisan efforts.
有一项类似法案通过众议院的执行，而且我从通过两党的努力受到了鼓励。
This is progress – not liberal ideas or conservative ideas, but common-sense solutions to the challenges we face.
这是（一种）进步－不是自由的思想或是保守的思想，而我们面临的是常识性的解决方法。

From the halls of Congress to the classrooms in our schools, we pledge allegiance to one nation under God with liberty, and justice, for all.
从国会大厅到我们学校的教室，我们为所有人这个上帝之下的国家的人表示忠诚，承诺自由和正义。
Justice means that every child deserves a chance to grow up safe and secure, without the threat of violence.
司法意味着每一个孩子都值得拥有一个没有暴力威胁下安全又安心的成长机会。
Justice means that the punishment should fit the crime.
司法意味着惩罚应当和罪过适应。
And justice means allowing our fellow Americans who have made mistakes to pay their debt to society, and re-join their community as active, rehabilitated citizens.
司法也意味着允许曾犯过错的同胞向社会赔付欠款，以及作为积极的恢复的市民重新加入他们的社区。

Justice has never been easy to achieve, but it’s always been worth fighting for.
司法公平的达到从来都不简单，但是这（却）永远值得我们努力寻求。
And it’s something I’ll keep fighting for as long as I serve as your president.
并且，只要我还在任都将继续为此努力。

Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），并祝周末愉快。

incarceration n.关押
disparity n.悬殊的差异
persuasion n.说服；信仰
mandatory adj.法定的
rehabilitated adj.重新恢复的

a vicious cycle of… …的恶性循环
address the issue 解决问题
behind bars 在监狱中
safe and secure 安全又安心
