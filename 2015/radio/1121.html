Weekly Address: In the Face of Terror, We Stand as One

每周演讲：面对恐惧，我们团结一致

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the Vice President spoke to his and the President’s commitment to protecting our country from terrorists, while also providing refuge to some of the world’s most vulnerable people.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，副总统谈到了他和总统的职责是保护我们的国家免受恐怖袭击，同时为世界上最易迫害的人提供庇护。
He emphasized that he and the President consider the safety of the American people to be their first priority.
他强调了他和总统将美国人的安全看作他们的首要职责。
But slamming the door in the face of refugees fleeing precisely the type of senseless violence that occurred in Paris last week would be a betrayal of our values.
但是上周在巴黎，发生的正好打在逃亡者脸上的无谓暴力和我们价值观背驰。
The vast majority of Syrian refugees are women, children, and orphans; survivors of torture; and people desperately in need of medical help.
绝大多数的叙利亚难民是妇女，孩子，和失去家人的人们；磨难下的生存者；和极其需要医疗救助的人们。
And all refugees undergo the highest level of security checks of any category of traveler to the United States.
而所有的遇难者在进入美利坚之前都需要接受任一类旅行者的最高检查规格。
The Vice President reminded us that ISIL wants us to turn our backs on Muslims victimized by terrorism.
副总统提醒我们，ISIL 想让我们抛弃被恐怖主义迫害的穆斯林。
We win by prioritizing our security while refusing to compromise our fundamental American values of freedom, openness, and tolerance.
把安全放在首位，拒绝妥协我们的基本价值观，自由，开放，和宽容，我们因此而胜利。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, November 21, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 11 月 21 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
November 21, 2015
2015 年 11 月 21 日


Text：
正文：

Good morning everyone.
大家早上好。
This past week we’ve seen the best and the worst of humanity.
过去的一周我们见识到了最善和最恶的人性。
The heinous terrorist attacks in Paris and Beirut, in Iraq and Nigeria.
十恶不赦的恐怖分子袭击了巴黎和贝鲁特，伊拉克和尼日利亚。
They showed us once again the depths of the terrorist’s depravity.
这些（事件）向我们再一次证明恐怖分子的罪恶之深。

And at the same time we saw the world come together in solidarity.
而同时，我们看到了世界走向了团结。
Parisians opening their doors to anyone trapped in the street, taxi drivers turning off their meters to get people home safety, people lining up to donate blood.
巴黎人为困在街道上的人们敞开家门，出租车司机为让人们安全到家取消计程，人们排队献血。
These simple human acts are a powerful reminder that we cannot be broken and in the face of terror we stand as one.
这些简单的举动（却）是一个强有力的提醒，我们不能被分歧而独自面对恐惧。
In the wake of these terrible events, I understand the anxiety that many Americans feel.
在接踵而至的恐怖事件，我理解了许多美国人所担心的。
I really do.
我真切地感受到了。
I don’t dismiss the fear of a terrorist bomb going off.
我不会忘记恐怖分子炸弹爆炸时（所带来）的恐惧。
There’s nothing President Obama and I take more seriously though, than keeping the American people safe.
没有什么比保证美国人民安全更让我和奥巴马总统担心。

In the past few weeks though, we’ve heard an awful lot of people suggest that the best way to keep America safe is to prevent any Syrian refugee from gaining asylum in the United States.
在过去的几周，我们已经听说很多人建议阻止任何叙利亚难民在美国获得避难以保证美国安全。

So let’s set the record straight how it works for a refugee to get asylum.
所以让我们澄清让一位难民获得庇护是如何开展的。
Refugees face the most rigorous screening of anyone who comes to the United States.
任何一位前往美国的难民面临着最严格的筛查。
First they are finger printed, then they undergo a thorough background check, then they are interviewed by the Department of Homeland Security.
首先，他们要录取指纹，然后接受彻底的背景调查，接着进行国土安全部的面试。
And after that the FBI, the National Counterterrorism Center, the Department of Defense and the Department of State, they all have to sign off on access.
再之后，FBI，国家反恐中心，国防部，和国务院，全部需要他们在进入时签字作证。

And to address the specific terrorism concerns we are talking about now, we’ve instituted another layer of checks just for Syrian refugees.
而为致力于现在我们所正担心的特定的恐怖主义，我们已经开始针对叙利亚难民实行另外的一层检查。
There is no possibility of being overwhelmed by a flood of refugees landing on our doorstep tomorrow.
未来将会有一大波难民涌进国门是不可能的。
Right now, refugees wait 18 to 24 months while the screening process is completed.
现在，难民（需要）等待 18 到 24 个月直到检查进程完成。
And unlike in Europe, refugees don’t set foot in the United States until they are thoroughly vetted.
而不像在欧洲，难民在美国经过严格的检查才能落脚。

Let’s also remember who the vast majority of these refugees are: women, children, orphans, survivors of torture, people desperately in need medical help.
让我们同样铭记绝大多数的叙利亚难民是妇女，孩子，和失去家人的人们；磨难下的生存者；和极其需要医疗救助的人们。

To turn them away and say there is no way you can ever get here would play right into the terrorists’ hands.
（而）把他们赶走，并让他们无法来这里（避难）将正中恐怖分子下怀。
We know what ISIL – we know what they hope to accomplish.
我们知道 ISIL－我们知道他们所妄想的目的。
They flat-out told us.
他们尽一切可能告诉我们。

Earlier this year, the top ISIL leader al-Baghdadi revealed the true goal of their attacks.
今年早些时候，ISIL 最高头目巴格达迪披露了他们袭击的真正目的。
Here’s what he said: “Compel the crusaders to actively destroy the gray zone themselves.
他说道：”迫使（这场）运动（的）参与者激烈地自己销毁在灰色地带。
Muslims in the West will quickly find themselves between one and two choices.
身在西方的穆斯林将很快发现他们自己面临一和二的选择。
Either apostatize or emigrate to the Islamic State and thereby escape persecution.”
弃教或是向伊斯兰国移民而因此逃离迫害。
So it’s clear.
所以这很清楚。
It’s clear what ISIL wants.
（我们）很清楚 ISIL 的目的。
They want to manufacture a clash between civilizations.
他们想在（不同的）文明之间制造冲突。
They want frightened people to think in terms of “us versus them”.
他们想要恐吓人民相信”我们对他们”。

They want us to turn our backs on Muslims victimized by terrorism.
他们想让我们抛弃被恐怖主义迫害的穆斯林。
But this gang of thugs peddling a warped ideology, they will never prevail.
但是这帮恶徒散播歪曲的意识观念，他们将永不得胜利。
The world is united in our resolve to end their evil.
世界团结起来决心消灭邪恶。
And the only thing ISIL can do is spread terror in hopes that we will in turn, turn on ourselves.
ISIL 仅仅传播恐惧，而反过来我们才是自己的希望。
We will betray our ideals and take actions, actions motivated by fear that will drive more recruits into the arms of ISIL.
如果我们将背叛理想，采取恐惧所激化的行动，将驱使更多的人加入 ISIL 当中。
That’s how they win.
这是他们的胜利。
We win by prioritizing our security as we’ve been doing.
而当我们把安全放在首位时，才是我们的胜利。
Refusing to compromise our fundamental American values: freedom, openness, tolerance.
拒绝违背我们基本的美国价值观：自由，开放，宽容。
That’s who we are.
这就是我们。
That’s how we win.
这就是我们的胜利。

May God continue to bless the United States of America and God bless our troops.
愿上帝能继续保佑美利坚，并保佑我们的军队。
