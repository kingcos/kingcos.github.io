Weekly Address: Giving Veterans their Chance

每周演讲：给予退伍军人机会

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President Obama honored our nation’s veterans, who have served and sacrificed in defense of our country.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，奥巴马总统向我国那些献出生命保卫祖国的退伍军人致敬。
This past week, Americans came together on Veterans Day to pay tribute to those men and women in uniform who have risked their lives to protect our freedom.
在过去的一周，我们一起在退伍军人纪念日向那些曾冒着生命危险守护我们自由的军人们致敬。
In recent years we’ve worked to reduce the veterans’ unemployment rate to 3.9 percent and slash the disability claims backlog at the VA by nearly 90 percent from its peak.
近些年来，我们已经努力降低退伍军人失业率到 3.9%，并把退伍军人事务所的积压索赔（案件）从（原本的）峰值解决了约 90%。
But there is still more that can be done.
但路还有很长要走。
Since day one of his Administration, the President has remained committed to serving the brave men and women who have served us.
自从他当政起，总统已承诺要服务于那些曾为我们服役的勇敢的人们。
And in his address, he reminded us that we all have a role to play in ensuring that veterans have the opportunities and support they deserve.
在他的演讲中，他提醒我们每个人都要保证退伍军人（能得到他们）应得的机会和支持一份子。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, November 14, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 11 月 14 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
November 14, 2015
2015 年 11 月 14 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
This week, America came together to salute our veterans – to express our appreciation to all who served so that we might live free.
本周，我们一同向我们的退伍军人致敬－向那些曾从军为自由的（人们）表达我们的敬意。
But our gratitude should extend beyond what our veterans have done for us in the past.
但是我们感激之情应当扩展到不仅仅是退伍军人为我们过去所做的。
It should remind us of our responsibility to serve them as well as they have served us.
这提醒了我们的责任是服务他们就如同他们曾为我们服役。
It should compel us to keep our veterans central to the ongoing work of this nation.
这应是规定我们要保证退伍军人是我国中心工作的重中之重。

In recent years, we’ve made historic investments to boost the VA budget, expand veterans’ benefits, and improve care for our wounded warriors.
近些年来，我们已经做了历史性的投资以激励退伍军人事务所预算，让退伍军人受益更多，改善受伤战士的保障。
We’ve now slashed the disability claims backlog by nearly 90 percent from its peak.
现在我们已经把退伍军人事务所的积压索赔（案件）从峰值解决了约 90%。
We’re reducing the outrage of veterans’ homelessness and we’ve helped tens of thousands of veterans get off the streets.
我们正在减少无家可归的退伍军人的愤怒，而且我们已解决了数以万计的退伍军人露宿街头。
The veterans’ unemployment rate is down to 3.9 percent – even lower than the national average.
退伍退伍军人的失业率下降到 3.9%－甚至低于全国平均水平。

Of course, we’re not satisfied.
当然，我们还不满意。
We’ve still got more work to do – and I’ve directed my Administration to keep doing everything it can to fulfill our promise to our veterans.
但我们的路还有很长要走－而我已指示我的政府继续做一切实现我们对退伍军人承诺的事。
But this isn’t just a job for government alone.
但这不只是政府的工作。
We all have a role to play.
我们人皆有份。
Less than one percent of Americans are serving in uniform.
少于 1% 的美国人正在服役。
So it’s true most Americans don’t always see and appreciate the incredible skills and assets that our veterans can offer.
所以大多数美国人并不总是看到意识到我们的退伍军人难以置信的技能和优势。
But every American should know that our veterans are some of the most talented, capable people in the world.
但是每一位美国人应当知道我们的退伍军人是世界上最有天分，最有能力的一部分人。
They’ve mastered skills and technologies and leadership roles that are impossible to teach off the battlefield.
他们还掌握了技术和科技，以及领导职能，这些战场外不可能教授（的知识）。
They know how to get stuff done.
他们知道如何完成使命。

And as our veterans will tell you themselves, they’re not finished serving their country.
而我们的退伍军人将向我们证明，他们还远未结束为祖国效劳。
They’re teachers and doctors, engineers and entrepreneurs, social workers and community leaders.
他们是老师，医生，工程师，企业家，社会工作者和社区领导者。
They serve in statehouses across the country and in Congress.
他们在遍及全国的州政府和国会效劳。
As I tell small business owners and CEOs on a regular basis, if you want to get the job done, hire a vet.
定期我会告诉小企业主和 CEO 们，如果你想完成工作，雇一位退伍军人。
Every sector, every industry, every community in this country can benefit from the incredible talents of our veterans.
这个国家的每一个行业，每一个企业，每一个社区可以从我们退伍军人难以置信的天分中受益。

Our troops and veterans give us their very best.
我们的部队和退伍军人将他们最好的献给我们。
That’s what a soldier named Captain Florent Groberg proved.
这就是陆军上尉 Florent Groberg 所证明的。
Three years ago, on patrol in Afghanistan, Flo saw a suicide bomber coming toward his unit.
三年前，在阿富汗的巡逻时，Flo 看到了一个自杀式袭击者正在前往他的小队。
Without hesitating, Flo grabbed him by his vest and helped push him to the ground.
毫无犹豫，Flo 用马甲抓住他并将他制服在地。
When the bomb went off, Flo was badly injured, and four of his comrades were killed.
当炸弹引爆，Flo 受到了重伤，而他的四个战友被杀害。
But many more were saved because of Flo’s sacrifice.
但是更多的人因 Flo 的勇敢而得救。
Flo represents the very best of America – and this week, I was proud to present him with the Medal of Honor for his actions.
Flo 代表着美国的卓越者－而这周，因他的所作所为，我很荣幸的授予他荣誉勋章。

Veterans like Flo, they deserve our undying gratitude.
像 Flo 这样的退伍军人，他们永远值得我们感激。
They deserve the chance to keep serving the country they risked everything to defend.
他们理应有机会继续服务于他们冒一切风险所守卫的祖国。
And so we must come together to keep giving them that chance, not just on Veterans Day, but on every single day of the year.
而因此我们必须一同给予他们这个机会，而不只是在退伍军人纪念日，而是一年中的每一日。
May God bless all those who serve and all who have given their lives for our country.
愿上帝保佑所有为这个国家献身的人们。
And may God bless the United States of America.
也希望天佑美利坚。
