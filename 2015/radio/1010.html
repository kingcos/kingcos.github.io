Weekly Address: Congress Should Do its Job and Pass a Serious Budget

每周演讲：为全球经济定下规则

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President spoke to the merits of the high-standards trade agreement reached this past week.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统谈到了上周达成的高标准贸易协定的价值。
The Trans-Pacific Partnership helps level the playing field for American workers and businesses, so we can export more Made-in-America products all over the world, supporting higher-paying American jobs here at home.
跨太平洋合作伙伴关系（即 TPP 协议）协助了美国工人和企业提供了公平的竞争环境，所以我们能向全世界输出更多的 Made in America 的产品，在国内为美国人提供了薪资更高的职位。
The President acknowledged that past trade agreements have not always lived up to expectations, but emphasized that this is a good deal, with the strongest commitments on labor and environment of any trade agreement in history.
总统承认过去的贸易协定并不总是无愧于（民众的）期望，但强调（TPP 协议）是一个很好的协定，是历史上对劳工和环境（保护）的贸易协议中的最强承诺。
It reflects America’s values and gives our workers the fair shot at success they deserve.
这反映了美国的价值观，并为我们的工人提供他们应当获得成功的公平机会。
The President encouraged everyone to read the agreement, which will be available online well before he signs it, and looked forward to working with lawmakers from both parties as they consider and approve this deal.
总统鼓励每一个人阅读这项协定，在其被签署之前将一直在线上存在，并期待着来自两党的立法者考虑并批准这项协定。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, October 10, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 10 月 10 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
October 10, 2015
2015 年 10 月 10 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
This week, after five years of effort with eleven other nations, we reached agreement on a new trade deal that promotes American values and protects American workers.
本周，在和其他 11 个国家五年努力之后，我们在新的贸易协定上达成了一致，这促进了美国人的价值观并保护了美国工人。

There’s a reason this Trans-Pacific Partnership took five years to negotiate.
TPP 协议历时五年时间谈判（达成）有一个原因。
I wanted to get the best possible deal for American workers.
我想要为美国工人争取到最好的贸易可能。
And that is what we’ve done.
这就是我们所做到的。
Here’s why it matters.
这就是为什么它如此重要。

Ninety-five percent of the world’s consumers live outside our borders – 95 percent.
全世界 95% 的消费者生活在我们的国度之外——95%。
They want to buy American products.
他们想要美国的产品。
They want our cars; our music; our food.
他们想要我们的汽车；我们的音乐；我们的食物。
And if American businesses can sell more of their products in those markets, they can expand and support good jobs here at home.
并且如果美国企业可以在这些市场销售他们更多的产品，他们可以在国内扩大并提供好的工作岗位。

So it’s no wonder that exports played a huge role in helping America recover from the Great Recession.
因此也难怪，出口在美国从大萧条恢复时扮演了及其重要角色。
In fact, last year, we set a new record for American exports for the fifth year in a row, selling more than $2 trillion in goods and services.
实际上，去年，我们的出口（额）已连续增长四年，在商品和服务方面销售了超过 2 万亿美元。
Our exports support roughly 12 million American jobs – and they’re jobs that typically pay better than other jobs.
我们的出口业提供了大约 1200 万美国人的岗位——并且这些岗位比以往其它岗位有更高的薪酬。

But here’s the thing: Outdated trade rules put our workers at a disadvantage.
但是还有一个问题：过时的贸易规则使我们的工人处于劣势地位。
And TPP will change that.
而 TPP 协议将改变它。

Right now, other countries can cut their costs by setting lower standards to pay lower wages.
现在，其他国家可以通过更低的标准、减少薪资来减少开支。
This trade agreement, TPP, will change that, holding partner countries to higher standards and raising wages across a region that makes up nearly 40 percent of the global economy.
这项贸易协定，TPP 协议，将改变这些，坚持合作国家通过占据 40% 全球经济的地带提高标准并提高薪资。

Right now, other countries charge foreign taxes on goods that are made in America.
现在，其他国家在美国制造的商品上收取外国税（译者注：进口税？）。
Japan, for example, puts a 38 percent tax on American beef before it even reaches the market.
举个例子，日本在美国牛肉到达市场之前要支付 38% 的税。
Malaysia puts a 30 percent tax on American auto parts.
马来西亚要为美国汽车零部件支付 30% 的税。
Vietnam puts taxes as high as 70 percent on every car American automakers sell there.
越南要为每一辆售出的美国汽车支付高达 70% 的税。
Those taxes and other trade barriers put our workers at a disadvantage.
这些税收和其它贸易障碍时的我们的工人处于不利地位。
It makes it more expensive to make goods here and sell them over there.
这使得在这里制造并在那里销售的商品变得更加昂贵。
Well, TPP is going to change that.
那么，TPP 协议正计划改变这些。
It eliminates more than 18,000 of these taxes on American goods and services.
它废除了美国商品和服务的超过 18,000 项税收。
And that way, we’re boosting America’s farmers, ranchers, manufacturers, and small business owners – make it easier for them to sell their products abroad.
而且这么一来，我们正鼓励美国的农场主，牧场主，生产者，以及个体经济所有者——使得他们可以更方便的将他们的产品销往国外。

That’s what it means to level the playing field for American workers and businesses.
这意味着为美国工人和企业平衡竞争环境。
And when the playing field is level, and the rules are fair, Americans can out-compete anybody in the world.
并且，当竞争环境平衡，规则公平，美国人可以胜过世界上任何人。

Now, I’m the first person who will say that past trade agreements haven’t always lived up to their promise.
现在，我将是承认过去的贸易协议并不总是违背其承诺的第一人。
Sometimes they’ve been tilted too much in the direction of other countries and we haven’t gotten a fair deal.
有时，那些协议向其它国家倾斜太多而我们没有受到公平的对待。
And that makes folks suspicious of any new trade initiatives.
并且那使得市民怀疑任何新的贸易举措。
But let’s be clear.
但是，我们要清楚。
Our future depends not on what past trade deals did wrong, but on doing new trade deals right.
我们的未来并不依赖于过去错误的贸易协定，而是依赖正确的新贸易协定。
And that’s what the TPP does.
这就是 TPP 协议所（能）做的。

It includes the strongest labor standards in history, from requiring fair hours to prohibiting child labor and forced labor.
它包含史上最强大的的劳工标准，从公平的工作时间到禁用童工，到强迫劳动。
It includes the strongest environmental standards in history.
它包含了史上最健全的环境标准。
All these things level the playing field for us, because if they have to follow these rules, then they can’t undercut us and sell their products cheaper because they’re violating these rules.
所有的这些都是为我们平衡竞争环境，因为如果他们不得不遵守这些法规，那么他们不会剥削我们，也不会更便宜的售卖他们的产品，因为他们正在违反这些法则。
And unlike past trade agreements, these standards are actually enforceable.
并且不像过去的贸易协定，实际上这些标准是强制执行的。

Without this agreement, competitors that don’t share our values, like China, will write the rules of the global economy.
没有这项协定，竞争者们将不会认同我们的价值观，例如中国，将书写全球经济的规则。
They’ll keep selling into our markets and try to lure companies over there; meanwhile they’re going to keep their markets closed to us.
他们将继续向我们的市场销售，并试图在那里吸引公司；同时，他们正计划保持他们的市场不面向我们。
That’s what’s been going on for the last 20 years.
这就是近 20 年所发生的。
That’s what’s contributed so much to outsourcing.
这就促使了如此多的业务外包。
That’s what has made it easier for them to compete against us.
这就使得他们更加易于和我们竞争。
And it needs to change.
而这需要改变。

With this Trans-Pacific Partnership, we are writing the rules for the global economy.
有了 TPP 协议，我们正为全球经济书写规则。
America is leading in the 21st century.
美国正在 21 世纪（处于）领导（地位）。
Our workers will be the ones who get ahead.
我们的工人将是出人头地的。
Our businesses will get a fair deal.
我们的企业将是公平的。
And those who oppose passing this new trade deal are really just accepting a status quo that everyone knows puts us at a disadvantage.
而反对通过这项新的贸易协定的人真的只是接受现状，每个人都知道我们正处于不利地位。

Look, you don’t have to take my word for it.
等着，你不需要（选择）相信我的话。
In the coming weeks and months, you’ll be able to read every word of this agreement online well before I sign it.
在接下来的几周和几月，在其被我签署之前你可以在线上阅读这项协定的每一个单词。
You’ll be able to see for yourself how this agreement is better than past trade deals – and how it’s better for America’s working families.
你将会看到这项协定是如何比以往的贸易协定都要更好——以及它是如何对于美国工薪家庭都要更好。
You can learn more at WhiteHouse.gov.
你可以在 WhiteHouse.gov 浏览更多。
And I look forward to working with both parties in Congress to approve this deal – and grow our economy for decades to come.
而我会期望在国会来的两党将批准这项协定——并在（即将）到来的十年中发展我们的经济。

Thanks, everybody.
感谢大家（倾听）.
And have a great weekend.
并祝周末愉快。
