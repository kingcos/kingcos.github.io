Weekly Address: The Affordable Care Act is Here to Stay

每周演讲：《平价医疗法案》将植根于此

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President called the Supreme Court’s decision on the Affordable Care Act a victory for hardworking Americans across the country, whose lives are more secure because of this law.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统谈到，最高法院关于《平价医疗法案》的裁决是全国辛勤工作的美国人的胜利，他们的生活将因为这个法案而更加有保障。
The Affordable Care Act is working, and it is here to stay.
《平价医疗法案》已经生效，并扎根下来。
So far more than 16 million uninsured Americans have gained coverage.
现在，超过 1600 万没有保险的美国人将得到（医保的）全面覆盖。
Nearly one in three Americans who was uninsured a few years ago is insured today.
近三分之一的美国人没有保险，而在多年之后的今天将拥有保险。
The uninsured rate in America is the lowest since we began to keep such records.
在美国，从我们开始执行这项纪录起未投保率将降到最低。
With this case behind us, the President reaffirmed his commitment to getting more people covered and making health care in America even better and more affordable.
为了我们身后更好的未来，总统重申他的承诺是让更多的人被（保险）覆盖并让美国的医疗保障更加健全和惠民。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, June 27, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 6 月 27 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
June 27, 2015
2015 年 6 月 27 日


Text：
正文：

Five years ago, we finally declared that in America, health care is not a privilege for a few, but a right for all.
五年之前，我们终于宣布，在美国医疗保险并不是少数人的特权，而是每个人的权利。
And this week, after more than fifty votes in Congress to repeal or weaken this law; after a Presidential election based in part on preserving or repealing this law; after multiple challenges to this law before the Supreme Court, we can now say this for certain: the Affordable Care Act still stands, it is working, and it is here to stay.
而在本周，国会上有超过五十票试图废除或削弱这个法案；在总统选举中有部分假借或是保留或是废除这个法案为基（来造势）；在最高法院裁决之前遇到的重重挑战；但现在我们（终于）可以确定的说：《平价医疗法案》仍将矗立，它已经生效，并将植根于此。

On Thursday, when the Court upheld a critical part of the Affordable Care Act, it was a victory for hardworking Americans all across this country whose lives are more secure because of this law.
周四，当法院（宣布）维持《平价医疗法案》的关键部分时，这是属于全国上下辛勤工作的美国人的胜利，他们的生活将因为这个法案而更加有保障。
This law means that if you’re a parent, you can keep your kids on your plan until they turn 26.
这个法案意味着，如果你是父母，你可以一直为你的孩子计划（医保）到他们 26 周岁。
If you’re a senior, or an American with a disability, this law gives you discounts on your prescriptions.
如果你是长者，或是残疾人，这个法案将使你享有处方折扣。
You can’t be charged more just because you’re a woman.
你不会因为是女性而被多收取（医疗）费用。
And you can’t be discriminated against just for having a pre-existing condition.
并且你也不会由于之前患病而遭受歧视。

This law is working exactly as it’s supposed to – and in some ways, better than we expected it to.
这个法案将会按照本该的样子去执行－甚至在一些情况下，它将执行得比我们预计的更好。
So far more than 16 million uninsured Americans have gained coverage.
现在，超过 1600 万没有保险的美国人将得到（医保的）全面覆盖。
Nearly one in three Americans who was uninsured a few years ago is insured today.
近三分之一的美国人没有保险，而在多年之后的今天将拥有保险。
The uninsured rate in America is the lowest since we began to keep such records.
在美国，从我们开始执行这项纪录起未投保率将降到最低。

The law has helped hold the price of health care to its slowest growth in 50 years.
50 年中，法律已经协助将医保费保持在最低增率。
If your family gets insurance through the workplace, not through the Affordable Care Act, you’re paying about $1,800 less per year on average than you would be if trends before this law had continued – which is good for workers and it’s good for the economy.
如果你的家人通过工作获得保险，而不通过《平价医疗法案》，在这个法案通过之前，平均每年你需要付大约 1800 美元－这是工人的好事，更是经济的大好事（借鉴 Apple 官方中文文案）。

The point is, this is not some abstract political debate.
一切的关键是，这并不是一些抽象的政治辩论。
For all the misinformation campaigns, and doomsday predictions; for all the talk of death panels and job destruction; for all the repeal attempts – this law is helping tens of millions of Americans.
为了所有的误报行动和末日预言；为了所有的死刑和岗位损失的言论；为了所有的废除企图－这个法案正在协助成千上万的美国人。
This isn’t just about Obamacare.
这不仅仅是奥巴马医改。
This is health care in America.
这是全美的医疗保障。

With this case behind us, we’re going to keep working to make health care in America even better and more affordable, and to get more people covered.
为了我们身后更好的未来，我们计划继续健全美国的医疗保障并使其更加惠民，而且将覆盖更多的人群。
But it is time to stop refighting battles that have been settled again and again.
但是，是时候停下一次次已经确立的争战。
It’s time to move on.
是时候继续前进了。

Because as Americans, we don’t go backwards, we move forwards.
因为我们是美国人，我们不会倒退，我们勇往直前。
We take care of each other.
我们互帮互助。
We root for one another’s success.
我们为他人的胜利喝彩。
We strive to do better, to be better, than the generation before us, and we try to build something better for the generation coming behind us.
我们力争比上一代人做到更好，变得更好，并且我们尝试为了下一代建立更好的（基础）。
With this behind us, let’s come together and keep building something better right now.
为了我们身后更好的未来，让我们团结起来，现在开始，向更好进发。

Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快。

supreme adj.至上的；卓越的
court n.法庭
reaffirm v.重申
repeal v.废除
election n.选举
