Weekly Address: Making Our Communities Stronger through Fair Housing

每周演讲：通过《公平住房法》保障我们的社区

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President discussed a new rule announced by his Administration earlier this week to make it easier for communities to implement the Fair Housing Act.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统提及了在本周早些时候由政府宣布的一项新规，这使社区实现《公平住房法》变得更容易。
For nearly 50 years the Fair Housing Act has prohibited landlords from turning away tenants because of race, religion, sex, national origin, or disability, and has made a big difference in this country.
在近 50 年中，在国内《公平住房法》已经禁止房东因种族，宗教，性别，国籍或残疾方面（拒绝他们租住），并且现在已经大有改善。
This week the Administration announced new steps to provide communities with the tools they need to ensure that housing is fair, and that no American’s destiny is determined by a zipcode.
本周，政府宣布为社区提供其需要的措施来确保住房是公平的，因为美国人的命运并不是由邮政编码决定的。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, July 11, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 7 月 11 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
July 11, 2015
2015 年 7 月 11 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
It’s our job as citizens to make sure we keep pushing this country we love toward our most cherished ideals – that all of us are created equal, and all of us deserve an equal shot.
作为公民，我们的义务是要一直推动这个我们所热爱的国家向最美好的理想进发－我们每个人生来平等，并且享有公平的机会。

This week, my Administration took new steps to bring us closer to that goal.
本周，政府采取了新的举措使得我们更加靠近这一目标。

Almost 50 years ago, Republicans and Democrats in Congress came together to pass the Fair Housing Act.
大约 50 年前，共和党和民主党在国会达成一致通过《公平住房法》。
It’s a law that says landlords can’t turn away tenants solely because of their race, religion, sex, national origin, or disability.
这是一部禁止房东完全只是因为种族，宗教，性别，国籍或残疾原因拒绝租户居住。
And it made a difference in this country.
并且现在已经大有改善。

Still, the work of the Fair Housing Act remains unfinished.
尽管如此，《公平住房法》的职责仍未完成。
Just a few weeks ago, the Supreme Court ruled that policies segregating minorities in poor neighborhoods, even unintentionally, are against the law.
仅仅几周前，最高法院裁定的在少数（一些）贫困区的政策和这个法律存在无意性地冲突。
The Court recognized what many people know to be true from their own lives: that too often, where people live determines what opportunities they have in life.
法院认可了许多人从生活得出的（道理）是正确的：大多数时候，一个人出生在哪里便决定了他所享有的机会。

In some cities, kids living just blocks apart lead incredibly different lives.
在一些城市，孩子们仅仅因为生活紧邻的街区而过着极其不同的生活。
They go to different schools, play in different parks, shop in different stores, and walk down different streets.
他们在不同的学校上学，在不同的公园玩耍，在不同的商店购物，并走在不同的道路上。
And often, the quality of those schools and the safety of those parks and streets are far from equal – which means those kids aren’t getting an equal shot in life.
并且通常，这些学校的质量，公园的安全性和与道路的距离是相近的－这意味着这些孩子们在生活中并不能享有平等的机会。

That runs against the values we hold dear as Americans.
这些违背了我们作为美国人所坚守珍视的价值观。
In this country, of all countries, a person’s zip code shouldn’t decide their destiny.
在这个国家，的所有地方，一个美国人的命运并不是由邮政编码决定的。
We don’t guarantee equal outcomes, but we do strive to guarantee an equal shot at opportunity – in every neighborhood, for every American.
我们不保证相同的结果，但我们努力保证平等的机会－对每一个社区，的每一个美国人。

Now, the Fair Housing Act also says that this isn’t the responsibility of a landlord alone – local governments have a role to play, too.
现在，《公平住房法》同样宣布，这不仅仅是房东的责任，地方政府也可以发挥作用。
That’s why, this week, my Administration announced that we’ll make it easier for communities to implement this law.
这就是为什么，本周政府宣布，我们将使社区实现《公平住房法》变得更容易。
We’re using data on housing and neighborhood conditions to help cities identify the areas that need the most help.
我们正在使用住房和社区条件的数据来帮助城市判断最需要帮助的地区。
We’re doing more to help communities meet their own goals.
我们正在做更多来帮助社区达成他们自己的目标。
Plus, by opening this data to everybody, everyone in a community – not just elected officials – can weigh in.
此外，通过向每个人开放这个数据，社区中的每一个分子，不仅仅是被民选官员都可以参与进来。
If you want a bus stop added near your home, or more affordable housing nearby, now you’ll have the data you need to make your case.
如果你想在你的家附近设一个公交站，或是想在附近租住更实惠的房子，现在你讲可以使用适合你的情况的你所需要的数据。

These actions won’t make every community perfect.
这些举措不会让每个社区完美。
That’s something we all have to strive for in our own lives.
这需要我们在各自所生活的地方共同争取。
But they will help make our communities stronger and more vibrant.
但它们将会使我们的的社区更加强大，更加充满活力。
And they’ll help keep this a country where kids from every background can grow up knowing that no matter who you are, what you look like, or where you live, you can write your own story.
并且它们将在全国范围内传播，在这里来自不同背景的孩子们渐渐长大将会懂得：无论你是谁，无论你的外貌，无论你的出处，你都可以书写属于你自己的篇章。

That’s the America I love.
这就是我所热爱的美国。
And it’s the America I’ll keep fighting for.
这就是我将为之不停奋斗的美国。
Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快。

implement n.工具；v.实施
prohibit v.禁止，阻止
landlord n.房东
tenant n.租户
solely adv.完全地；单独地
unintentionally adv.无意地
incredibly adv.极其

national origin 国籍
weigh in 加入比赛；参与进去
