Weekly Address: Pass the USA Freedom Act
每周演讲：通过《美国自由法案》

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President addressed critical pieces of national security business that remained unfinished when the Senate left town.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统宣布了国家安全事务的关键部分在参议院休整时仍未完成。
This Sunday at midnight, key tools used to protect against terrorist threats are set to expire.
本周日午夜，过去反恐的关键法案将失效。
The USA Freedom Act strikes a balance between security and privacy, reauthorizing important measures that give our national security professionals the authorities they use to keep us safe, while also implementing reforms that enhance the privacy and civil liberties of our citizens.
《美国自由法案》将力争保证安全与隐私的平衡，把重要条款的重新授权职责交给曾保护我们的国家安全机关，同时实施改革，以加大保障公民隐私权和公民自由。
But currently, a small group of senators is standing in its way.
但是现在，一小部分参议员却站在自己的立场。
The President asked Americans to speak with one voice to the Senate to put politics aside, put the safety of the American people first, and pass the USA Freedom Act now.
总统希望美国人一致声援参议院，将政治放在一边，把美国人民的安全放在首位，立即通过《美国自由法案》。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, May 30, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 5 月 30 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
May 30, 2015
2015 年 5 月 30 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
As President and Commander in Chief, my greatest responsibility is the safety of the American people.
身为总统和总司令，我最大的责任即是保障美国人民的安全。
And in our fight against terrorists, we need to use every effective tool at our disposal – both to defend our security and to protect the freedoms and civil liberties enshrined in our Constitution.
在打击恐怖分子的战斗中，我们需要使用每一个可供使用的有效手段——既要保障我们的安全，又要捍卫宪法赋予（我们的）独立和自由。

But tomorrow – Sunday, at midnight – some important tools we use against terrorists will expire.
但是明天——周日午夜——（过去）反恐的一些关键法案将失效。
That’s because Congress has not renewed them, and because legislation that would – the USA Freedom Act – is stuck in the Senate.
这是因为国会还未更新他们，（而且）《美国自由法案》（的通过）在参议院受阻。
I want to be very clear about what this means.
我想这其中的意味十分清楚。

Today, when investigating terrorist networks, our national security professionals can seek a court order to obtain certain business records.
现在，当我们调查恐怖组织时，我们的国家安全机关可以寻求法院来获得某些事项记录。
Our law enforcement professionals can seek a roving wiretap to keep up with terrorists when they switch cell phones.
（这样）我们的执法人员可以在恐怖分子切换手机时通过使用漫游窃听继续跟踪他们。
We can seek a wiretap on so-called lone wolves – suspected terrorists who may not be directly tied to a terrorist group.
（这样）我们可以利用名为”孤狼（lone wolves）”的手段推测非直系属于恐怖组织的嫌疑分子。
These tools are not controversial.
这些措施并没有争议。
Since 9/11, they have been renewed numerous times.
自从 911 事件以来，这些条款已经更新多次。
FBI Director James Comey says they are “essential” and that losing them would “severely” impact terrorism investigations.
FBI 局长 James Comey 称这些措施”必不可少”，而失去他们将”严重”影响恐怖主义侦查。
But if Congress doesn’t act by tomorrow at midnight, these tools go away as well.
但如果国会在明天午夜之前没有达成，这些措施将会付水东流。

The USA Freedom Act also accomplishes something I called for a year and a half ago: it ends the bulk metadata program – the bulk collection of phone records – as it currently exists and puts in place new reforms.
《美国自由法案》同样达成我一年半之前所呼吁的一些事务：它终止了大量元数据项目－大量收集通信记录——因此现在存在并正在实施新的改革。
The government will no longer hold these records; telephone providers will.
政府将不再持有这些记录；（而）电信运营商将仍将（持有）。
The Act also includes other changes to our surveillance laws – including more transparency – to help build confidence among the American people that your privacy and civil liberties are being protected.
法案同样包含监视法的其它改变——包括：更加透明——来帮助在美国人中重建自信，（因为）你的隐私和自由是受保护的。
But if Congress doesn’t act by midnight tomorrow, these reforms will be in jeopardy, too.
但如果国会在明天午夜之前没有达成，这些改变也会有危险。

It doesn’t have to be this way.
完全没有必要（发展成）这样。
The USA Freedom Act reflects ideas from privacy advocates, our private sector partners and our national security experts.
《美国自由法案》反映出了隐私拥护者，我们的私营合作伙伴和我们的国家安全专家们的想法。
It already passed the House of Representatives with overwhelming bipartisan support – Republicans and Democrats.
它已经通过众议院压倒了两党的支持——共和党和民主党。
A majority of the Senate – Republicans and Democrats – have voted to move it forward.
参议院的主要组成——共和党和民主党——已经投票决定推进（实施）。

So what’s the problem?
所以问题出在哪里？
A small group of senators is standing in the way.
一小部分参议员却站在自己的立场。
And, unfortunately, some folks are trying to use this debate to score political points.
并且，不幸的是，一些人正在试图利用这次辩论打上政治分数。
But this shouldn’t and can’t be about politics.
但是这不该也决不关于政治。
This is a matter of national security.
这是国家安全事务。
Terrorists like al Qaeda and ISIL aren’t suddenly going to stop plotting against us at midnight tomorrow.
像”基地”组织和伊拉克和黎凡特伊斯兰国的恐怖组织决不会在明天午夜停下（对我们的）密谋。
And we shouldn’t surrender the tools that help keep us safe.
并且我们决不会放弃我们的安全措施。
It would be irresponsible.
（否则）这是不负责任的（行为）。
It would be reckless.
（否则）这是鲁莽的（行为）。
And we shouldn’t allow it to happen.
并且我们决不允许这种事情发生。

So today, I’m calling on Americans to join me in speaking with one voice to the Senate.
所以今天，我在呼吁大家和我一起一致声援参议院。
Put the politics aside.
将政治放在一边。
Put our national security first.
把我们的安全放在首位。
Pass the USA Freedom Act – now.
通过《美国自由法案》——现在。
And let’s protect the security and civil liberties of every American.
让我们保护每一个美国人的安全和自由。
Thanks very much.
非常感谢大家（的倾听）。
