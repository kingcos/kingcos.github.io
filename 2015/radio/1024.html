Weekly Address: Protecting our Planet for Future Generations

每周演讲：为了未来一代保护我们的星球

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President laid out the importance of serving as good stewards of the environment and maintaining the planet for generations to come.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统指出作为好的环境管理者对待（环境）以及为子孙保护星球的重要性。
Since taking office the President has prioritized protecting the places that make America special.
自从上任以来总统已优先保护那些美国特色（的地方）。
He has repeatedly said that no challenge poses a greater threat to our future than a changing climate, which is why he’s taken bold actions at home and encouraged historic action abroad on the issue.
他一再强调，没有任何挑战比气候变化对我们的未来更严重，这就是为什么他在国内采取大胆的措施并在国外鼓励具有历史意义的行动。
In his address, he encouraged Congress to reauthorize and fully fund the Land and Water Conservation Fund which has protected more than 5 million acres of land for more than half a century, without costing taxpayers a dime.
在他的演讲中，他鼓励国会授权并全力建立水土保护基金，其在半个世纪以来已经保护了超过 500 万英亩的陆地，（但）没有花费纳税人一分钱。
Republicans in Congress let the fund expire despite strong bipartisan support.
国会中的共和党人使得基金失效，尽管两党均强力支持。
And he reminded us that we all have to do our part to address climate change, promote clean energy and energy efficiency, and ensure a cleaner, more stable environment for future generations.
并且总统提醒我们，我们每一个人必须尽我们一己之力来解决气候变化，促进清洁能源和高效能源，并确保为（我们的）下一代（提供）一个更加干净，更加稳定的环境。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, October 24, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 10 月 24 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
October 24, 2015
2015 年 10 月 24 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
Our country is home to some of the most beautiful God-given landscapes in the world.
我们的祖国是世界上（少数）几个拥有天赐的最漂亮的风景名胜的家园之一。
We’re blessed with natural treasures – from the Grand Tetons to the Grand Canyon; from lush forests and vast deserts to lakes and rivers teeming with wildlife.
我们被（这些）自然之宝所保佑－从大铁顿到美国大峡谷；从茂盛的森林和巨大的沙漠到充满野生动植物的湖泊与河流。
And it’s our responsibility to protect these treasures for future generations, just as previous generations protected them for us.
这是我们的责任为新一代保护这些珍贵（的资源），就如上一代为我们而保护它们（一样）。

Since taking office, I’ve set aside more than 260 million acres of public lands and waters – more than any President in history.
自从上任，我已经拨出 2.8 亿英亩公共陆地和水域－比历史上任何一位总统都要多。
Last month, we announced that 11 states had come together with ranchers, and industry groups to protect a threatened species – the sage grouse – without jeopardizing local economies.
上月，我们宣布有 11 个州因牧场主和工业小组而达成共识，来保护濒临灭绝的物种－艾草鸡－（而这并）没有危机到当地的经济。
Two weeks ago, we announced that we’re creating one new marine sanctuary on the Potomac River in Maryland, and another along Lake Michigan in Wisconsin – part of unprecedented efforts to restore the Chesapeake Bay and the Great Lakes.
两周前，我们宣布，我们正在马里兰州的波托马克河建立一个新的海洋（生物）庇护所，并在威斯康星州的密西根湖旁建立另一个－（用）前所未有的努力来恢复切萨皮克湾河和北美五大湖。
We also joined a coalition of countries cracking down on illegal fishing that threatens jobs and food security around the globe.
在全球，我们同样加入了一个打击危害就业和食品安全的非法捕鱼国家联盟。
And I’m going to keep protecting the places that make America special, and the livelihoods of those who depend on them.
并且我将继续保护那些美国富有特色的地方，和那些赖此生存的人们。

We’ll also keep doing what we can to prevent the worst effects of climate change before it’s too late.
我们同样将继续尽我们所能在为时未晚前阻止气候变化带来的最严重的后果。
Over the past six years, we’ve led by example, generating more clean energy and lowering our carbon emissions.
在过去的六年，我们已经试行领导过，产生更多的清洁能源并降低我们的碳排放。
Our businesses have stepped up in a big way, including just this past week.
我们的企业已经加大力度，包括刚刚过去的这周。
Some of our biggest companies made new commitments to act on climate – not just because it’s good for the planet, but because it’s good for their bottom line.
我们的一些最大的公司已经作出新的关于气候的承诺－不仅仅是因为这有利于我们的星球，而是因为这是他们的底线。

This is how America is leading on the environment.
（以下）就是美国正在环境（方面）是如何领导的。
And because America is leading by example, 150 countries, representing over 85% of global emissions, have now laid out plans to reduce their levels of the harmful carbon pollution that warms our planet.
由于美国以身作则，150 个国家，相当于超过 85% 的国际排放，现在已经按计划减少了有害于地球的碳排放等级。
And it gives us great momentum going into Paris this December, where the world needs to come together and build on these individual commitments with an ambitious, long-term agreement to protect this Earth for our kids.
而这给了我们今年 12 月动身去巴黎的强大动力，在那里世界需要联合并给出有决心的单独承诺，（以及）长期的为我们的孩子们保护地球的协议。

Now Congress has to do its job.
现在，国会不得不履行其职责。
This month, even as Republicans in Congress barely managed to keep our government open, they shut down something called the Land and Water Conservation Fund.
本月，尽管共和党在国会勉强保持我们的政府继续开张，（但）他们关闭了水土保护基金。
For more than half a century, this fund has protected more than 5 million acres of land – from playgrounds to parks to priceless landscapes – all without costing taxpayers a dime.
半个多世纪以来，这项基金以及保护了超过 500 万英亩的陆地－从游乐场到公园再到无价的风景名胜－全都没有花费纳税人一分钱。
Nearly every single county in America has benefited from this program.
美国几乎每一个县都将因这个项目而受益。
It has bipartisan support in both the House and the Senate.
它受到了来自众议院和参议院中两党的共同支持。
Republicans in Congress should reauthorize and fully fund the Land and Water Conservation Fund without delay.
国会中的共和党应该重新授权并毫无迟疑的全力建立水土保护资金。

After all, as Pope Francis reminds us so eloquently, this planet is a gift from God – and our common home.
毕竟，正如教宗方济如此频繁地提醒我们，这个星球是上帝赐予我们的一份礼物－（也）是我们共同的家园。
We should leave it to our kids in better shape than we found it.
我们应该为我们的孩子把它建设的比我们降临这颗星球时更加美好。

Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），并祝周末愉快。

steward n.乘务员；管理员
stable adj.稳定的
canyon n.峡谷
species n.物种
marine adj.海洋的
sanctuary n.庇护所
coalition n.联盟
eloquently adv.雄辩地
