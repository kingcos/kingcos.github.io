Weekly Address: This Thanksgiving, Recognizing the Greatness of American Generosity

每周演讲：今年的感恩节，向美国人的无私致敬

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President wished everyone a happy Thanksgiving, and reflected on America’s history of welcoming men and women seeking a safer, better future for themselves and their families.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统祝愿每个人感恩节快乐，并（希望大家）纪念那些在美国历史上曾为他们自己和家人寻求更安全更美好未来的友好的人们。
On this uniquely American holiday, he recognized the greatness of American generosity, as evidenced by people are the country who use the day to volunteer and give back to others.
在这个独特的美国节日，他赞赏了美国人无私的伟大，因为这个国度的那些在感恩节当志愿者和回报他人的人们。
And he shared stories of Americans who, in that same spirit of generosity, have written letters to him expressing their willingness to open their homes to refugees fleeing the brutality of ISIL.
他又分享了美国人的故事，同样的无私精神，（那些人）给他写信表示他们情愿敞开自己的家门接纳受 ISIL 迫害的难民。
Like the pilgrims who set sail on the Mayflower nearly four centuries ago, these refugees are looking for safety and another chance.
就像大约四世纪以前扬帆起航的五月花号船上的朝圣者，这些难民正寻找安全和其他机会。
And it is important to remember that they undergo the highest security checks of anyone traveling to the United States.
（当然，）很重要的一点，不要忘了任何人前往美国都会接受最高等级的安全检查。
The President reminded us that providing refuge to some of the world’s most vulnerable people is an American tradition, and part of what makes this country the greatest on Earth.
总统提醒我们，向世界上最易受伤害的一些人们提供庇护是美国的传统，这也是造就美国成为世界上最强大国家的一部分原因。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, November 25, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 11 月 25 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
November 25, 2015
2015 年 11 月 25 日


Text：
正文：

Hi, everybody.
大家好。
In 1620, a small band of pilgrims came to this continent, refugees who had fled persecution and violence in their native land.
1620 年，一小帮朝圣者来到这片大陆，他们是在故土受到暴力而逃离迫害的难民。
Nearly 400 years later, we remember their part in the American story – and we honor the men and women who helped them in their time of need.
大约 400 年后，我们（仍然）铭记他们在美国历史中（所存在）的部分－并且我们（同样）纪念那些在危急时刻向他们伸出援手的人们。

Thanksgiving is a day for food and football, and for hoping the turkey didn’t turn out too dry.
感恩节是食物和橄榄球的节日，希望土鸡不要变得太干（译者注：个人感觉这里在含沙射影些什么… turkey n. 土耳其；土鸡）。
But it’s also a day to count our blessings and give back to others – a reminder that no matter our circumstances, all of us have something to be grateful for.
但是，这同样是感恩和回报他人的日子－记得，无论我们身处什么样的状况，我们都应心存感激。
Maybe it’s good health, a new addition to the family, or a child taking a next step toward college or a career.
可能是健康的身体，或是一位新添的家庭成员，或是孩子迈向大学或者入职。
Maybe it’s a new job, or long overdue raise.
可能是一份新的工作，或是积蓄已久的升职。
Maybe it’s something as simple, and as important, as the chance to spend time with the people who matter most.
可能是一些很简单的事情，也（可能是）很重要的事情，或者（只是一个）和最在乎你的人一起度过的机会。

Of course, every American can be thankful for the chance to live in a country founded on the belief that all of us are created equal.
当然，每一位美国人应当欣慰（，我们）生长在一个生来平等的国家。
And as President, I’m thankful that I get to see the best of America every day – the courage of our troops and veterans, the resilience of our families, and the basic goodness of the ordinary people who call this country.
而我作为总统，我很欣慰，每一天我都能看到最好的美利坚－（受到）我们军队和退役老兵的鼓舞，（受到）我们家庭（而）恢复，以及组成这个国家的普通人的基本美德。

On this uniquely American holiday, we also remember that so much of our greatness comes from our generosity.
在这个独特的美国节日，我们同样铭记来自我们无私的伟大。
There’s the generosity of Americans who volunteer at food banks and shelters, making sure that no one goes hungry on a day when so many plates are full.
这是来自在食品仓库和庇护所的美国义工的奉献，确保没有人在这样一个食物富足的日子挨饿。
There’s the generosity of Americans who take part not just in Black Friday and Cyber Monday, but Giving Tuesday – recognizing that in the holiday season, what you give is as important as what you get.
这是那些不只是参与黑色星期五和网购星期一，还参与善施星期二的美国人的无私－在这个节日季（我们）认识到你所付出的和你所得到的是一样重要的。

And I’ve been touched by the generosity of the Americans who’ve written me letters and emails in recent weeks, offering to open their homes to refugees fleeing the brutality of ISIL.
而最近几周，我被一些写给我的信件和邮件的人们所触动，（他们说愿意）为那些受到凶残的 ISIL 迫害的难民敞开自己的家门。

Now, people should remember that no refugee can enter our borders until they undergo the highest security checks of anyone traveling to the United States.
现在，人们应该知道所有进入美国边境的人包括难民，需要接受最高级别的安全检查。
That was the case before Paris, and it’s the case now.
这是在巴黎事件之前的形势，现在也是。
And what happened in Paris hasn’t stopped Americans from opening their arms anyway.
并且发生在巴黎的事件并没有阻止美国人张开的双臂。

One woman from Pennsylvania wrote me to say, “Money is tight for us in my household … But I have a guest room.
一位来自宾夕法尼亚州的女士写信给我说，”钱对于我们家来说很紧俏… 但是我有一间客房。
I have a pantry full of food.
（还有）一间放满食物的储物室。
We can do this.”
我们可以这样做。”
Another woman from Florida told me her family’s history dates back to the Mayflower – and she said that welcoming others is part of “what it means to be an American.”
另一位来自佛罗里达州的女士告诉我她们家族历史追溯到五月花号船－并且她说友好的人们是”身为美国人的意义所在”的一部分。

Nearly four centuries after the Mayflower set sail, the world is still full of pilgrims – men and women who want nothing more than the chance for a safer, better future for themselves and their families.
在五月花号扬帆起航后的大约四个世纪后，世界仍充满了朝圣者－那些仅仅是为了（寻求一个）让自己和家族更安全更美好未来的人们。
What makes America America is that we offer that chance.
是什么让美利坚（成为）美国就是这样的机会。
We turn Lady Liberty’s light to the world, and widen our circle of concern to say that all God’s children are worthy of our compassion and care.
我们向世界点亮自由女神之光，并扩大关注圈以表示所有神的子民都值得我们同情和关心。
That’s part of what makes this the greatest country on Earth.
这是造就美国成为世界上最强大国家的部分原因。

I hope that you and your family have wonderful Thanksgiving, surrounded by loved ones, and full of joy and gratitude.
我希望你和你的家人能够过一个美好的感恩节，和所爱的人一起，充满快乐和感激。
And together, may we all play our own small part in the American story, and write a next chapter that future generations can be thankful for.
并且，我们所有人都在美国的故事中一起扮演着自己的小角色，并书写着未来一代将会感激的新篇章。

From the Obama family to yours, have a great Thanksgiving.
来自奥巴马全家的问候，感恩节快乐。
