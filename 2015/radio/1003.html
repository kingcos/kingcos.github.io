Weekly Address: Congress Should Do its Job and Pass a Serious Budget

每周演讲：国会应当尽其职并通过严格预算案

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President emphasized that we need to do everything we can to strengthen economic growth and job creation.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统强调了我们需要尽一切努力加强经济建设和创造就业机会。
This week, despite the fact that more than half of Republicans in Congress voted to shut down the government for the second time in two years, Congress managed to pass a last-minute bill to keep the government open for another ten weeks.
本周，尽管事实是国会中超过半数的共和党人投票在两年内第二次停摆政府，国会决定通过一项最后时刻的法案来保持政府在另外的 10 周内继续开放。
That means that in December, we could face yet another Republican threat to shut down the government.
这意味着在十二月，我们会面对共和党停摆政府的又一次威胁。
The President emphasized that Congress needs to stop kicking the can down the road and do its job.
总统强调，国会需要停止踢皮球并尽其职责。
He stressed that Republicans and Democrats need to work together to pass a budget that fully funds the government and reverses the harmful sequestration cuts, and vowed that he would not sign another shortsighted spending bill like the one Congress sent him this past week.
他强调，共和党人和民主党人需要合作来通过预算案，全面建立政府并扭转有害的隐藏削减，再发誓：他将不会签署另一项上周国会递交给他的目光短浅的开支法案。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, October 3, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 10 月 3 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
October 3, 2015
2015 年 10 月 3 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
Yesterday, we learned that our businesses created another 118,000 new jobs in September.
昨天，（据）我们了解，在九月我们的企业创造了另外的 118,000 个新的工作岗位。
That makes 67 straight months of job creation, and 13.2 million new jobs in all.
这是连续 67 月的岗位增长，并且一共有 1,320 万新的工作岗位。

But we would be doing even better if we didn’t have to keep dealing with crises in Congress every few months.
但是如果没有每隔几月不得不在国会持续处理危机，我们甚至能得更好。
And especially at a time when the global economy is softening, our own growth could slow if Congress doesn’t do away with harmful austerity measures.
并且尤其在全球经济放缓的同时，如果国会不废除有害的紧缩措施，我们自己的（经济）增长将会变慢。

Now, on Wednesday, more than half of Republicans in Congress voted to shut down the government for the second time in two years.
现在，本周三，国会中超过半数的共和党人在两年内第二次投票停摆政府。
Fortunately, there were enough votes in both parties to pass a last-minute bill to keep the government open for another ten weeks.
幸运的是，来自两党足够的选票通过了一项最后时刻的法案来维持政府可以额外开张 10 周。
Unfortunately, that gimmick only sets up another shutdown threat two weeks before Christmas.
不幸的是，这个诡计只是设置在圣诞节前两周面临的又一次停摆的风险。
Look, that’s not the way America should operate.
（你）看，这不是美国应当运转的方式。
It just kicks the can down the road without solving any problems or doing any long-term planning for the future.
这就像是将垃圾罐踢向路边而不解决任何问题也不为未来做长期规划。
And that’s why I will not sign another shortsighted, short-term spending bill like the one Congress sent me this week.
这就是我为什么拒绝签署国会本周递交给我的另一项目光短浅的短期开支法案。

Here’s why.
原因就在这里。
A few years ago, both parties agreed to put in place harmful, automatic cuts that make no distinction between spending we don’t need and spending we do.
几年前，两党曾同意正式实施有害的、自动的削减，使得花费在我们不需要的方面和花费在我们所做的方面之间没有差异。
Those cuts have actually kept our economy from growing faster.
这些削减实际上已经阻碍了我们的经济发展越来越迅速。
Even worse, they’re actually undermining the middle class.
甚至更糟的是，他们实际上暗中伤害了中产阶级。

Here’s one example.
举个例子。
If we don’t undo these mindless cuts, then next year, we’ll be funding our kids’ education at the same levels per pupil we did in the year 2000.
如果我们不做这些盲目的削减，那么下一年，我们为孩子们筹备的教育基金将和 2000 年时处于人均同一水平。
Compared to my budget, that would be like cutting federal funding for 4,500 schools, 17,500 teachers and aides, 1.9 million students.
与我的预算案相比，这将可能为 4,500 所学校，17,599 老师和助理，190 万学生削减联邦基金。

That’s not good for our kids or our economy.
这对于我们的孩子和我们的经济并不好。
It’s a prescription for American decline.
这是美国衰落的处方药。
And it shouldn’t happen.
而这也不应该发生。
We should invest in things like education today, or we’ll pay the price tomorrow.
今天，我们应该投资像教育（等诸如此类的），否则我们将在未来付出代价。

Congress should do its job, stop kicking the can down the road, and pass a serious budget rather than flirt with another shutdown.
国会应该尽其职责，停下踢皮球，并通过一项严格的预算案而不是随随便便再一次停摆（政府）。
A serious budget is one that keeps America strong through our military, our law enforcement; that keeps America generous through caring for our veterans and our seniors; that keeps America competitive by educating our kids and our workers.
一项严格的预算案，它能够强大我们的军事，我们的法律；它能够保持关心我们的老战士和前辈；它能够通过教育我们的孩子和工人保持美国的竞争力。

That’s what I want to work with serious people in both parties to achieve.
这就是我想和来自所有党派的人一起努力去争取的。
Because that’s how we’ll build on the progress of 13 million new jobs, and help the middle class get ahead.
因为这是我们如何建立 1,300 万新的工作岗位，并帮助中产阶级获取成功的进展情况。

Thanks everybody, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），并祝周末愉快。

sequestration n.扣押
shortsight adj.目光短浅的
crisis n.危机 pl: crises
austerity n.朴素
gimmick n.花招，噱头
undermine v.暗中颠覆
aide n.助手
flirt v.调情，不认真对待
