Weekly Address: Fighting for Trade Deals that Put American Workers First

每周演讲：争取在贸易协定中将美国工人放在第一位

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President laid out why new, high-standards trade agreements are important for our economy, our businesses, our workers, and our values.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统阐述了为什么新的高标准贸易协定对于我们的经济，企业，工人和价值观如此重要。
These new trade deals are vital to middle-class economics – the idea that this country does best when everybody gets their fair shot, everybody does their fair share, and everybody plays by the same set of rules.
这些新的贸易协定对于中产阶级的经济状况具有极其重要的意义——（对于）这个国家最好的时候的（一种）描述就是每个人都可以得到公平的机会，每个人都平等地做好自己份内的事情，每个人在相同的规则中扮演（着自己的角色）。
The President has been clear – any deal he signs will be the most progressive trade agreement in our history with strong provisions for both workers and the environment.
总统已经明确——任何史上由他签署的最新协定将包含对于工人和环境强有力的（保障）条款。
It would also level the playing field – and when the playing field is level, American workers always win.
这也将使竞争环境变得公平——且当竞争环境（已然）公平，美国的工人将永远是胜出者。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, April 25, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 4 月 25 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
April 25, 2015
2015 年 4 月 25 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。

I’ve talked a lot lately about why new trade deals are important to our economy.
最近我已经谈了多次关于为什么新的贸易协定对于我们的经济十分重要。

Today, I want to talk about why new trade deals are important to our values.
今天，我想谈谈为什么新的贸易协定对于我们的价值观（同样）十分重要。

They’re vital to middle-class economics – the idea that this country does best when everyone gets their fair shot, everyone does their fair share, and everyone plays by the same set of rules.
它们对于中产阶级的经济状况具有极其重要的意义－（对于）这个国家最好的时候的（一种）描述就是每个人都可以得到公平的机会，每个人都平等地做好自己份内的事情，每个人在同一套规则中扮演（着自己的角色）。

These are simple values.
这是些简单的价值观。
They’re American values.
它们是美国（人）的价值观。
And we strive to make sure our own economy lives up to them, especially after a financial crisis brought about by recklessness and greed.
并且我们努力确保我们自己的经济基于它们而发展，尤其是（在经历了）鲁莽和贪婪导致的金融危机之后（的时期）。
But we also live in a world where our workers have to compete on a global scale.
但是我们同样生活在一个我们的工人必须在全球范围内竞争的世界中。
Right now, on an uneven playing field.
眼下，正在一个不平等竞争的环境中。
Where the rules are different.
（不同）区域的规则是不同的。
And that’s why America has to write the rules of the global economy – so that our workers can compete on a level playing field.
这就是为什么美国必须（主导）制定全球经济协定－这样我们的工人才可以平等的竞争。

I understand why a lot of people are skeptical of trade deals.
我理解为什么很多人对于贸易协定（持有）怀疑（态度）。
Past deals didn’t always live up to the hype.
过去的协定总是相悖于夸张的宣传。
They didn’t include the kind of protections we’re fighting for today.
他们并不包含我们今天所努力争取的保障（权益）。

We have lessons to learn from the past – and we have learned them.
我们（今天）需要吸取过去的教训－并且我们已经改过自新。
But trying to stop a global economy at our shores isn’t one of those lessons.
但是试图在我们的支撑下去阻止全球经济并不是这些教训之一。
We can’t surrender to the future – because we are meant to win the future.
我们不能放弃未来－因为我们应当赢得未来。
If America doesn’t shape the rules of the global economy today, to benefit our workers, while our economy is in a position of new global strength, then China will write those rules.
如果美国今天不去规范全球经济协定，去受益我们的工人，（否则）当我们的经济处于新的全球实力位置之时，中国将（主导）制定这些协定。
I’ve seen towns where manufacturing collapsed, plants closed down, and jobs dried up.
我已经看到（某些）城镇工业重创，工厂倒闭，工人失业。
And I refuse to accept that for our workers.
我拒绝接受我们工人这样的现状。
Because I know when the playing field is level, nobody can beat us.
因为我明白，当竞争环境变得公平，没有人可以打败我们。

That’s why, when I took office, we started thinking about how to revamp trade in a way that actually works for working Americans.
这就是为什么，当我在任时，便开始考虑如何重整贸易，一种实实在在为了（我们）美国工人的方式。
And that’s what we’ve done with a new trade partnership we’re negotiating in the Asia-Pacific – home to the world’s fastest-growing markets.
而这就是我们和新的贸易伙伴组织所正在（努力）做的－（连通）家到世界上发展最迅速的市场。

It’s the highest-standard trade agreement in history.
这就是史上最高标准的贸易协定。
It’s got strong provisions for workers and the environment – provisions that, unlike in past agreements, are actually enforceable.
它包含对于工人和环境强有力的（保障）条款－（而）不像是过去的，（因为）这些条款实际是强制执行的。
If you want in, you have to meet these standards.
如果你想加入（贸易伙伴组织），你必须遵守这些标准。
If you don’t, then you’re out.
如果你不遵守，那么你将出局。
Once you’re a part of this partnership, if you violate your responsibilities, there are actually consequences.
一旦成为这个贸易伙伴组织的一部分，如果违反职责，将会有实质性后果。
And because it would include Canada and Mexico, it fixes a lot of what was wrong with NAFTA, too.
并且由于它将适用于加拿大和墨西哥，它同样修正了许多与北美自由贸易协定相矛盾（的条款）。

So this isn’t a race to the bottom, for lower wages and working conditions.
所以这并不是一个自甘堕落，更少的薪水和更恶劣的工作条件的竞赛。
The trade agreements I’m negotiating will drive a race to the top.
由我所参与谈判的贸易协定将推向新的高度。
And we’re making sure American workers can retool through training programs and community colleges, and use new skills to transition into new jobs.
并且我们正确保美国工人可以通过培训项目，社区大学革新自我，并使用新的技术过渡到新的工作。

If I didn’t think this was the right thing to do for working families, I wouldn’t be fighting for it.
如果我不认为这有利于工薪家庭，我将不会为之争取。
We’ve spent the past six years trying to rescue the economy, retool the auto industry, and revitalize American manufacturing.
我们已经在过去六年试图挽救经济，重整汽车业，重振制造业。
And if there were ever an agreement that undercut that progress, or hurt those workers, I wouldn’t sign it.
并且如果曾有协定，违背这些准则，或者不利于（我们的）工人，我绝不会签字（允许）。
My entire presidency is about helping working families recover from recession and rebuild for the future.
我的整个总统任期就是帮助工薪阶层从经济衰退中恢复，并重建未来。
As long as I’m President, that’s what I’ll keep fighting to do.
只要我仍在任，我将（不遗余力）坚持为其努力。

Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快。

provision n.供应，提供，规定，条款
strive v.努力，奋斗
recklessness n.鲁莽
greed n.贪婪
skeptical adj.怀疑的
hype v./n.大肆宣传
shore v./n.支撑，支柱
surrender v./n.投降，放弃
manufacturing n.制造业，工业
collapse v./n.暴跌，失败
plant n.工厂，植物
revamp v./n.翻新，修补
negotiate v.谈判，协商
responsibility n.责任，职责
consequence n.后果，结果
auto n.<美>汽车
revitalize v.使具有新的活力
undercut v.切除n.牛里脊，浮雕
uneven adj.不对等的，不平坦的

do one’s share 做好某人份内的事
financial crisis 金融危机
be meant to do 应该做，照规矩做
dry up 干涸
take office 就职
