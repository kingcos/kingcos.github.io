Weekly Address: Honoring Our Fallen Heroes this Memorial Day

每周演讲：在阵亡将士纪念日缅怀烈士

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President commemorated Memorial Day by paying tribute to the men and women in uniform who have given their lives in service to our country.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统通过向那些为国牺牲的烈士表达敬意来迎接阵亡将士纪念日。
The President will spend the first Memorial Day since the end of the war in Afghanistan at Arlington Cemetery, remembering the more than 2,200 American patriots who gave their lives in that conflict, as well as all of our fallen soldiers.
这是在阿富汗战争结束后迎来的第一个阵亡将士纪念日，总统将在阿灵顿公墓纪念在阿富汗战争超过 2200 名阵亡的爱国者，以及我们所有的烈士。
The President asked that all Americans spend Monday honoring the memory and sacrifice of those heroes, and remain committed to the cause of freedom and the country for which they fought.
总统希望全美人民在周一纪念历史，为英雄献祭，并铭记为了自由所付出的（鲜血）和这个他们为之奋斗的国家。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, May 23, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 5 月 23 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
May 23, 2015
2015 年 5 月 23 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
This weekend is Memorial Day—a time to pay tribute to all our men and women in uniform who’ve ever given their lives so that we can live in freedom and security.
本周末是阵亡将士纪念日，是个向那些为祖国牺牲的人表达敬意的日子，（因为他们）我们才获得自由和安全。
This year, the holiday is especially meaningful.
而今年，这个纪念日意味深长。
It’s the first Memorial Day since our war ended in Afghanistan.
这是我们在阿富汗战争结束后迎来的第一个阵亡将士纪念日。

On Monday, at Arlington Cemetery, I’ll join our Gold Star families, veterans, and their loved ones to remember all our fallen heroes, including the more than 2,200 American patriots who gave their lives in Afghanistan.
在周一，我会在阿灵顿公墓参加拥有金星奖章的家庭，退伍军人和他们曾经所爱的所铭记的那些烈士，包括在阿富汗战争超过2200 名阵亡的爱国者。
And I plan to share a few of their stories.
而我计划分享一些他们的故事。

Growing up in Arizona, Wyatt Martin loved the outdoors.
出生在亚利桑那州（Arizona）的 Wyatt Martin 曾经热爱户外。
To him, a great day was a day spent fishing.
对于他来说，钓一天鱼便是人生乐事。
After high school, he enlisted in the Army because he believed that the blessings he enjoyed as an American came with an obligation to give back to his country.
高中毕业后，他应征入伍，因为他相信他作为美国人所享受的福利便有义务为他的国家效忠。

Ramon Morris was born in Jamaica, and as a teenager came to Queens.
Ramon Morris 出生在牙买加（Jamaica），并在青年时期来到皇后区。
Like so many proud immigrants, he felt a calling to serve his new country and joined the Army.
像许多自豪的移民一样，他感到为国效力是一种召唤便应征入伍。
He fell in love, got engaged, and the thing he wanted most was to make the world safer for his three-year-old daughter.
（后来）他坠入爱河，受到雇佣，他（这辈子）的愿望便是让自己三岁的孩子所在的世界更加安全。

In their lives, Specialist Wyatt Martin and Sergeant First Class Ramon Morris travelled different paths.
在他们的生命中，专家 Wyatt Martin 和陆军上士 Ramon Morris 过着不同的生活。
But in December, their paths intersected as the final two Americans to give their lives during our combat mission in Afghanistan.
但是在十二月，他们的生命却在阿富汗的一次军事任务中戛然而止。

This weekend also reminds us that, around the world, our men and women in uniform continue to serve and risk their lives.
本周末同样提醒我们，在全世界我们的战士仍冒着生命危险保护我们。
In Afghanistan, our troops now have a new mission—training and advising Afghan forces.
在阿富汗，现在我们的军队有了新的任务，即培训阿富汗安全部队。
John Dawson was one of them.
John Dawson 便是其中一员。
From Massachusetts, he loved the Bruins and the Pats.
他来自马萨诸塞州（Massachusetts），热爱 Bruins 队和 Pats。
In April, he gave his life as an Army combat medic—the first American to give his life in this new mission.
在四月，他作为一名战地医生牺牲，（他是）这项新的军事任务中第一个牺牲的美国人。
This Memorial Day, we’ll honor Corporal Dawson as well.
这个纪念日，我们同样缅怀下士 Dawson。

Like generations of heroes before them, these Americans gave everything they had—not for glory, not even for gratitude, but for something greater than themselves.
像这些英雄的一代人，献出了他们的一切，不为光荣，甚至不为感激，但是为了比他们生命更伟大的东西。
We cannot bring them back.
我们不可能让他们回来。
Nor can we ease the pain of their families and friends who live with their loss.
我们也不能减轻他们家人和朋友的失去之痛。

But we are the Americans they died to defend.
但是我们是他们用生命保卫下（成长）的美国人。
So what we can do—what we must do—is fulfill our sacred obligations to them, just like they fulfilled theirs to us.
因此我们能做些什么，我们必须做什么，这是我们对他们的义务，就像他们为我们实现的一样。
We have to honor their memory.
我们要缅怀他们的历史。
We have to care for their families, and our veterans who served with them.
我们要照顾他们的家人，和那些曾与他们一起服役的退伍军人。
And as a nation, we have to remain worthy of their sacrifice—forever committed to the country they loved and the freedom they fought for and died for.
并且，作为一个民族，我们必须永远铭记他们牺牲的价值，永远致力于他们所爱的国家和他们争取甚至用生命换来的自由。

Thank you, have a wonderful weekend, and may God bless our fallen heroes and their families.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快，上帝会保佑我们的烈士和他们的家人。
