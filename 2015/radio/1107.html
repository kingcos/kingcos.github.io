Weekly Address: If You Haven’t Gotten Covered, Now’s Your Chance

每周演讲：如果你还未被医保保障，现在机会来了

返回

Overview：
概览：
WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President discussed the importance of reducing the number of people without health insurance.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统谈到了减少不参加医保人数的重要性。
Because of the Affordable Care Act, more people now have the security of health insurance than ever before.
由于《平价医疗法案》，现在，更多的人受到比以往（更加）安全的保险（保障）。
As the law’s coverage provisions have taken effect, 17.6 million Americans have gained coverage, and the nation’s uninsured rate now stands at its lowest level ever.
当法案适用范围生效，1760 万美国人获得覆盖，并且全国的未投保率现在处于历史最低。
The ACA is working, making health care more affordable, accessible, and of higher quality for millions of people.
《平价医疗法案》生效了，使得医疗让数以百万的人更加支付得起，更加容易获得，并且质量更高。
But there are still Americans around the country who are eligible for Marketplace coverage yet remain uninsured.
但是全国各地仍有人有资格享有却仍未投保。
The President encouraged those who do not have health insurance at this point, especially those whose communities are part of the Healthy Communities Challenge, to go online, take advantage of the open enrollment period that began this past weekend, and sign up for health care coverage.
总统鼓励这些现在（仍）未加入医保的人们，尤其是（参与）健康社区挑战的社区，（在即将）过去的周末，在线上，利用开放注册时期并注册医保。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, November 7, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 11 月 7 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
November 7, 2015
2015 年 11 月 7 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
For decades, too many working Americans went without the security of health insurance – and their financial well-being suffered because of it.
几十年来，太多的美国人因没有受到医保的保障而逝去－而他们的财务福祉却因此承担着。

We’ve begun to change that.
我们已改变此状。
As the Affordable Care Act has taken effect, we’ve covered 17.6 million Americans.
当《平价医疗法案》生效，我们已经覆盖了 1760 万美国人。
Since 2010, the uninsured rate has decreased by 45%.
2010 年迄今，未投保率下降了 45%。
And for the first time, more than 90% of Americans are covered.
并且，第一次，超过 90% 的美国人被覆盖。

If you haven’t gotten covered yet, or if you care about someone who hasn’t gotten covered yet, now’s your chance.
如果你还未被医保保障，或者你关心的人还没有投保，现在机会来了。
It’s open enrollment season for the Health Insurance Marketplace.
现在是医疗保险集市开放注册季。

What that means is, with a few clicks on HealthCare.gov, you’ll find private insurance companies competing for your business.
这意味着，在 HealthCare.gov 点击几下，你将会找到私人保险公司正竞争着为你（服务）。
You can compare plans and choose the one that’s right for your family.
你可以比较（保险）计划，并选择一个适合你家人的（保险）。
In fact, most Americans will find an option that costs less than $75 a month.
事实上，大多数人将找到一个月供低于 75$ 的选择。
Even if you already have insurance through the Marketplace, check it out.
哪怕你已经在（保险）集市投过保，再查查看。
Shopping around can save you a lot of money – last year, consumers who shopped saved almost $400.
货比三家可以为你节省许多钱－去年，有些货比三家的消费者节省了几乎 400$。

Take the story of a man named Phil Viso, who emailed me earlier this year.
听听这个今年早些时候给我发邮件的 Phil Viso 的故事。
Phil’s a software developer from my hometown of Chicago.
Phil 是来自我的家乡芝加哥的一位软件开发者。
Last winter, he had an idea for a new app and decided to start his own company.
去年冬天，他萌生了一个新 App 的想法，并决定开始自己开公司。
And that can be scary when you need to get your own insurance.
而当你需要自己为自己保险的时候是很可怕的。
But Phil logged on to HealthCare.gov, answered a few questions, picked a plan, and even found out he was eligible for a tax credit that saved him money.
但是 Phil 登陆 HealthCare.gov，咨询了些问题，选择了一个（保险）计划，并且他甚至发现自己有资格享有可以节省开支的税额减免。

Here’s what he wrote: “I’m still sort of in shock about how great the experience of signing up for health care was…
他写道：”当我注册医保时良好的体验让我仍有几分触动…
I will have a lot to worry about over the course of the year as I try to get my app released, but thankfully, good health care will not be one of those worries.”
在这一年里，我将努力发布我的软件，将会遇到很多麻烦，但是谢天谢地，好的医保将不会让我烦恼。”

After he sent me that email, Phil ended up getting a new and better job anyway.
（而）在他给我发邮件之后，不管怎样，最后 Phil 得到了一份新的更好的工作。
But that’s the whole point of health insurance.
但这就是医保的全部意义。
Peace of mind.
内心的宁静。
And under the Affordable Care Act, if you want to change jobs, go back to school, or chase that new idea, you can do it without worrying about going broke if you get sick.
在《平价医疗法案》之下，如果你想换工作，重返校园，或者追赶新想法，你可以（放手）去做而不用担心在生病之后破产。
If you’ve got a pre-existing condition – diabetes or cancer or heartburn or a heart attack – you can no longer be charged more or denied coverage.
如果你已有病症－糖尿病，癌症，胃灼热或是心脏病－你不会被多收费或是被拒绝参保。
You can no longer be charged more just for being a woman.
你也不会因身为女士而多付费。
And preventive care like checkups and immunizations now come with no additional out-of-pocket costs.
而预防性医疗例如体检，免疫（接种）现在不会再有额外的（自掏）腰包的费用。

What we’re talking about is no longer just a law, and it’s certainly not the myths and scare tactics that the cynics have peddled our way for years.
我们正在谈论的已不只单单是一项法案，并且这当然不是愤世嫉俗者多年来所散播那样的骗术和恐吓。
This is reality.
这就是事实。
This is health care in America.
这就是美国的医保。
And the bottom line is, Americans like it.
而底线是，美国人喜欢它（这项法案）。
They’re happy with their plans and their premiums.
人们对自己的保险计划和保险费满意。

So join them.
所以成为他们中的一员。
Give it a shot.
试一试。
Check out HealthCare.gov, CuidadoDeSalud.gov, or call 1-800-318-2596 to find a plan that’s right for you or someone you care about.
打开 HealthCare.gov，CuidadoDeSalud.gov，或者拨打 1-800-318-2596 为你在乎的人选择一个适合的（保险）计划。

And by the way – if you live in one of the 20 cities participating in our Healthy Communities Challenge, I want to see how many of your neighbors you can get signed up.
顺便说下－如果你生活在参与健康社区挑战的 20 个城市之一，我想看看你有多少邻居会注册。
I’ll come visit the city that enrolls the highest percentage of folks who aren’t covered right now.
我将会去注册率最高的城市，见见现在还没有被保障的人们。
That’s a promise.
这是一项承诺。

After all, this country is at its best when we look out for each other.
毕竟，当我们互相关心时，这个国家就处在最好的状态。
And together, we can help more Americans get the security that they and their families deserve.
并且我们可以一起帮助更多的美国人获得应得的保障。

Thanks, everybody.
感谢大家（倾听）。
Have a great weekend.
祝周末愉快。
