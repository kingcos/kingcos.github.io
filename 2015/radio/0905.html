Weekly Address: This Labor Day, Lets Talk About the Budget

每周演讲：今年的劳工节，让我们谈谈预算案

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President recognized Labor Day by highlighting the economic progress our country has made, and underlining what needs to be done to continue that growth.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统谈到通过强调我国所达到的经济发展进程并深化我们要做什么来延续经济增长（庆祝）劳工节。
Our businesses have created 13.1 million new jobs over the past five and a half years, the unemployment rate is the lowest it’s been in seven years, and seventeen states across the country have raised the minimum wage.
我们的企业已经在过去五年半中创造了 1310 万个新的就业岗位，失业率降到七年来最低，并且全国有十七个州已经提高最低薪资。
The President stressed that to continue this progress, Congress needs to avoid a government shutdown that would hurt middle-class Americans and pass a responsible budget before the end of September.
总统强调，延续（增长）进程，国会需要避免政府停摆，（因为）这将伤害到中产阶级的美国人，（国会）应当在九月底前通过一项负责任的财政预算案。
The President emphasized that Congress should not play games with our economy, and instead pass a budget that invests in our middle-class and helps those who work hard and play by the rules to get ahead.
总统指出，国会不应该和我们的经济玩游戏，相反，通过一项预算案，投资我们辛勤工作的中产阶级，让他们利用规则出人头地。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, September 5, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 9 月 5 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
September 5, 2015
2015 年 9 月 5 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
I hope most of you are gearing up for a long weekend with family and friends – maybe some barbeques, road trips, or fantasy drafts.
我希望你们中的大部分人正准备和家人朋友享受一个较长的周末－可能吃吃烧烤，旅旅游，或者（参加）梦幻选秀。
But I wanted to take a moment to talk to you about the real meaning of Labor Day – a day we set aside every year to honor the hardworking men and women who fought for so many of the rights that we take for granted today.
但是我想占用大家的几分钟，和你们谈谈劳工节的真正意义－（劳工节）是个我们每年空出时间为那些努力争取当今所准许的权利且辛勤工作的人们的节日。

The eight-hour workday, 40-hour workweek, weekends.
工作日工作八小时，每周工作 40 小时，周末。
Overtime and the minimum wage.
加班费和最低薪资。
Safer workplaces.
更加安全的工作场所。
Health insurance, Social Security, Medicare, and retirement plans.
健康保险，社会安全，医疗保险和退休计划。
All of those gains were fought for and won by the labor movement – folks who were working not just for a bigger paycheck for themselves, but for more security and prosperity for the folks working next to them as well.
所有的这些的利益是通过劳工运动奋起争取所赢得的－工作的人们并不只是为了他们自己更高的薪水，而是和其同样的劳工，（争取）更加安全更加繁荣。
That’s how we built the great American middle class.
这就是我们如何构建伟大的美国中产阶级。

That’s the spirit we’ve been working to restore these past six and a half years.
这是我们在过去六年半为了恢复信心所一直努力的精神。
On Friday, we found out that the economy created another 173,000 jobs in August.
在周五，我们查清了八月份经济，我们已经创建了另外的 173,000 工作岗位。
Over the past five and a half years, our businesses have created 13.1 million new jobs in total – the longest streak of job creation on record.
在过去的五年半时间里，我们的企业已经创造了一共 1310 万新的就业岗位－史上连续最长的工作岗位创造纪录。
The unemployment rate dropped to 5.1%, the lowest it’s been in seven years.
失业率跌到 5.1%，这是七年之内的最低值。
The American auto industry is on track to sell more cars and trucks this year than it has in more than a decade.
美国的汽车工业正处在良性阶段，今年比以往十年售出更多的汽车和卡车。
Sixteen million Americans have gained the security of health insurance.
1600 万美国人已经获得了健康保险的保障。
Seventeen states and about 30 cities and counties have raised the minimum wage.
十七个州和大约 30 个城市和乡村已经提高了最低薪资。
And we’ve proposed extending overtime protections to as many as five million Americans.
并且我们已经提出为多达 500 万美国人扩大超时（工作）保护。
All of that is progress.
所有这些都是进步。

This month, Congress has an opportunity to continue that progress.
本月，国会有机会继续推动这项举措。
As always, the deadline for Congress to pass a budget is the end of September.
如往常一样，国会通过一项预算案的最后期限在九月底。
Every year.
每一年。
This is not new.
这并不新鲜。
And if they don’t, they’ll shut down the government for the second time in two years.
并且如果他们不这么做，这将是他们在两年内第二次停摆政府。
At a time when the global economy faces headwinds and America’s economy is a relative bright spot in the world, a shutdown of our government would be wildly irresponsible.
在全球经济面临逆风时，并且美国经济（算是）在全球（经济）中的相对亮点，我们政府的停摆将是极其不负责任的。
It would be an unforced error that saps the momentum we’ve worked so hard to build.
这将是一个被迫的错误，削弱了我们如此努力才得以建立的成功。
Plain and simple, a shutdown would hurt working Americans.
很显然，也很简单，（政府）停摆将伤害工薪（家庭）的美国人。

It doesn’t have to happen.
（然而）这并不是不得不发生的。
If Congress wants to support working Americans and strengthen our middle class, they can pass a budget that invests in, not makes cuts to, the middle class.
如果国会想要支持工薪（家庭）的美国人，并加强我们的中产阶级，他们将通过这项预算案，投资而不是剥削中产阶级。
If they pass a budget with shortsighted sequester cuts that harm our military and our economy, I’ll veto it.
如果他们通过了一项目光短浅、偷偷剥削（的预算案），伤害我们的军事和经济，（那么）我将否决它。
If they make smart investments in our military readiness, our infrastructure, our schools, public health, and research, I’ll sign that budget – and they know that.
如果他们聪明的投资我们的军事完好性，我们的基础设施，我们学校，公共健康，和研究。我将签署这项预算案－并且他们知道这一点。

So let’s get it done.
所以让我们完成它。
Our economy doesn’t need another round of threats and brinksmanship.
我们的经济并不需要另一轮的威胁和边缘政策。
Nobody gets to play games with our economy – or the middle class I grew up in, and that you grew up in.
不要和我们的经济开玩笑，或是你我共同出身的中产阶级。
So tell Congress to pass a budget that reflects the values we honor on Labor Day.
所以要求国会通过预算案才是我们庆祝劳工节的价值（所在）。
Rewarding hard work.
奖励辛勤工作。
Giving everybody a fair shot.
给予每个人都享有的公平权利。
And working together to give all of our kids a better life.
并且一起为我们的孩子带来更好的生活。

Thanks everybody.
感谢大家（倾听）。
And enjoy your weekend.
并（愿大家）享受周末。
