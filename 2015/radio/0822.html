Weekly Address: It’s Time for Congress To Pass a Responsible Budget

每周演讲：国会是时候通过一项负责任的预算案了

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President spoke to the economic progress that our country has made, from 13 million new jobs created over the past five and a half years, to 17 states raising the minimum wage.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统谈到我国（已经）取得了经济进步，从过去五年半所创造的 1300 万个工作岗位到 17 个州提高最低工资标准。
Congress needs to do its part to continue to help grow the economy, but instead left town(译者注：这里的 town 开始我以为是 down 的误写，但是在后面的音频中确实为 town，可能译作 市民，大众 好点，句子内翻译不再改变) last month with a great deal undone.
国会需要尽其所能帮助拉动经济，但是（国会）很多事务未完成就开始了上月的休假。
Congress failed to reauthorize the Ex-Im Bank, which enjoys bipartisan support and is tasked solely with creating American jobs by growing exports.
国会没能重新批准为两党所共同支持的进出口银行，其任务就是通过扩大出口来创造（更多）美国人的就业岗位。
And most pressingly, the Republican Congress failed to uphold their most basic responsibility to fund the government, leaving them only a few weeks once they return to pass a budget, or shut down the government for the second time in two years.
并且最严重地是，共和党所主导的国会没有坚持他们最基本的责任来支持政府，使得一旦他们（只能）在短短几周内重回通过预算案，（否则）在两年之内政府将再次停摆。
The President made clear that Congress needs to get to work on behalf of the American people and reach a budget agreement that relieves the harmful sequester cuts and keeps our economy growing.
总统明确表示国会需要继续为美国人民的利益着想并达成一项减少有害的没收开支并保持经济增长的预算案。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, August 22, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 8 月 22 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
August 22, 2015
2015 年 8 月 22 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
Seven years after the worst economic crisis in generations, our economy continues to grow and create jobs.
在史上最严重的经济危机后的七年中，我们的经济（已）持续增长，并创造（更多的）就业岗位。
In fact, our businesses have created 13 million new jobs over the past five and a half years.
事实上，我们的企业在过去五年半创造了 1300 万个工作岗位。
But if we want to keep this momentum going – to make sure that working families feel like their hard work is being rewarded with a basic sense of security – then we all need to do our part.
但是如果我们想保持前进的动力－以确保工薪家庭有基本的安全感，即感到他们的辛勤工作能够得到应有的报偿－那么我们都需要做好自己的分内之事。

That’s why my Administration has been partnering with states and cities to help grow the middle class.
这就是为什么我的政府已经和州以及城市合作来帮助发展中产阶级。
Over the past few years, nearly 20 cities and counties have implemented paid sick days.
在过去的几年中，大约 20 座城市和村庄已经实施了带薪病假。
Six states have enacted paid sick days or paid family leave.
六个州已经实施了带薪病假活着带薪探亲假。
Seventeen states, and more than two dozen cities and counties, have raised their minimum wage.
十七个州，和超过二十多个城市和村庄已经提高了他们的最低工资。
All of this will help working families.
所有这一切将帮助工薪家庭。
And across the country, folks are proving that preparing all our kids for the future doesn’t have to be a partisan issue.
而放眼全国，人们正在证明为所有我们的孩子的未来准备并不一定是个党派问题。
Seattle, a city with a Democratic mayor, just passed universal pre-k, while Indianapolis, a city with a Republican mayor, is starting citywide preschool scholarships.
西雅图，一个民主党为市长的城市，刚刚通过了普适的学前教育，而印第安纳波利斯，一个共和党为市长的城市，正在开启全市性的学前奖学金。
All told, 34 states have increased funding for preschool.
总而言之，34 个州已经增加了学前教育的资金。
And that’s good for all of us.
并且这有利于我们大家。

Now, we need Congress to do its part to boost the economy, as well.
现在，我们同样需要国会来尽其所能刺激经济。
Unfortunately, Congress left town for five full weeks – and they left behind a stack of unfinished business.
不幸的是，国会休假五个整星期－并且他们留下一堆未完成的事务。
For the first time ever, Congress failed to reauthorize the Export-Import Bank.
这是史上第一次，国会未能重新批准进出口银行。
That left thousands of business owners and their employees at a serious disadvantage compared to their competitors overseas.
这使得成千上万的企业主们和他们的雇员和海外的竞争者们（相比）处于严重的不利地位。
That’s not good for jobs.
这不利于工作。
It’s not good for our economy.
也同样不利于我们的经济。
When it returns from recess, reauthorizing the bank ought to be a top agenda for members of Congress.
当他们休假回来，重新批准银行应该是国会成员的首要议题。

Congress also hasn’t passed a budget – and when they return from vacation, they’ll only have a few weeks to do so, or shut down the government for the second time in two years.
国会同样没有通过预算案，并且当他们休假回来，他们将只有短短几周，或者在两年之内再次停摆政府。
They’ve had all year to do this.
（但）他们已经有一整年时间来这么做。
Months ago, I put forward a detailed plan to strengthen our economy and our national security in a fiscally responsible way.
几个月之前，我曾推进一项详尽的计划以负责任的财政方式来强化我们的经济和国家安全
And for months, I’ve said I will veto any budget that locks in the sequester—those senseless cuts to domestic and national security priorities.
并且几个月以来，我已经说过我将否决任何藏匿的预算－（包括）那些无意义的削减国内和（关乎）国家安全的优先事项。
Remember, we can’t cut our way to prosperity.
请记住，我们不能减少（通往）富强的道路。
We should be investing in things that help our economy grow today and tomorrow, like education or infrastructure or scientific research.
我们应当投资那些有助于发展现在与未来经济的事务，例如教育，或是基础设施，或是科学研究。

Democrats in Congress have made it clear they’re ready to sit down and work with Republicans to find common ground on this.
国会的民主党人士已经明确，他们正准备坐下来和共和党人士一起寻找这方面的共同点。
After all, Americans expect Congress to help keep our country strong and growing – not threaten to shut down our government.
毕竟，美国人期望国会来帮助保持我们经济的强健和增势－（而）不是以停摆政府来威胁。
When Congress gets back, they should prevent a shutdown, pass a responsible budget, and prove that this is a country that looks forward – a country that invests in our future, and keeps our economy growing for all Americans.
当国会归来，他们应当避免（政府）停摆，通过一项预算案，并保证这是一个有远见的国家－一个投资未来的国家，并保证我们的经济为每个美国人增长。

Thanks, everybody and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快。

solely adv.完全地；单独地
pressingly adv.强求地；恳切地；固执地
uphold v.保持，支持
sequester v.藏匿，扣押
momentum n.动量，动力
implement n.工具；v.实施
partisan n.坚定的支持者；adj.偏袒的
preschool n.幼儿园；adj.学前的
boost n./v.促进
recess n.暂停
fiscally adv.财政上地
infrastructure n.基础设施

Ex-Im / Export-Import Bank 进出口银行
sense of security 安全感
all told 总而言之
