Weekly Address: Honoring the 70th Anniversary of V-E Day

每周演讲：庆祝第二次世界大战欧战胜利 70 周年纪念日

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President honored the 70th anniversary of V-E Day.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统向第二次世界大战欧战胜利 70 周年表达了敬意。
On this occasion, we commemorate the Allied victory in Europe during World War II.
在此之际，我们共庆第二次世界大战欧战盟军的胜利。
It is a day to pay tribute to the men and women who decades ago served and sacrificed for the cause of freedom.
这是向为（人类）自由（事业）而贡献、牺牲的人们的致敬之日。
This was the generation that, by ending the war, literally saved the world, laying a foundation for peace.
那是通过结束战争，真正拯救世界，奠定和平基础的一代人。
The President asked that in addition to commemorating this important anniversary, we honor the men and women in uniform who currently serve our country, and recommit ourselves to the values we share with our allies in Europe and beyond: freedom, security, democracy, human rights, and the rule of law around the world.
总统谈到，除了庆祝这个重要的周年纪念日，我们（同样）要向此时此刻（正在）为我们服务的军人表达敬意；并且（总统）再次强调了我们与欧洲和其它地区的盟军共同的价值观：自由，安全，民主，人权，和世界各地的法规。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, May 8, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 5 月 8 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
May 8, 2015
2015 年 5 月 8 日


Text：
正文：

Hello everybody.
大家好。
Today marks an historic anniversary—70 years since the Allied victory in Europe during World War II.
今天是第二次世界大战欧战胜利 70 周年的历史性纪念日。
On V-E Day after the Nazi surrender, people swarmed the streets of London and Paris and Moscow, and the cloud of fear that had hung for so many years finally lifted.
在纳粹投降后，即第二次世界大战欧战胜利日，人们蜂拥在伦敦、巴黎和莫斯科的街头，弥漫多年的恐惧之云终于消散。
Here at home, from small towns to Times Square, crowds gathered in celebration, singing and dancing with joy.
在（美国）国内，从小城镇到时代广场，蜂拥的人们欢聚在一起，开心地歌唱，跳舞。
There would still be three more months of deadly fighting in the Pacific.
（虽然当时）距离太平洋（战争）的完全胜利仍有三个多月。
But for a few hours, the world rejoiced in the hope of peace.
但在那几个小时中，全世界都在渴望和平中欢庆。

General Eisenhower announced the news with little fanfare.
艾森豪威尔将军悄然宣布了这个消息。
“The Mission of this Allied Force,” he said, “was fulfilled.”
“盟军武装的使命”，他说，”已经完成。”
But his simple message belied the extraordinary nature of the Allied victory—and the staggering human loss.
但是在他简单的话语中，（却）掩盖了盟军胜利的特殊性质和触目惊心的伤亡人数。
For over five years, brutal fighting laid waste to an entire continent.
五年中，野蛮的杀戮摧毁了整个大洲。
Mothers, fathers, children were murdered in concentration camps.
（无数的）母亲，父亲，孩子在集中营被杀害。
By the time the guns fell silent in Europe, some 40 million people on the continent had lost their lives.
当（弥漫在）欧洲大陆的枪声销声匿迹，这片大陆上已有大约四千万人失去了生命。

Today, we pay tribute to all who served.
今天，我们向所有曾经服役的军人致敬。
They were patriots, like my grandfather who served in Patton’s Army—soldiers, sailors, airmen, marines, coast guard, merchant marines—and the women of the WACs and the WAVES and every branch.
他们是爱国者，就像曾在巴顿将军麾下服役的我的祖父一样——士兵，海员，飞行员，海军陆战队队员，海岸警卫队队员，商船队队员，陆军妇女队（Women’s Army Corps）和志愿紧急服役妇女队（Women Accepted for Volunteer Emergency Service）的女性及它们其中每一个支队。
They risked their lives, and gave their lives so that we, the people the world over, could live free.
他们不顾危险，献出了自己（宝贵）的生命，因此我们——全世界的人民才能自由地活着。
They were women who stepped up in unprecedented numbers, manning the home front, and—like my grandmother—building bombers on assembly lines.
她们是在战争后方前赴后继的众多女性，就像我的祖母——在装配线上组装炮弹。

This was the generation that literally saved the world—that ended the war and laid a foundation for peace.
这就是真正拯救世界的一代人——他们结束了战争，并为和平奠定了基础。

This was the generation that traded in their uniforms for a college education so they could marry their sweethearts, buy homes, raise children and build the strongest middle class the world has ever known.
这就是为了接受大学教育，卖掉军装的一代人。他们因此才能与爱人结婚，购买住房，抚养孩子，并建立了世界上有史以来最强大的中产阶级。

This was the generation that included heroes like the Tuskegee Airmen, the Navajo Code Talkers and the Japanese-Americans of the 442nd Regiment—and who continued the fight for freedom here at home, expanding equality and opportunity and justice for minorities and women.
这就是英雄辈出的一代人。像塔斯克基飞行员，纳瓦霍密码风语者，由日裔美国人组成的美国陆军第 422 步兵团，和那些在国内继续为自由而战，为少数族裔和妇女争取平等，机会和正义的人们。

We will be forever grateful for what these remarkable men and women did, for the selfless grace they showed in one of our darkest hours.
我们将永远感激这些伟大的人们的所做所为，感激在我们最黑暗的时代他们展现的无私恩惠。
But as we mark this 70th anniversary, let’s not simply commemorate history.
但是当我们庆祝 70 周年的纪念日，（请）不要（只是）简单的纪念历史。
Let’s rededicate ourselves to the freedoms for which they fought.
让我们为了他们所争取的自由而奉献自己。

Let’s make sure that we keep striving to fulfill our founding ideals—that we’re a country where no matter who we are or where we’re from or what we look like or who we love, if we work hard and take responsibility, every American will have the opportunity to make of our lives what we will.
让我们确保，我们要保持为实现我们的建国理想而奋斗。我们拥有（共同的）祖国，无论我们是谁，或者来自哪里，无论我们长相几何，或者爱谁，如果我们努力工作，富有责任心，（那么）每个美国人都将有机会过上他们想要的生活。

Let’s stand united with our allies, in Europe and beyond, on behalf of our common values—freedom, security, democracy, human rights, and the rule of law around the world—and against bigotry and hatred in all their forms so that we give meaning to that pledge: “Never forget. Never again.”
让我们和我们的盟友团结在一起，在欧洲和其它地区，在普遍价值观方面——自由，安全，民主，人权，和世界各地的法规——反对任何形式的偏见与敌意，这样我们才能赋予 “Never forget. Never again.（永远铭记（历史），绝不重现（悲剧））” 誓言（新的）含义。

Most of all let’s salute once more to that generation of Americans whose courage and sacrifice are the reason we’re here today in peace and freedom.
首先，让我们再一次向这一代美国人致敬，他们的鼓舞和牺牲换来了我们今天的和平与自由。
Their spirit lives on in our brave men and women in uniform and families who continue to defend the very freedoms our parents and grandparents fought for.
他们的精神仍长存于我们勇敢的军人和其家族，他们仍旧守护着我们父辈与祖父辈所争取的自由。
As Americans grateful for their sevice, let’s truly honor them on this day and every day with the gratitude they have earned and the respect that they deserve.
作为美国人，（我们要）感激他们的服役。让我们在这个日子以及每一天真诚地感激他们的付出并给予他们应得的尊重。

May God bless them and may he continue to bless the United States of America.
愿上帝保佑他们，并继续保佑美利坚合众国。
