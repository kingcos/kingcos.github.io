Weekly Address: A New College Scorecard

每周演讲：一张新的大学计分卡

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President announced the launch of a new College Scorecard, meant to help students and parents identify which schools provide the biggest bang for your buck.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统宣布推出新的大学计分卡，目的是帮助学生和家长识别出哪所大学拥有最高的”性价比”。
Designed with input from those who will use it most, the Scorecard offers reliable data on factors important to prospective students, such as how much graduates earn, and how much debt they have when they graduate.
为那些最大可能的使用者投入设计，积分卡为潜在的学生依据重要的因素提供了可信的数据，例如毕业生的薪资水平，和他们毕业时需要缴纳的欠款。
In an economy where some higher education is still the surest ticket to the middle class, the choices that Americans make when searching for and selecting a college have never been more important.
在（这样）一个经济中，更高等级的教育仍然是通往中产阶级最有把握的门票，当美国人搜索和选择大学从未如此重要。
That’s why the President is committed to making sure there exists reliable information that helps students find the college that best fits their needs so that they can succeed.
这就是为什么，总统承诺确保存在可信的信息，以帮助学生找到最适合他们需求因而能使其成功的学校。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, September 12, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 9 月 12 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
September 12, 2015
2015 年 9 月 12 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
Next week marks seven years since a financial crisis on Wall Street that would usher in some hard years for working families on Main Street.
下周标志着自华尔街经济危机（至今）已经七年，这代表着在过去平民阶层的工薪家庭（所度过）艰难的几年。
Soon after that, I took office.
之后的不久，我上任了。
And we set out to rebuild our economy on a new foundation for growth and prosperity by investing in things that grow our middle class – things like jobs, health care, and education.
并且我们以新的成长基础和通过投资振兴，着手重建我们的经济－例如工作岗位，健康保险，和教育。

Today, our businesses have created more than 13 million new jobs over the last five and a half years.
今天，我们的企业已经在过去的五年半中创造了超过 1300 万新就业岗位。
The unemployment rate is the lowest it’s been in more than seven years.
失业率是七年来的最低。
Another 16 million Americans have gained health insurance.
另外的 1600 万美国人已经获得了健康保险。
Our high school graduate rate is the highest it’s ever been, and more people are graduating from college than ever before.
我们高中的毕业率是有史以来最高，并且比以往更多的大学生从大学毕业。
We are coming back – and stronger.
我们回来了－并且更加强壮。

Still, in an economy that’s increasingly based on knowledge and innovation, some higher education is the surest ticket to the middle class.
然而，在一个以知识和创新为基础的经济中，更高等级的教育仍然是通往中产阶级最有把握的门票。
By the end of this decade, two in three job openings will require some higher education.
在这十年的结束之时，2 / 3 的职务空缺将需要一些更高的教育（水平）。
That’s one reason why a degree from a two-year college will earn you $10,000 more each year than someone who only finished high school.
这就是（拥有）两年制（社区）大学学位将会比只高中就毕业（的人）每年工资多 10,000 美元的一个原因。
One study showed that a degree from a four-year university earns you $1 million more over the course of a lifetime.
一项研究表明，（拥有）四年制的大学学位（的人）一生将多赚一百万美元。

The country with the best-educated workforce in the world is going to win the 21st century economy.
（拥有）最高教育水平劳动力的国家将在 21 世纪的经济中拔得头筹。
I want that to be America.
我希望这会是美国。
But as college costs and student debt keep rising, the choices that Americans make when searching for and selecting a college have never been more important.
但是当大学花费和学生贷款持续增长，美国人在搜索和选择大学变得空前重要。
That’s why everyone should be able to find clear, reliable, open data on college affordability and value – like whether they’re likely to graduate, find good jobs, and pay off their loans.
这就是为什么每个人应当能找到大学负担和价值的清晰，可信，公开的数据－例如他们是否能毕业，是否能找到好的工作，和是否能付清他们的贷款。
Right now, however, many existing college rankings reward schools for spending more money and rejecting more students – at a time when America needs our colleges to focus on affordability and supporting all students who enroll.
但是，现在，许多现存的大学评级（只）因大学花更多的钱，拒收更多的学生而受到好的评级－在这样一个美国需要我们的大学专注于（学费）负担和所有注册的学生（能否）支撑的时间。
That doesn’t make sense, and it has to change.
这并没有奏效，所以必须改革。

So, today, my Administration is launching a new College Scorecard, designed with input from those who will use it the most – students, families, and counselors.
所以，现在，我的政府正在推出新的大学计分卡，专为那些最长的使用者设计－学生，家长，和咨询顾问。
Americans will now have access to reliable data on every institution of higher education.
美国人现在将可以在任何高等教育的金融机构访问到可信的数据。
You’ll be able to see how much each school’s graduates earn, how much debt they graduate with, and what percentage of a school’s students can pay back their loans – which will help all of us see which schools do the best job of preparing America for success.
你将能看到学校毕业生的薪资，他们毕业时的欠款，和学校学生能付清贷款的百分比－这将帮助所有人看到哪些学校在尽力做好工作帮助美国成功。
And to reach more folks, we’re working with partners in the academic, non-profit, and private sectors that will help families use this new data to navigate the complicated college process and make informed decisions.
并且为了覆盖更多的人，我们正和学术界，非营利性组织，和私营企业合作，将帮助（你的）家人使用这些新的数据来为你的繁琐的大学之路导航并作出有建议性的决策。

The status quo serves some colleges and the companies that rank them just fine.
现状是，一些大学和公司提供的评级还算可以。
But it doesn’t serve our students well – and that doesn’t serve any of us well.
但是其并不能很好的为我们的学生提供服务－并且不能为任何人服务好。
There are colleges dedicated to helping students of all backgrounds learn without saddling them with debt.
有些大学努力帮助所有背景学生学习，而没有使欠款成为负担。
We should hold everybody to that standard.
我们应该为每个人坚持标准。
Our economic future depends on it.
我们未来的经济取决于它。

This work is just beginning.
这项工作只是开始。
In the coming weeks and months, we’ll continue to improve the Scorecard based on what we learn from students, parents, counselors, and colleges themselves.
在未来的几周和几月内，我们将继续推进计分卡，以我们从学生，父母，咨询顾问和大学自身所学到的。
The goal is to help everybody who’s willing to work for a higher education search for and select a college that fits their goals.
目的是帮助每个期望因寻找和选择适合他们目标的大学，（进入）更高的教育水平而工作的人，
Together, we can make sure that every student has the chance to get a great education and achieve their full potential.
总之，我们能确信每个学生有机会获得良好的教育并发掘他们的潜力。

Thanks, everybody.
感谢大家（倾听）。
And have a great weekend.
并祝周末愉快。
