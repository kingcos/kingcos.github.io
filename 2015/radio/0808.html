Weekly Address: Reaffirming Our Commitment to Protecting the Right to Vote

每周演讲：重申我们的承诺以保障选举的权利

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President celebrated the 50th Anniversary of the Voting Rights Act by underscoring the importance of one of the most fundamental rights of our democracy – that all of us are created equal and that each of us deserves a voice.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统通过强调我们民主政体最根本的权利之一——我们每个人生来平等且应拥有发言权的重要性，来庆祝投票选举权法案成立 50 周年。
The enactment of the Voting Rights Act wasn’t easy – it was the product of sacrifice from countless men and women who risked so much to protect every person’s right to vote.
选举权的通过并不是一帆风顺的——这是牺牲无数保护每个人选举权的人们的（最后）产物。
The President reminded us about their struggle and that while our country is a better place because of it, there is still work to be done.
总统希望我们牢记他们（曾经）的斗争，虽然现在我们的国家因此而更好，但需要做的还有很多。
He promised to continue to push Congress for new legislation to protect everyone’s right to the polls, and asked that all Americans regardless of party use every opportunity possible to exercise the fundamental right to vote.
他承诺继续推动国会制定新的法律以保护每个人的投选权利，并要求所有美国人不分政党地利用任何可能的机会来行使投票选举的基本权利。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, August 8, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 8 月 8 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
August 8, 2015
2015 年 8 月 8 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
The right to vote is one of the most fundamental rights of any democracy.
投票选举的权利是任何民主政体最基本的权利之一。
Yet for too long, too many of our fellow citizens were denied that right, simply because of the color of their skin.
然而很长一段时间，太多我们的同胞被剥夺了权利，仅仅因为他们的肤色。
Fifty years ago this week, President Lyndon Johnson signed a law to change that.
50 年前的这一周，约翰逊总统签署了一项法律以改变这种状况。
The Voting Rights Act broke down legal barriers that stood between millions of African Americans and their constitutional right to cast ballot.
投票选举权法案击破了矗立在数百万非裔美国人与宪法所赋予的投票权利之间的法律障碍。
It was, and still is, one of the greatest victories in our country’s struggle for civil rights.
这曾是，现在仍是，我们国家内为公民权利所作出斗争的最伟大的胜利之一。

But it didn’t happen overnight.
但是这并没有在一夜之间发生。
Countless men and women marched and organized, sat in and stood up, for our most basic rights.
无数人有组织的团结起来，为我们最基本的权利或默默或站起来抗议。
For this they were called agitators and un-American, they were jailed and beaten.
为此，他们被称作鼓动者和非美国人，他们被关押，被殴打。
Some were even killed.
甚至有一些被杀害。
But in the end, they reaffirmed the idea at the very heart of America: that people who love this country can change it.
但在最后，他们在美国中心重申：爱国之人将会改变它。

Our country is a better place because of all those heroes did for us.
我们的国家因所有这些为我们付出的英雄而变得更美好。
But as one of those heroes, Congressman John Lewis, reminded us in Selma this past March, “There’s still work to be done.”
但作为这些英雄的一员，国会议员 John Lewis，在今年三月在 Selma 提醒我们，”我们需要做的工作还有很多。”
Fifty years after the Voting Rights Act, there are still too many barriers to vote, and too many people trying to erect new ones.
在投票选举权法案颁布后的 50 年，投票选举中仍有许多障碍，并且有很多人试图建立新的法案。
We’ve seen laws that roll back early voting, force people to jump through hoops to cast a ballot, or lead to legitimate voters being improperly purged from the rolls.
我们已经看到法律又回到了早期的投票选举，迫使人们跳出枷锁来投票，或者将合法投票人违法地从投票中抹去。
Over the years, we have seen provisions specifically designed to make it harder for some of our fellow citizens to vote.
多年来，我们已经看到（一些）特殊规定，使得我们的部分同胞更加难以参与投票。
In a democracy like ours, with a history like ours, that’s a disgrace.
与历史和我们一样悠久的民主国家（相比），这是个耻辱。

That’s why, as we celebrate the 50th Anniversary of the Voting Rights Act, I’m calling on Congress to pass new legislation to make sure every American has equal access to the polls.
这就是为什么，当我们庆祝投票选举法案成立 50 周年之际，我正在呼吁国会通过新的法律以确保每个美国人拥有平等的投票机会。
It’s why I support the organizers getting folks registered in their communities.
这就是为什么，我支持那些在自己社区登记选民的组织者们。
And it’s why, no matter what party you support, my message to every American is simple: get out there and vote – not just every four years, but every chance you get.
这也是为什么，无论你支持哪个政党，我要传达给每个美国人的信息很简单：离开这里并投票－并不仅仅是每四年一次，而是任何你拥有的机会。
Because your elected officials will only heed your voice if you make your voice heard.
因为如果你发出声音，你选出的官员将会听取。

The promise that all of us are created equal is written into our founding documents – but it’s up to us to make that promise real.
每个人生来平等的承诺写在我们的开国宣言中－但是承诺是否成真取决于我们。
Together, let’s do what Americans have always done: let’s keep marching forward, keep perfecting our union, and keep building a better country for our kids.
让我们一起继续践行美国人所一直做的：让我们团结前行，完善我们的联邦，并保持为我们的孩子建设一个更好的国家。

Thanks everybody.
感谢大家（倾听）
Have a great weekend.
周末愉快。
