Weekly Address: It’s Time for Congress To Pass a Responsible Budget

每周演讲：国会快通过负责任的预算案吧

（译者注：本标题和 2015-08-22 期完全一致，所以意译为此）

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President discussed the significant progress we have made in our economy since the financial crisis seven years ago this week, and the steps we can take to build on that momentum and strengthen the economy for the long term.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统谈到了自七年前这一周（开始）的金融危机（到现在）我们在经济中所达到的显著进步，以及长期强化经济所采取的重要举措。
Thanks to the hard work and resilience of folks around the country, our businesses have created over 13 million jobs over the past 66 straight months, housing is bouncing back, manufacturing is growing again, and the unemployment rate is the lowest it’s been in over seven years.
多亏了人们努力的工作和城市周边人民的适应能力，我们的企业在过去连续 66 个月中已经创造了超过 1300 万个工作岗位，房产业正迅速回暖，制造业正恢复增长，并且失业率达到七年的最低。
We’ve come a long way from the darkest days of the financial crisis, but there is still more to be done.
我们已经在金融危机（带来）最黑暗的阴云下走了很久，但是需要做的还有很多。
To keep our economy growing, we must avoid self-inflicted wounds and damaging brinksmanship: that starts with Congress passing a responsible budget before the end of the month.
为了保持经济的增长，我们必须要避免自己对自己造成的伤害和破坏边缘政策：从国会本月底前通过一项负责任的预算案开始。
The President has called on Republicans in Congress to stop playing games with our economic progress and instead do its job and pass a budget that reverses the harmful cuts known as the sequester and avoids shutting down the federal government.
总统已经呼吁国会的共和党不要和我们的经济发展玩游戏，相反（应当）尽其责并通过预算案，减少有害的藏匿开支并避免政府停摆。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, September 19, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 9 月 19 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
September 19, 2015
2015 年 9 月 19 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
It’s hard to believe, but it was seven years ago this week that one of Wall Street’s biggest investment banks went bankrupt, triggering a meltdown on Wall Street and the worst economic crisis since the Great Depression.
很难相信，（如今）已是华尔街最大的投资银行破产第七个年头，自从（二战前的）经济大萧条后的华尔街危机和最严重的经济危机。
And in the months that followed, millions of Americans lost their jobs, their homes, and the savings they’d worked so hard to build.
而在那随后的几个月中，数以百万的美国人丢掉工作，失去了他们努力工作所换回的储蓄和家园。

Today’s a different story.
今天当刮目相看。
Over the past five and a half years, our businesses have created more than 13 million new jobs.
在过去的五年半中，我们的企业已经创造了超过 1300 万个新的工作岗位。
The unemployment rate is lower than it’s been in over seven years.
失业率也降到了七年来的最低。
Manufacturing is growing.
制作业正在增长。
Housing is bouncing back.
房地产业正在回暖。
We’ve reduced our deficits by two-thirds.
我们已经减少了 2/3 的赤字。
And 16 million more Americans now know the security of health insurance.
并且现在超过 1600 万的美国人已经了解了医疗保险的安全保障。

This is your progress.
这是你们的功绩。
It’s because of your hard work and sacrifice that America has come back from crisis faster than almost every other advanced nation on Earth.
这是因为你们辛勤的工作和奉献，（因此）美国几乎比地球上其他任何进步的国家都要更快地从危机中恢复。
We remain the safest, strongest bet in the world.
我们确信我们仍然是世界上最迅速最强大的（国家）。

Of course, you might not know all that if you only listened to the bluster of political season, when it’s in the interest of some politicians to paint America as dark and depressing as possible.
当然，如果你只是听听政治赛季的咆哮，你可能并不知道这一切，当一些政治家感兴趣于将美国描绘的尽可能黑暗、萧条。
But I don’t see it that way.
但我并不这么认为。
I’ve met too many Americans who prove, day in and day out, that this is a place where anything is possible.
我见过太多可以证明的美国人，每一天，这是一个任何事情都会生的地方。
Yes, we have a lot of work to do to rebuild a middle class that’s had the odds stacked against it now for decades.
的确，我们要做很多工作来重建中产阶级，现在在十年内已经有不和叠起来反对他。
That’s the thing about America – our work is never finished.
这是关乎美国的事情－我们的努力从未停止。
We always strive to be better – to perfect ourselves.
我们总是努力做到更好－做最好的自己。

We just have to make the right choices.
我们只需要做出正确的选择。
And if Republicans want to help, they can choose, right now, to pass a budget that helps us grow our economy even faster, create jobs even faster, lift people’s incomes and prospects even faster.
如果共和党想给予帮助，他们（也）可以选择，现在，通过预算案来帮助我们更快地发展经济，更快地创造岗位，更快地提高人们的收入和前景。
But they’ve only got until the end of the month to do it – or they’ll shut down our government for the second time in two years.
但是他们已经拖到在月底前完成它－否则，他们将在两年内再次停摆政府。

Democrats are ready to sit down and negotiate with Republicans right now.
民主党人士现在已经准备和共和党人坐下来谈判。
But it should be over legitimate issues like how much do we invest in education, job training, and infrastructure – not unrelated ideological issues like Planned Parenthood.
但是这应当在合法的问题之上，例如在教育，工作培训，和基础设施（分别）投资的多少－而不是不相关的意识形态问题，例如计划生育。
We need to set our sights higher than that.
我们需要把我们的眼界放的更宽。
We need to reverse harmful cuts to middle-class economic priorities, close loopholes that benefit only a fortunate few at the top, and invest more in the things that help our entire economy grow.
我们需要减少中产阶级的经济优先中的有害的开支，修复只受益于顶层少数人的漏洞，并加大投资，帮助我们所有（形式的）经济的发展。

There’s nothing principled about the idea of another government shutdown.
没有什么原则是关于另一个政府停摆的想法。
There’s nothing patriotic about denying the progress you’ve worked so hard to make.
没有什么爱国是否认你们如此努力所创造的辉煌。
America is great right now – not because of our government, or our wealth, or our power, but because of everyone who works hard every day to move this country forward.
美国现在是伟大的－并不是我们的政府，也不是我们的财富，更不是权力，而是因为每个人，那些每天努力工作推动这个国家前进的人们。
Now Congress needs to work as hard as you do.
现在，国会需要像你们一样工作。

Thanks, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），并祝周末愉快。

resilience n.适应能力
self-inflicted adj.自己造成的
trigger n.扳机，触发器
meltdown n.彻底垮台
brinksmanship n.边缘政策
reverse n.相反情况；v.转变
legitimate adj.合法的

66 straight months 连续 66 个月
bounce back 回暖
go bankrupt 破产
Planned Parenthood 计划生育
