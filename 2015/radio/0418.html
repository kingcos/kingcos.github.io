Weekly Address: Climate Change Can No Longer Be Ignored

每周演讲：气候变化不能再被忽视

返回

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC — In this week’s address, the President spoke about his commitment to combatting the threat of climate change and to keeping ourselves and future generations safe.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统讲述了他的承诺，关于遏制气候变化（所带来）的危机并保障我们与子孙后代的安全。
The effects of climate change can no longer be denied or ignored – 2014 was the planet’s warmest year recorded, and 14 of the 15 hottest years on record have happened this century.
气候变化所产生的影响已不能再被否认与忽视－2014 年是全球有纪录以来最热的一年，并且纪录中最热的 15 年其中有 14 年均发生在本世纪中。
Climate change poses risks to our national security, our economy, and our public health.
气候变化给我们的国家安全，经济和公众健康带来风险。
The President has already taken historic steps to address climate change, but there’s more that the United States and the international community can do.
总统已经采取历史性的措施去寻找气候变化的原因，但是美国和国际社会（仍）可以做的更多。
That’s why next Wednesday, on Earth Day, in the latest part of his effort to call attention to and act on the threat of climate change, the President will visit the Florida Everglades and speak about the threat that climate change poses to our economy and to the world.
这就是为什么下周三，（世界）地球日，他的最近努力是（为乐）唤起（人们去）注意气候变化的（所引发的）征兆并行动起来。（那天）总统将去弗罗里达州大沼泽地国家公园，探讨气候变化对于经济预示和全世界的影响。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, April 18, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 4 月 18 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
April 18, 2015
2015 年 4 月 18 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
Wednesday is Earth Day, a day to appreciate and protect this precious planet we call home.
本周三是（世界）地球日，是一个感激并保护这颗被我们称之为珍稀星球的日子。
And today, there’s no greater threat to our planet than climate change.
且当今，在我们的星球上，（已经）没有比气候变化更大的威胁了。

2014 was the planet’s warmest year on record.
2014 年是全球有纪录以来最热的一年。
Fourteen of the 15 hottest years on record have all fallen in the first 15 years of this century.
纪录中最热的 15 年其中有 14 年均在在本世纪的前 15 年中。
This winter was cold in parts of our country – as some folks in Congress like to point out – but around the world, it was the warmest ever recorded.
我们国家的部分地区这个冬天（还）是寒冷的，正如国会中的一些议员（总）喜欢指出：但是纵观全球，这是纪录中最暖的（冬天）。

And the fact that the climate is changing has very serious implications for the way we live now.
并且事实就是，气候变化对我们现在的生活方式产生更加严重的影响。
Stronger storms.
更猛烈的暴风雨。
Deeper droughts.
更严重的干旱。
Longer wildfire seasons.
更漫长的山火季节。
The world’s top climate scientists are warning us that a changing climate already affects the air our kids breathe.
全世界的顶尖气候学家正警告我们，变化的气候已经（开始）影响我们孩子们所呼吸的空气。
Last week, the Surgeon General and I spoke with public experts about how climate change is already affecting patients across the country.
上周，卫生局局长和我并与公共专家（一起），谈及气候变化是如何已经在全国范围内影响病人的。
The Pentagon says that climate change poses immediate risks to our national security.
国防部（五角大楼）称气候变化给我们的国家安全带来紧迫的风险。

And on Earth Day, I’m going to visit the Florida Everglades to talk about the way that climate change threatens our economy.
在（世界）地球日（当天），我将去弗罗里达州大沼泽地国家公园，探讨气候变化对于经济预示的方式。
The Everglades is one of the most special places in our country.
大沼泽地国家公园是我们国家最奇特的地方之一。
But it’s also one of the most fragile.
但同时又是最脆弱的地方之一。
Rising sea levels are putting a national treasure – and an economic engine for the South Florida tourism industry – at risk.
上升的海平面正在使南佛罗里达州的国之宝藏，经济支柱的旅游业面临风险。

So climate change can no longer be denied – or ignored.
所以气候变化（绝）不能再被否认或是忽视。
The world is looking to the United States – to us – to lead.
世界正在关注美国－我们，去带领（他们）。
And that’s what we’re doing.
这就是我们所正在做的。
We’re using more clean energy than ever before.
我们正在使用比以往更加清洁的能源。
America is number one in wind power, and every three weeks, we bring online as much solar power as we did in all of 2008.
美国是风能利用的第一大国，并且每三周，我们所上线的太阳能相当于我们 2008 年全年利用的总量。
We’re taking steps to waste less energy, with more fuel-efficient cars that save us money at the pump, and more energy-efficient buildings that save us money on our electricity bills.
我们正在逐步采取措施减少能源浪费，通过（生产）更多可以省油（直译：帮我们在加油站省钱）的低耗高效汽车，（建造）更多可以节省电费开支的节能建筑。

So thanks in part to these actions, our carbon pollution has fallen by 10 percent since 2007, even as we’ve grown our economy and seen the longest streak of private-sector job growth on record.
所以这部分要归功于（实施）这些方案，我们的碳排放自 2007 年起已下降了 10%，恰恰在此时，我们发展了经济，又看到了私营部门就业最长久的连续增长。
We’ve committed to doubling the pace at which we cut carbon pollution, and China has committed, for the first time, to limiting their emissions.
我们已经承诺以两倍的速度减少碳排放，并且中国（也）第一次承诺限制他们的排放量。
And because the world’s two largest economies came together, there’s new hope that, with American leadership, this year, the world will finally reach an agreement to prevent the worst impacts of climate change before it’s too late.
并且由于全球最大的两个经济体达成了共识，今年有希望在美国的领导下，全世界将就在为时已晚前防止气候变化引发最坏的效果达成协议。

This is an issue that’s bigger and longer-lasting than my presidency.
这是一个比我的任期更加重大且漫长的问题。
It’s about protecting our God-given natural wonders, and the good jobs that rely on them.
这事关保护我们的天赐自然奇观，和所依赖它们的好工作。
It’s about shielding our cities and our families from disaster and harm.
这事关保护我们的城市和我们的家庭免受病患和伤害。
It’s about keeping our kids healthy and safe.
这事关保障孩子们的健康与安全。
This is the only planet we’ve got.
这是我们唯一的星球。
And years from now, I want to be able to look our children and grandchildren in the eye and tell them that we did everything we could to protect it.
多年以后，我希望可以无愧于心正视我们的孩子和子孙，并且告诉他们，我们（曾）尽一切可能去保护她（译者注：地球妈妈）。

Thanks everybody, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快。
