Weekly Address: Stand Up for American Workers and Pass TAA

每周演讲：捍卫美国工人并通过《贸易调整援助》

Overview：
概览：

WASHINGTON, DC – In this week’s address, the President reiterated that his top priority is to grow the American economy and ensure that every hardworking American has a fair shot at success.
华盛顿特区－本周演讲中，总统重申了他的首要使命是发展美国经济并确保每个努力奋斗的美国人都拥有公平的机会（取得）成功。
It’s because of this commitment that the President has worked to enact smart new trade agreements that level the playing field for our workers, open new markets for our businesses, and hold other countries to the kinds of high standards that Americans are proud to hold ourselves to here at home.
正是因为这个承诺，总统已努力去制定灵活的新型贸易协议，为我们的工人平衡竞争环境，为我们的企业打开新兴市场，并让其他国家遵守美国人在国内所引以为傲的各种各样的高标准。
On Friday, Republicans and Democrats in the House took an important step by voting to help the United States negotiate and enforce high-standard trade deals.
周五，共和党和民主党迈出了重要一步，（他们）在众议院通过投票表决来帮助美利坚合众国进行协商并执行高标准的贸易协议。
But they also failed to renew Trade Adjustment Assistance, despite the fact that it provides vital support to about 100,000 workers, and passed the Senate with bipartisan support.
但同时他们停止续签 TAA（《贸易调整援助》），尽管事实是它已为大约 10 万工人提供必不可少的帮助，（但它）在两党支持之下通过了参议院。
The President urged the House to pass TAA without delay so that more middle-class workers can earn the chance to participate and succeed in our global economy.
总统敦促众议院尽快通过 TAA，这样更多的中产阶级工人可以有更多机会参与进全球经济化（浪潮中）并取得成功。

The audio of the address and video of the address will be available online at www.whitehouse.gov at 6:00 a.m. ET, June 13, 2015.
演讲的音频和视频将在 2015 年 6 月 13 日于美国东部时间上午 6:00 发布在 www.whitehouse.gov。


Remarks of President Barack Obama
贝拉克·奥巴马总统的演讲
Weekly Address
每周例行演讲
The White House
白宫
June 13, 2015
2015 年 6 月 13 日


Text：
正文：

Hi everybody.
大家好。
My top priority as President is to grow the economy and help more hardworking Americans get ahead.
我作为总统的首要使命是发展经济并帮助更多努力奋斗的美国人取得成功。
And after the worst economic crisis in our lifetimes, our businesses have now created 12.6 million new jobs over the past 63 months.
而在经历了迄今最严重的经济危机之后，我们的企业在过去的 63 个月内已经创造了 1260 万个新的就业岗位。

That’s a record streak of job creation.
这是就业岗位记录的连续增长。
And it’s come as we’ve been working to reform our schools, revitalize manufacturing and the auto industry, revamp our job training programs – and rework our health care system, covering more than 16 million uninsured Americans so far.
随之而来，我们已经努力改革我们的学校，恢复生产业和汽车业，重组我们的就业培训项目－并重启我们的医疗保健系统，至今已覆盖超过 1600 万没有保险的美国人。

We’ve done all of this to rebuild our economy on a new foundation, a foundation for growth that benefits not only us, but our kids, and their kids.
我们已经尽所有可能重建新的经济基础，它的发展（壮大）将不止使我们受益，更让我们的子子孙孙共同受益。
Because we do live in a new economy.
因为我们（的确）生活在一个全新的经济当中了。
And we’ve got to adapt to make sure America leads the way in this new century, just like we did in the last.
并且我们已经开始调整来确保美国人在新世纪中这条道路领航，就像我们在过去所做的一样。

Part of that means sparking new sources of growth and job creation that keep us on the cutting edge.
这部分意味着新资源和工作岗位激增把我们推向风口浪尖。
And one big way to do that is through smart new trade agreements that level the playing field for our workers, open new markets for our businesses, and hold other countries to the kinds of high standards that Americans are proud to hold ourselves to here at home.
而要做到这些的一个捷径便是通过灵活的新型贸易协议来为我们的工人平衡竞争环境，为我们的企业打开新兴市场，并让其他国家遵守美国人在国内所引以为傲的各种各样的高标准。

Simply put, America has to write the rules of the 21st century economy in a way that benefits American workers.
简单地说，美国必须定下 21 世纪的经济规则，这样才能使我们的工人（更加）受益。
If we don’t, countries like China will write those rules in a way that benefits their workers.
如果我们不这样做，像中国等（一些）国家将会定下利于他们工人的规则。

Now, on Friday, Republicans and Democrats in the House of Representatives voted to help the United States negotiate new trade deals that are both free and fair – deals that expand opportunity for our workers and our businesses alike.
此前，周五，共和党和民主党在众议院投票来帮助美利坚合众国协商自由且平等的贸易协议，新的协议将为我们的工人和企业扩大机遇。
And that’s good.
并且结果很好。
These kinds of trade deals say no to a race for the bottom, for lower wages and working conditions.
这些贸易协议不会自甘堕落，降低工资和工作条件。
They’re about starting a race to the top, for higher wages, and better working conditions, stronger environmental protections, and a smarter way to crack down on countries that break the rules of the global economy.
它们是向高处发展，为了更高的工资和更好的工作条件，更加完善的环境保护并拥有灵活的方式抗击那些不遵守全球经济规则的国家。

But that’s not all we should be doing for our workers.
但这仅仅是我们可以为我们的工人所做的一部分（直译：但这并不是全部）。
Right now, something called Trade Adjustment Assistance provides vital support, like job-training and community college education, to tens of thousands of American workers each year who were hurt by past trade deals – the kind we’re not going to repeat again.
眼下，《贸易调整援助》为每年成千上万的美国工人提供了必要的保障，例如就业培训和社区大学教育，他们曾被过去的贸易协议所伤害－我们不会让历史重演。
Republicans and Democrats in the Senate have voted to renew this initiative, but so far, the House of Representatives has chosen to let it expire in just a few months, leaving as many as 100,000 American workers on their own.
共和党和民主党在参议院投票表决续签这个提案，但到目前为止，众议院已经选择让它在短短几个月内到期，不顾多达 10 万名美国工人。
For the sake of those workers, their families, and their communities, I urge those Members of Congress who voted against Trade Adjustment Assistance to reconsider, and stand up for American workers.
为了这些工人，他们的家庭和他们的社区的利益，我敦促那些反对 TAA 的国会议员重新考虑，捍卫美国工人。

Because these smart new trade deals aren’t just about growing our economy and supporting good new American jobs.
因为这些灵活的新型贸易协议不仅仅能够提升我们的经济并提供在美国好的新就业岗位。
This is about the kind of country we want to build for our kids and our grandkids.
这是关乎于我们要建立为子孙后代造福的国家。
And if I did not think that smart new trade deals were the right thing to do for working families, I wouldn’t be fighting for it.
如果我认为灵活的新型贸易协议对于工薪家庭毫无用处，我不会争取它。

This is the right thing to do.
这是（完全）正确的事情。
Trade that’s fair and free and smart will grow opportunity for our middle class.
自由，平等，灵活的贸易将为我们的中产阶级带来更多机遇。
It will help us restore the dream we share, and make sure that every American who works hard has a chance to get ahead.
这将帮助我们重拾共同的梦想，并确保每一个努力奋斗的美国人有机会出人头地。
That’s a cause worth fighting for – today, and every day I have the honor of serving as your President.
这是值得为之奋斗的事业－我有幸担任你们的总统的今天和每一天。

Thanks everybody, and have a great weekend.
感谢大家（倾听），周末愉快。
